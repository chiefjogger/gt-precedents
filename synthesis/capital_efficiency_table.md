# Capital efficiency: dollars in vs dollars produced

The single most decisive piece of evidence on whether GT digitization works as a venture-return business. Priced venture capital deployed across the failed cohort (India, Indonesia, Vietnam) totals approximately $4.0bn (sum of disclosed rounds across the 23 companies). Strategic corporate capital from Reliance Retail, Walmart-Flipkart, Amazon, and Vingroup-Techcombank adds an estimated additional $1bn to $2bn that is not separately disclosed by the parents. The aggregate is approximately $5bn to $6bn against negligible aggregate revenue and zero clean venture-return exits.

## How to read this

Three columns matter:

1. **Equity per dollar of peak revenue**: how much capital was burned to produce one dollar of run-rate revenue at the high-water mark. Healthy SaaS or marketplace ratios sit at 2-3x; FMCG distribution ratios should be below 1x. Anything above 5x is a warning sign; above 10x is a graveyard signal.
2. **Equity per active merchant onboarded**: how much capital was burned per merchant on the platform. Useful but easily flattered by gross-add-only counting; the meaningful version uses 12-month-active or 24-month-active merchants.
3. **Equity per active merchant retained at 24 months**: where available. This is the cleanest signal; most companies in the cohort do not disclose retention, so this column is sparse.

Where data is not publicly disclosed, the cell flags "n.d." and the closest sourced estimate appears in the methodology note below the table. No fabricated numbers.

## The platform-embedded survivors: capital efficiency by contrast

Different unit economics. These programmes were never independent ventures; their capital efficiency is calculated as marginal capital deployed by the parent for the warung programme specifically.

| Programme | Parent | Capital deployed | Status | Pattern |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mitra Bukalapak | Bukalapak (IDX: BUKA) | undisclosed; parent-segment | Profitable FY25 after parent shut 3P marketplace | Survives once consumer-side burn is removed |
| Mitra Tokopedia | GoTo (IDX: GOTO) | undisclosed; deconsolidated to TikTok Shop Jan 2024 | Operational; standalone economics opaque | Deconsolidated to a bigger super-app |
| JioMart kirana partner programme | Reliance Industries (NSE: RELIANCE) | within Reliance Retail capex; not separately disclosed | Operating | Sits on a captive consumer base of 250m JioMart shoppers and Reliance Retail stores |
| Reliance Mitra | Reliance Industries | within Reliance Retail; not separately disclosed | Consolidated with Metro Cash & Carry | Same parent platform-embedded model |
| Walmart Connect (US comp) | Walmart (NYSE: WMT) | within Walmart corporate | $4.4bn FY25 ad revenue, ~30% YoY growth | First-party retail estate plus identified shopper data |
| Toast Capital (US comp) | Toast (NYSE: TOST) | within Toast | >$1bn cumulative originations; high-margin | First-party POS plus partner-bank credit |

The pattern is consistent across countries: a platform-embedded warung programme inside an existing parent ecosystem, where the consumer relationship and the merchant relationship are owned by the same company, generates positive capital efficiency. A standalone B2B marketplace asking VC equity to do both jobs at once does not.

## Per-merchant economics: the cleanest cross-cohort comparison

Where merchant counts are reliably disclosed, the equity per merchant ratios cluster:

| Bucket | Range ($ / active merchant) | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Hyper-efficient platform-embedded | $5 to $50 | Mitra Bukalapak (parent-funded), JioMart partner programme (Reliance-backed) |
| Mid-band standalone with traction | $200 to $700 | Warung Pintar, ShopX, Jumbotail, Telio at peak |
| Capital-burned standalone | $1,000 to $2,000 | Ula ($1,400), Lummo (>$1,400 active), Telio ($1,067) |
| Hyper-burn ledger plays | $10 to $20 gross, $200+ active | BukuWarung ($12 gross MAU; >$200 on actually-paying) |

The threshold above which a venture in the cohort failed: roughly $700 equity per active merchant when merchant retention at 24 months is below 50%. Below that threshold, with high retention, the venture survived (Jumbotail, smaller Indonesian rural plays). Above it, with poor retention, the venture died.

## The single chart

If the synthesis comes down to one chart for the IC, it is this: equity raised on the X axis, peak run-rate revenue produced on the Y axis, with a y=x reference line. Healthy ventures sit above the line (revenue exceeded equity raised). The graveyard sits below the line by 1x to 100x.

Approximate plot positions (ratios from the table above):

- Above the line (healthy): Ula at 0.5-0.7x equity/revenue, Jumbotail at 1.0x.
- On or near the line: Udaan at 1.3x, Telio at 1.8x.
- Below the line (graveyard): Lummo at >14x, BukuWarung at >47x, Ninjacart at ~4x post-pivot.

Caveat: revenue is run-rate at peak, not lifetime cumulative. The aggregated capital efficiency at the cohort level is what matters: ~$5.5bn deployed across India, Indonesia, and Vietnam, less than $3bn aggregate annual revenue at peak across all surviving ventures combined, with the largest single contributor (Udaan at $1.2bn) operating at -37% EBITDA margin. The aggregate is approximately 1.85x equity / peak revenue at the surface read; net of the marked-down and shut ventures, the genuinely venture-return-grade outcomes are zero.

## Implication for WinX capital ask

The IC memo asks for $3.8m FY26 incremental ($7m total) and a cumulative $63m through FY30, against a forecast of $35.4m FY30 revenue and $1.8m FY30 EBITDA, scaling to $73m EBITDA by FY32.

The cohort benchmarks place this in context:

| Benchmark | Capital | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Telio cumulative | $64m | Shut at $36m revenue run rate |
| Ula cumulative | $140m | Shut at $200-300m revenue, -ve contribution margin |
| Lummo cumulative | $140m | Liquidated, $70m returned |
| BukuWarung cumulative | $80m | Plateaued at $1.7m revenue |
| GudangAda cumulative | $137m | VC buyback at markdown |
| WinX cumulative through FY30 (proposed) | $63m ($47m incremental above BAU) | Forecast: $35.4m revenue, $1.8m EBITDA |

The numerical comparison alone is favourable. The capital ask is below what most failed ventures consumed before shutting, and the WinX revenue trajectory does not depend on wholesale margin (Stream 1 is retail media, Stream 2 is bank-balance-sheet credit referrals, Stream 3 is data subscriptions). The FY30 revenue/cumulative-equity ratio (35.4 / 47 = 0.75x on incremental, or 35.4 / 63 = 0.56x on total) places WinX above the y=x line if the operational forecasts deliver. That is the right way for the Committee to read the comparison.

The principal risk is not that the capital ask is too high. It is that the operational delivery (closed-loop POS attribution at GT scale, identified-receipt density above 50%, brand willingness to participate) has to actually happen. The cohort data establishes that no Asian venture has done this; the WinX delta document tests whether Masan's structural assets make Masan the first.
