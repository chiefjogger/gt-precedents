### 3.3 Product architecture for SMEs and merchants

Two product families anchor the proposition:

- BusinessOne (corporate transactional package). BusinessOne Credit Plus is the unsecured / partially-secured working-capital line, with a stated approved limit "up to VND 48 billion" for SMEs [source: Techcombank, "BusinessOne Credit Plus loan solutions for small enterprises", https://techcombank.com/en/household-and-small-enterprise/small-enterprise/borrow/business-one-credit-plus]. The IC memo's references to "up to VND 5bn unsecured" and "up to VND 20bn partially-secured" pre-approved limits for the FMCG vertical fit inside the BusinessOne Credit Plus envelope but as ticket-tier guidance for FMCG specifically; the public 48B headline is the cap, not the typical FMCG draw. Treat the 5B / 20B pre-approved tier ticket sizes as confidential pricing not yet in any public deck.
- MerchantOne (merchant package on top of BusinessOne). Provides "a holistic payment and collection solution tailored to merchants' specific business personas" [source: VietnamNews, 27 Apr 2025, as cited above].

### 3.4 Six priority verticals

The Jan 2026 investor deck p.27 explicitly enumerates the six sectors where TCB intends to compete with embedded finance: Banking, Securities and Asset Management, Real Estate, Consumer Goods and Services, Logistics, Infra / Energy / ESG, and Healthcare and Insurance. The FY25 corporate-loan disclosure (Earnings Call p.12) groups Real Estate, FMCG / Retail / Logistics, Construction, Materials, Utilities and Telco, and Others. The combined picture: FMCG / Retail / Logistics is one of TCB's named priority verticals, both for corporate exposure and for the SME / merchant population sitting beneath it. This is the vertical where WinX sits.

### 3.5 Risk framing

TCB's "low risk, high return" model is explicit (deck p.5). For SMEs they say the strategy is "Mid-Market and Upper SME" first, with selective expansion into Micro SME via "Digitally-led Convenience, Micro segment, Product bundle, Best price." Risk discipline shows up in FY25 NPL of 1.13% and corporate NPL of 0.3%. The implication for WinX: TCB will not absorb undifferentiated tail risk on micro merchants without a data partner; an operating-system partner that supplies merchant transaction history, geo-spatial behaviour, and basket-level data fits the stated capability gap. Page 26 of the Jan 2026 deck calls this out in plain English: "Risk-Cost Advantage via Ecosystem Integration: Smarter underwriting with use of better data, Lower CAC through owned platforms, Higher conversion through embedded moments (such as payments)".

### 3.6 SME context in Vietnam

SMEs account for c.97% of Vietnam's c.940,000 active enterprises, contribute c.45% of GDP, and employ c.5.5M workers [source: VietnamPlus, "PM Pham Minh Chinh urges SMEs to break limits for growth", https://en.vietnamplus.vn/pm-pham-minh-chinh-urges-smes-to-break-limits-for-growth-post310717.vnp]. Politburo Resolution 68 targets 2 million private businesses by 2030 (Investor deck p.15). TCB's 259K SME / 2.2M merchant footprint is therefore a small but growing slice of a market that the State is actively trying to formalise; the new household-business tax-conversion framework starting in 2026 (TCB-VTCA cooperation signed 27 Dec 2025, FY25 Press Release p.7) is the policy tailwind that brings the next several million micro-businesses into the formal banking system.

VPBank has external capital earmarked for SME lending; TCB has the merchant transaction franchise and data infrastructure. ACB and MBB have scale but neither has TCB's transaction-banking #1 share (16.3%) nor its CASA franchise (40.4%). For an embedded-finance integration where the partner needs (i) instant payments rails, (ii) wallet / Auto-earning consumer infrastructure, and (iii) credit-decisioning engines that can underwrite a kiosk merchant from cashflow data alone, the natural ranking from TCB's published capabilities is: TCB primary, VPBank secondary on capital availability, MB tertiary on retail tech, ACB fallback on conservative book.

## Section 4: Mapping TCB's stated direction onto the WinX Stream 2 thesis

The WinX IC memo positions Stream 2 (Embedded Finance) on three claims: (i) TCB is the natural anchor SME credit partner; (ii) the WinX merchant graph is unique enough to materially lower TCB's underwriting cost; (iii) TCB's stated strategy will direct credit growth to the SME / merchant segment in a window that aligns with the WinX rollout. Each tested below.

### 4.1 Is TCB the natural anchor?

Six pieces of TCB-published evidence say yes.

- TCB explicitly names "Embedded Finance" as one of its four growth pillars (Investor deck p.22).
- TCB explicitly names "Retail and SME Commerce: Working capital, payments, credit (via OneMount)" as a core embedded-finance proposition (Investor deck p.27). OneMount is the existing TCB / VinShop / OneHousing technology platform; the WinX merchant footprint is a direct extension of the same logic into the General Trade channel.
- TCB explicitly names "Start a Shop: Onboarding by VinShop while TCB provides SME loan, payment / digital wallet services" as a value bundle (Investor deck p.27). The exact pattern WinX is replicating at scale.
- TCB owns the #1 transaction-banking franchise (16.3% market share) and the #1 CASA franchise (40.4%) in Vietnam, the two infrastructure layers that an embedded-finance partner needs.
- TCB has 0% default on real-estate projects and 1.13% group NPL through three credit cycles (Investor deck p.7), giving Masan board comfort that credit underwriting will be conservative.
- Chairman Ho Hung Anh's brother Ho Hung Dung sits on Masan Group, giving the partnership a longstanding governance bridge that no other Vietnamese bank can match.

### 4.2 Will the WinX merchant graph lower underwriting cost?

TCB has self-identified the gap. Investor deck p.26 states "Risk-Cost Advantage via Ecosystem Integration: Smarter underwriting with use of better data, Lower CAC through owned platforms, Higher conversion through embedded moments (such as payments)." The 700% merchant lending growth in 2024-25 was, per the FY25 AGM coverage, "achieved through personalizing services based on deep data analysis." The bottleneck is not appetite; it is signal density. WinX brings shopkeeper-level transactional, behavioural, and basket data that VinShop alone cannot supply (VinShop covers an FMCG-narrow merchant cohort; WinX adds geo, basket, GT route, and in-store fulfilment signals).

### 4.3 Does the timing line up?

| Timing input | Year | What it does for WinX |
| --- | --- | --- |
| TCB embarks on new 5Y strategy | 2026 | Anchor partner is actively writing its credit growth playbook now |
| 2026 credit growth quota | VND 849T (+12%) | TCB has SBV-approved capacity to direct incremental book to SME / merchant |
| 2026 PBT plan | VND 35-37.5T (+7.6% to +15%) | Target requires high-yield asset growth, which is what unsecured SME / merchant lending provides |
| Household-business tax conversion | from 2026 | 5M+ households entering formal banking; greenfield SME book at TCB |
| WinX rollout | 2026-27 | Sits exactly inside the formalisation window |
| Vietnam private business target | 2M by 2030 | Same compounding window as TCB's 4x TOI / 2x share path |

The thesis test is positive on all three legs. The Stream 2 IC framing is consistent with what TCB is publicly committing to in its own primary documents.

### 4.4 What backup banks look like

If TCB does not move at the pace WinX needs, VPBank is the cleanest backup because its IFC-backed sustainability bond explicitly earmarks SME on-lending and the bank has a higher tolerance for unsecured retail risk. MB Bank is third on technology depth. ACB is the conservative fallback. None of the three has the data-platform / OneMount integration story that TCB has, so any switch would re-set the partnership 18-24 months.

## Section 6: Thin-research flags

- The 2026 AGM presentation deck PDF itself was not directly listed on the TCB IR page at the time of writing. - The 2026 AGM Resolutions document was not yet uploaded to the TCB AGM page at the time of writing; the meeting was held on 25 Apr 2026 and resolutions are typically uploaded within 5-10 working days. The capital-increase, dividend, and 2026 plan numbers are taken from press coverage of the 25 Apr 2026 meeting.
- The TCB FY2025 Annual Report PDF (the formal IFRS book) is typically published end-March / early-April. As of 27 Apr 2026 the FY25 disclosure stack available is Press Release, Earnings Call deck, and Investor Presentation; the full annual report had not been uploaded. The financial figures used here come from the audited press release, not the annual report.
- The "60++" project count and "0% default" claim is a marketing summary rather than an audited disclosure; TCB has not published the project list.
- The +700% merchant lending growth datapoint is sourced from press coverage of the 27 Apr 2025 AGM, not a primary TCB document. It is internally consistent with disclosed FY25 retail-unsecured growth of 3.5x YTD, so it is plausible but not separately auditable.
- The "5MM Auto-earning customers" datapoint reconciles with public press references to "millions" of Auto-earning users but is below the 13.8M figure that one Vietnam-language press summary used; the deck-level 5M is the right number for the AGM slide because it represents Auto-earning 2.0 active users, while 13.8M includes the broader Rewards loyalty pool.
