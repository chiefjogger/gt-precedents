# One Mount Group: the holding-company shell, asset by asset

Reference period: November 2019 (incorporation) to October 2025 (OneShop relaunch / latest leadership rotation). Built for the WinX IC memo. The IC memo treats One Mount as the parent of VinShop. This file covers the holding company itself, the four operating pillars, the shareholder evolution, and what the January 2025 government tasking implies for the digital agenda.

## 1. Birth and shareholder structure

One Mount Group was formed in late 2019 with initial charter capital of approximately VND 3,000 billion (approximately US$130m at the 2019 FX), backed jointly by Vingroup (51.22%) and Techcombank-aligned shareholders. The cited VnExpress article notes the charter capital rose to VND 4,047 billion by mid-2020 and VND 5,400 billion by mid-2021 as the group scaled [source: VnExpress International, "Digital platform One Mount Group restructures shares", 2022, https://e.vnexpress.net/news/companies/digital-platform-one-mount-group-restructures-shares-4444896.html]. The official mandate: "build the largest technological ecosystem with solutions focused on optimization and bridging of value chain gaps across high-growth economic sectors in Vietnam" [source: One Mount official communication via VIR, "A closer look at One Mount Group", 2021, https://vir.com.vn/a-closer-look-at-one-mount-group-and-the-vision-to-build-vietnams-largest-digital-ecosystem-80387.html].

The original chairmanship sat with Ho Anh Ngoc, brother of Techcombank chairman Ho Hung Anh; the CEO seat went to Nguyen Thi Diu, former Deputy CEO of Vingroup [source: ViettTimes, "Dich chuyen so huu tai One Mount Group", https://m.viettimes.vn/dich-chuyen-so-huu-tai-one-mount-group-post155669.html]. The board chair role later moved to Nguyen Thi Thanh Thuy, wife of Ho Hung Anh [source: ViettTimes, "Vingroup rút lui, One Mount Group đang thuộc về ai?", 2022, https://viettimes.vn/vingroup-rut-lui-one-mount-group-dang-thuoc-ve-ai-post185645.html]. The dynastic alignment to the Techcombank principals was deliberate from inception.

In Q1 2022, Vingroup divested its 51.22% stake. The shares were absorbed by Techcombank-aligned holders. Vingroup is no longer the parent [source: VnExpress International, 2022, op cit].

Funding history. One Mount has not run a venture-style external priced round at any point. The capital base is parent contributions from Vingroup (until Q1 2022) and Techcombank-aligned shareholders (continuously). Press throughout 2021 styled One Mount as "Vietnam's next unicorn" [source: Digitimes, "Vietnam-based One Mount paves its way to becoming next unicorn", 29 Oct 2021, https://www.digitimes.com/news/a20211029PD209.html] but no priced external mark exists.

## 4. Capital and valuation

- Charter capital: ~VND 3,000bn (~US$130m) at inception in late 2019; rising to VND 4,047bn (mid-2020) and VND 5,400bn (mid-2021).
- Subsequent capital injections: not publicly disclosed at parent level. Subsidiary-level increases are visible (OneHousing scaled charter capital 30x between 2020 and May 2022; from VND 33bn to VND 620bn).
- External priced rounds: none.
- Peak valuation: not anchored. **Closest sourced estimate: $1bn implied by "next unicorn" press in 2021 but never confirmed by a priced transaction.**
- Government-directed capex commitment (Jan 2025): US$200-500m for the Layer-1 blockchain build [source: Thanh Nien, https://thanhnien.vn/one-mount-dau-tu-200-500-trieu-usd-xay-dung-mang-blockchain-layer-1-cua-viet-nam-185250115155946827.htm]. This is operating capex, funded by parent capital, not external venture money.

## 5. The Layer-1 blockchain mandate (January 2025)

On 15 January 2025, at the 6th National Forum on Developing Vietnam's Digital Technology Enterprises, General Secretary To Lam formally tasked One Mount Group with building a "Make in Vietnam" Layer-1 blockchain. The state press release listed the design criteria as "high speed, exceptional scalability, optimal security and a consensus mechanism" with the platform serving as a "core foundation, facilitating operations, exploitation, interaction and interoperability across various types of blockchain networks" [source: Bao Chinh Phu, 15 Jan 2025, https://baochinhphu.vn/one-mount-group-nhan-nhiem-vu-xay-dung-mang-blockchain-layer-1-make-in-vietnam-10225011516435619.htm].

One Mount committed US$200-500m of investment over the build cycle. Use cases listed: finance, healthcare, education, public services [source: Vietnam News, "One Mount to invest in building Blockchain Layer 1 network in Vietnam", https://vietnamnews.vn/media-outreach/1691021/one-mount-to-invest-in-building-blockchain-layer-1-network-in-vietnam.html].

What this implies. One Mount is being positioned as a state-aligned digital infrastructure vehicle, not as a private-equity-owned commercial platform. The implication is that the GT digitization line items (VinShop, OneShop) are no longer the strategic centre of gravity inside the parent. They are operating businesses that fund and feed the mother venture (digital infra), not the venture itself.

## 6. Why no IPO, no Series

Three reasons, layered:

1. **The shareholder base does not need an IPO marker.** Techcombank principals are already publicly listed via Techcombank itself (HOSE: TCB) and TCBS. Adding a separate One Mount listing would dilute focus.
2. **The economics are not ready for public-market scrutiny.** The four pillars trade at different multiple regimes (real estate, FMCG distribution, loyalty, fintech) and consolidation drags the multiple to the lowest common denominator.
3. **The state mandate creates a different incentive vector.** A government-tasked blockchain build creates strategic upside that a public listing could complicate. State-aligned infrastructure is best held privately; commercial revenue is harvested via the four pillars.

## 7. Voice and external commentary

- **State press**: Bao Chinh Phu, Tien Phong, Thanh Nien all framed the January 2025 blockchain tasking as a national capability commitment. The framing was patriotic, not commercial.
- **Vietnamese business press**: CafeF and ViettTimes consistently positioned One Mount as a "Vingroup x Techcombank" hybrid even after Vingroup's 2022 divestment, signalling that the popular narrative still attaches the Vingroup brand even when the cap table no longer does.
- **TheInvestor**, on Hung's appointment: "The leadership rotation between the major units in the ecosystem is a strategic move to optimize management resources and enhance integration capability between finance, technology, and real estate" [source: TheInvestor, Aug 2025, https://theinvestor.vn/former-techcombank-exec-appointed-ceo-of-technology-major-one-mount-group-d16525.html].
- **OutSystems** (October 2024): "OutSystems Powers One Mount Group's Digital Transformation in Vietnam"; the press release confirmed One Mount's adoption of OutSystems low-code platform across the operating units, a tell that the technical re-architecture started about a year before the CEO change [source: PR Newswire, https://www.prnewswire.com/apac/news-releases/outsystems-powers-one-mount-groups-digital-transformation-in-vietnam-302256796.html].

## 8. Read-across for WinX

What One Mount Group proves about the Vietnamese GT digitization opportunity at the parent-holdco level:

1. **The integrated four-pillar model loses to the focused single-pillar model on speed and unit economics.** Five years in, no pillar has reached take-out scale; VinShop / OneShop has only ~8.5% penetration of its addressable base, OneU has discontinued its e-wallet, OneHousing is a real-estate play. The cross-sell flywheel that would have justified the holdco architecture never materialised.
2. **The Vingroup divestment in Q1 2022 is the pivotal event in the franchise's history.** It severed the supermarket POS access from the loyalty card, which is the same data-loop break diagnosed for VinShop separately. Masan now owns the WinCommerce side of the loop; One Mount owns the OneU side; the two do not connect.
3. **The August 2025 Techcombank takeover and the January 2025 state-blockchain tasking together signal a strategic pivot away from venture-style commerce execution toward bank-aligned digital infrastructure.** The implication for WinX: One Mount as a competitor in GT digitization is fading; One Mount as a competitor in payments rails and tokenised loyalty is rising.
4. **The Techcombank financing pipe is the only piece of the One Mount stack that has survived without rebrand or restructure.** It is also the piece WinX most needs to either copy or partner around. A Masan-Techcombank conflict is structurally hard to avoid in any GT digitization scenario.

## 9. Thin-research flags

- One Mount Group consolidated P&L: not publicly disclosed at parent level.
- Internal capital allocation between the four pillars: not disclosed.
- True progress on the Layer-1 blockchain build: announcement-stage as of this research.
- Whether the four-pillar architecture survives the 2025 leadership change: open question; the OneShop relaunch in late October 2025 suggests the distribution arm is not being wound down.
