# Telio: deep narrative supplement

Companion to telio.md. Built for Dr. Quang's IC clearance read on the Vietnam GT digitization cohort. Coverage period: November 2018 founding through April 2026 founder repositioning. The base file documents capital, operating metrics, the OOPA litigation, and the wind-down sequence. This file fills in the experiments, the voice layer, the pivot ladder, the aftermath, the customer voice, and the IC clearance read.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran

**Telio Care Fund (December 2020 to early 2022).** A VND 1bn (~US$43,478) hardship fund formally launched at the Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City retailer recognition events at end of December 2020 and early January 2021. Mechanic: VND 5m (~$217) to VND 10m (~$435) per month income-replacement payout to retailers experiencing income loss from inpatient or outpatient medical events, conditional on having been an active Telio buyer. The fund honoured 600 mom-and-pop shops out of the 15,000 active retailers Telio counted at year-end 2020 [source: Vietnam Investment Review, "Telio announces Telio Care Fund to ensure business continuity for small retailers", 2021, https://vir.com.vn/telio-announces-telio-care-fund-to-ensure-business-continuity-for-small-retailers-82188.html; Vietnam Investment Review, "Telio Care supports struggling local retailers during COVID-19", 2021, https://vir.com.vn/telio-care-supports-struggling-local-retailers-during-covid-19-83135.html]. Lesson: the relationship layer never became a financing relationship; the Fund cap of VND 1bn against a 15,000-retailer base is roughly $3 per retailer of cumulative goodwill, which never converted to working-capital credit at the platform level. Telio did not run a Techcombank-equivalent merchant credit pipe.

**Zalo Mini App push (October 2020).** Telio became the first B2B platform inside the Zalo (VNG) messaging stack, against a then-quoted 64m monthly active user base. The bet: provincial reach without forcing app downloads. The cohort outcome: VNG anchored the November 2021 Pre-Series B at $22.5m partly on the strength of this rail [source: Hanoi Times, "Hanoi-based e-commerce platform raises US$22.5 million led by VNG", Nov 2021, https://hanoitimes.vn/hanoi-based-e-commerce-platform-raises-us225-million-led-by-vng-319261.html]. What happened: the Zalo channel scaled merchant onboarding, but Telio's principal warehousing model meant marginal merchants in tier-3 provinces still consumed warehouse and last-mile capex disproportionate to their order frequency. The acquisition channel worked; the unit economics did not.

**Vertical expansion into Lifestyle and Healthcare (2020 to 2021).** Layered on top of FMCG. Healthcare in particular was the most ambitious extension: pharma SKUs and medical-device distribution, in a regulated category, with no separate working-capital model. No traction milestone was ever announced in either category. By mid-2022 Phong characterised the de-prioritisation as "optimising and diversifying its product portfolio while avoiding low-margin items" [source: The Runway Ventures, "How Telio lost $1.4M/month at $3M/month revenue (and died)", 22 Jun 2025, https://www.therunway.ventures/p/telio]. Lesson: vertical expansion onto a loss-making FMCG core multiplied exposure without diversifying away from the core gross-margin trap.

**Teliobooks (2021).** A separate Android app for shop bookkeeping: payables and receivables, automatic SMS or Zalo reminders, summary reporting [source: Google Play, "TelioBooks (Quản lý cửa hàng đơn giản)", https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=vn.telio.book]. Adoption was never quantified in any company release. The product never integrated barcode scanning or POS; it was a debt ledger with a notification rail. Lesson: Telio digitised the merchant's order book toward Telio, then attempted to digitise the merchant's debt book, but did not digitise the till. Without the till, the data layer never closed.

**Geographic footprint expansion from 10 to 26 provinces (2021 to 2022).** Pre-Series B capital was deployed into province expansion at a velocity of roughly one new province every five weeks during 2021. Merchant count plateaued at ~60,000 by end-2022 even as province count moved from 10 to 26, implying per-province depth fell across 2022. The CEO statement on cost cuts in mid-2022 marks the rollback of the expansion thesis [source: The Runway Ventures, op cit].

**Q3 2024 capital and merger talks.** Phong told Tech in Asia in August 2024 that monthly losses had been cut 80% from $1.4m peak to $280k, with $2.5-3m monthly revenue and an EBITDA breakeven target by mid-2026. Bridge target: $10-15m. Both bridge fundraise and merger talks fell through; coverage cited "potential merger opportunities fell through" without naming a counterparty [source: DealStreetAsia Data Vantage, "Telio may have depleted runway and other updates", 2024, https://dealstreetasia.medium.com/data-vantage-telio-may-have-depleted-runway-and-other-updates-6df90dde9f35; The Runway Ventures, op cit]. The most plausible counterparty (per Vietnamese trade-press chatter) was VinShop / One Mount; no public confirmation has surfaced. Lesson: the strategic exit was assumed in the burn plan but never engineered; the natural acquirers (VinShop, MoMo, VNG, MEATLife) did not bid.

**Post-shutdown founder pivot: Kronia (2025 to 2026).** Phong incorporated Kronia, a Vietnamese AAA game development studio, in early 2025. VNG committed $2.5m of in-kind support over five years (engineers, artists, sound designers, project managers, infrastructure); total estimated development cost for Project P (debut title) of $10-12m [source: DealStreetAsia, "SE Asia Digest: Tokenize Xchange exits Singapore; VNG backs Telio founder's new venture", 2025, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/se-asia-digest-tokenize-vng-449779]. Lesson: VNG, the strategic shareholder that wrote off the largest single follow-on cheque on Telio, chose to back the founder personally on his next venture. This is a tell about how Vietnamese strategic capital prices the operator versus the asset post-failure.

## 4. Aftermath

**Founder.** Bui Sy Phong incorporated Kronia in early 2025, a Vietnamese AAA PC and console game development studio. VNG committed a $2.5m in-kind support package over five years: engineers, artists, sound designers, project managers, infrastructure. Project P, Kronia's debut title, has an estimated total development cost of $10-12m [source: DealStreetAsia, "SE Asia Digest: Tokenize Xchange exits Singapore; VNG backs Telio founder's new venture", 2025, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/se-asia-digest-tokenize-vng-449779]. The signal: the same Vietnamese strategic that wrote off Pre-Series B capital on Telio chose to back the operator on the next venture, separating the platform thesis from the operator's resilience.

**Co-founders.** Pham My Linh (Chief Strategy Officer) shows on LinkedIn as still Chief Strategy Officer at Telio with a tenure starting November 2018, suggesting she has not formally rebranded her profile post-cessation. No public announcement of a next role [source: LinkedIn, https://www.linkedin.com/in/linh-pham-aa3589129/]. Nguyen Nhat Huy (COO): no public destination announcement.

**Senior team and engineering.** The India tech team built around KeyValue Software Systems was a contractor relationship rather than direct hires; KeyValue continues operating as an independent entity [source: KeyValue case study on Telio, https://www.keyvalue.systems/case-studies/telio/]. The Vietnam-side engineering team has dispersed; LinkedIn shows ex-Telio profiles such as Ly Xuan Sang (https://www.linkedin.com/in/lyxuansang/) and Tuyet Duong (https://www.linkedin.com/in/tuyetduonghr/) without consolidated public destination data. **Closest sourced position: anecdotal absorption into MoMo, Tiki, GHN, and Masan / WinCommerce; no consolidated headcount placement disclosed.** Thin-research flag.

**Capital recovery.** Vietnam does not maintain a public Companies House equivalent; corporate filings live with the National Business Registration Portal and FiinPro for paid subscribers. Public coverage does not enumerate the creditor schedule. The dissolution was a member-led voluntary winding up rather than a court-supervised insolvency. With ~$2.5-3m monthly revenue and a small remaining cash position at cessation, severance for ~400 employees was reportedly paid out of remaining cash; brand and supplier payables outstanding were the largest write-off. No bankruptcy proceedings or creditor litigation have surfaced. Asia Private Equity Centre's note on the shutdown puts Tiger Global, Peak XV (Sequoia India), GGV, RTP Global, VNG, and Granite Oak as the principal write-offs [source: Asia Private Equity Centre, "Tiger Global, Peak XV-backed Telio Shuts Down", 2024, https://www.asiape.com/publication/tiger-global-peak-xv-backed-telio-shuts-down]. Granite Oak as sole Series B participant (August 2023, $15m) is the most-junior money in the cap table and the largest proportional loss given preferences likely flipped in a winding-up scenario.

**Physical assets.** Captive warehouses in HCMC and Hanoi; vehicles contracted via 3PL; IP including the Telio merchant app and Teliobooks. No public auction record; the most likely path is informal disposal during the wind-down period, with brand and SKU IP retained by OOPA Pte Ltd or its assigns given the 2021 Singapore court order. Thin-research flag: no public auction or asset-sale notice surfaced.

## 5. Customer voice (the most under-covered layer)

**App-store density.** The Telio merchant app (Telio.vn) and Teliobooks (vn.telio.book) on Google Play do not have publicly visible review density of the kind Indonesian peers (Bukalapak Mitra, Warung Pintar) accumulated. The Telio Vietnam Facebook page (https://www.facebook.com/Telio.vn/) carries the bulk of the merchant-facing public posts during 2019 to 2023; Vietnamese-language press paid more attention to the founder narrative than to merchant testimony. This is itself a finding: a B2B merchant platform that did not generate a public testimonial corpus did not embed deeply enough in shopkeeper daily life to surface organic content.

The journalist's 2020 visit captured the convenience-of-ordering benefit; nothing in the same piece on price, on delivery reliability beyond the 24-hour promise, or on returns.

**VinShop side commentary on Telio (competitor framing), Vietnamese trade press**: BizC.vn 2022 piece headlined "Telio: a heavy competitor of VinShop in the B2B e-commerce race." Source: VIR, op cit. Thin-research flag: the merchant testimonial archive does not exist at the depth available for VinShop's annual recognition events.

## 6. The IC clearance read

**Consumer truth.** Telio bet on a permanent atomic unit of demand: the Vietnamese mom-and-pop shopkeeper restocking weekly across hundreds of FMCG SKUs. The demand is real; the question is whether digital aggregation captures the value. The diagnostic from the burn arithmetic is that at the platform level, the demand exists but is too small per transaction to amortise principal-warehouse and last-mile costs. Phong's own framing: "Telio's scale wasn't big enough to make money selling small transactions at higher prices" [The Runway Ventures, op cit]. The atomic demand is permanent; the platform-margin capture is structurally constrained.

**Product truth.** The product delivered B2B order capture and 24-hour fulfilment in 26 provinces at peak. It did not deliver POS, financing, or trade-marketing measurement. Teliobooks shipped but never integrated barcode scanning. The product was a marketing-and-capital-led order channel that scaled merchant adds (60,000 cumulative onboarded) without solving the till-side data layer. The structural absence of a closed loop is a product-truth failure, not a marketing failure.

**Equity transferability.** Single product, not platform. Vertical extensions into Lifestyle and Healthcare were de-prioritised. Teliobooks and Telio Care Fund were peripheral, not generative. The Zalo Mini App rail was an acquisition channel that lived inside someone else's platform (VNG). Telio at cessation was a single-product B2B FMCG distributor with software in front; transfer value to a strategic acquirer was the warehouse footprint and the merchant relationships, both depreciating assets.

**Value creation sizing.** Total disclosed capital ~$64m; press-cited upper bound ~$92m. Peak post-money valuation closest sourced estimate $200-250m at Pre-Series B (Nov 2021); 2023 Granite Oak round was a down round to roughly half. No backer reached the $500m to $1bn bar. Tiger Global, Peak XV, GGV, RTP, VNG, and Granite Oak collectively wrote off the position. The economics did not converge to a tech-platform comp set.

**Competitive dynamics.** The structural gap Telio targeted (FMCG distribution into 1.4m mom-and-pop shops) is real. The structural advantage required to fill it is captive consumer brand, captive retail estate, captive financing, or captive logistics. Telio had none of these. Masan-style structural advantage (MCH brand portfolio + WinCommerce + MEATLife cold chain + Techcombank consumer financing pipe) was not available to Telio; its closest analogue (VinShop with One Mount + Techcombank + Vingroup-era commerce assets) reached only ~8.5% penetration of the addressable base over five years. The implication: this category is owned by structural balance sheets, not by venture capital.

**Learnability.** Masan's IC could have developed conviction on this category if the question were "should we build a B2B distribution layer onto our existing structural advantages." Masan's IC could not have developed conviction on this category if the question were "should we back a venture-funded standalone B2B distribution platform," which is what Telio was. The Telio post-mortem confirms the second answer; it does not falsify the first.
