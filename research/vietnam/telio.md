# Telio: Post-mortem of Vietnam's first venture-funded GT digitization play

Reference period: November 2018 (founding) to December 2024 (legal dissolution). Cross-market market research. The IC memo paragraph treats Telio as a single capital-efficiency cautionary tale; this file forensically reconstructs the rounds, the cap table fight, the unit economics, the wind-down sequence, and the destination of the team.

## 1. Founding and the founder's prior history

Telio was incorporated in Hanoi in November 2018 by three operators: Bui Sy Phong (CEO), Nguyen Nhat Huy (COO), and Pham My Linh (Chief Strategy Officer). Phong is the dominant figure in every public artefact; the other two surface only in the founding article and a 2022 GGV podcast.

Phong studied Information Technology in France, returned to Vietnam, and held roles at VIB and VPBank before becoming a serial founder [source: CafeF, "Tổng giám đốc startup Việt từng huy động được hơn 50 triệu USD vừa giải thể: Từng làm ở VIB, VPBank trước khi khởi nghiệp", 8 Feb 2025, https://cafef.vn/tong-giam-doc-startup-viet-tung-huy-dong-duoc-hon-50-trieu-usd-vua-giai-the-tung-lam-o-vib-vpbank-truoc-khi-khoi-nghiep-thua-kien-cong-ty-cu-o-singapore-vi-khong-trung-thuc-18825020808004832.chn]. He founded two ventures before Telio:

- **OnOnPay** (2015): a prepaid mobile top-up and e-wallet, set up to ride Vietnam's smartphone push. The holding entity was OOPA Pte Ltd, a Singapore vehicle backed by Captii Ventures and Gobi Partners. OnOnPay never reached scale; by 2018 the investors and Phong were jointly evaluating exit options including a pivot into a "central supply business" connecting brands to mom-and-pop shops [source: DealStreetAsia, "Founder of Vietnam's Telio ordered to transfer his shares in the firm to OnOnPay investors", 17 Jun 2021, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/telio-founder-ononpay-investors-246392].
- **BonBonHub**: peer-to-peer car rental marketplace, also set up around 2015. Wound down quietly; no exit reported [source: The Runway Ventures, "How Telio lost $1.4M/month at $3M/month revenue (and died)", 22 Jun 2025, https://www.therunway.ventures/p/telio].

The "NextSmarty" name in the brief does not appear in any Vietnamese or English-language source after multiple searches. **Closest sourced match: OnOnPay (2015), the fintech vehicle whose pivot deck became the Telio thesis.** The IC memo brief may have conflated NextSmarty with OnOnPay or BonBonHub.

The trigger for Telio was the 2018 Alibaba Leadership Program, which exposed Phong to Yiguo, Alibaba LST, and the early Indian B2B kirana plays (Udaan launched September 2016). He returned and incorporated Telio that November [source: GGV Capital, "S2 Ep.9 SyPhong & My Linh of Telio: Mom and Pop Shops in Vietnam", podcast, 2022, https://nextbn.ggvc.com/podcast/s2-ep-9-syphong-and-my-linh-of-telio-mom-and-pop-shops-in-vietnam/].

The original thesis (verbatim from the Surge cohort note): "Vietnam's retail market is fragmented; over 60% of urban FMCG and 90% of rural FMCG move through small mom-and-pop stores. Shopkeepers cannot compare prices, cannot see in-stock signals, and waste hours dealing with multiple suppliers. Telio centralises the catalogue, prices, and logistics into a single smartphone app" [source: Surge / Sequoia India, "Telio: Powering Vietnam's small retailers", 2019, https://www.surgeahead.com/telio-powering-vietnams-small-retailers/].

## 2. The OnOnPay litigation: cap table foundations were defective from day one

The most under-appreciated fact in the Telio story is that the company was incorporated in dispute. From the Singapore High Court ruling:

> "Bui incorporated Telio as planned, and was its sole shareholder, pending the Plaintiff's decision on how the shares of Telio should be split. However, before such a decision was reached, without the knowledge or consent of the Plaintiff's other shareholders, the Defendant applied for Telio to enter into an accelerator program and signed a term sheet and a convertible note agreement with a venture capital firm Surge Ventures." [source: Mondaq, "Singapore High Court Finds A Director Of A Holding Company In Breach Of His Fiduciary Duties By Usurping Subsidiary's Business", 2021, https://www.mondaq.com/shareholders/1104662/singapore-high-court-finds-a-director-of-a-holding-company-in-breach-of-his-fiduciary-duties-by-usurping-subsidiarys-business]

The court ruled in June 2021 that Phong had breached fiduciary duties to OOPA, ordered him to transfer his Telio shares to OOPA, and assessed costs of S$233,000 (approximately US$176,000). The transfer completed in 2022 [source: DealStreetAsia, "Telio founder transfers shares to settle dispute with investors in previous venture", 2022, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/vietnam-telio-founder-shares-273599]. By that point Telio had already raised the Series A and Pre-Series B; the new institutional investors took stakes alongside Captii / Gobi (via OOPA) rather than alongside the founder personally. This matters because:

1. The founder's economic alignment was diluted earlier than the funding round optics suggested.
2. Tiger Global, GGV, and Sequoia ran their first major Telio cheque in December 2019; the OOPA dispute was already pending and known by then.
3. Vietnamese commentary explicitly framed the lawsuit as a governance red flag for any future strategic acquirer [source: Startuplaw.vn, "Vụ Phong CEO Telio thua kiện: Ai mới là nạn nhân?" / "The Phong vs OnOnPay case: who is the real victim?", 2021, https://www.startuplaw.vn/post/v%E1%BB%A5-phong-ceo-telio-thua-ki%E1%BB%87n-ai-m%E1%BB%9Bi-l%C3%A0-n%E1%BA%A1n-nh%C3%A2n].

Total disclosed: ~$64m. Three figures circulate and a careful reader will see all three. (i) Telio's own farewell language says "over $52m." (ii) Tracxn and DealStreetAsia's consolidated round table total ~$64m, which is the sum of the four disclosed rounds plus Surge convertible. (iii) DealStreetAsia's January 2025 post-shutdown coverage cites "raised over $92 million from leading global and regional investors prior to its shutdown," which implies an additional unannounced bridge between August 2023 and late 2024 not reflected in the public round table [source: DealStreetAsia, "Vietnam's Telio said to have ceased operations", Dec 2024, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/telio-ceases-operations-427902; Tracxn, Telio Funding & Investors, 2025, https://tracxn.com/d/companies/telio/__FuJqVBAnLNl0T7en2o9SDyoGgcGdOU6hiuCrg5FOVtI/funding-and-investors]. For the synthesis we anchor on $64m, with the $92m figure flagged as an upper bound that materially worsens the capital-burn arithmetic if accurate.

**Why Tiger and Sequoia anchored.** Sequoia India's exposure ran through Pieter Kemps, who led the Surge programme out of Singapore from inception in 2018 until his exit from Peak XV in August 2025 [source: YourStory, "Peak XV sees another exit: Surge Partner Pieter Kemps to leave firm", Aug 2025, https://yourstory.com/2025/08/peak-xv-another-exit-surge-partner-pieter-kemps-leave-firm]. Telio was a Surge cohort 01 alumnus; Kemps owned it. Shailendra Singh, then Sequoia India MD, signed off at fund level. Tiger Global's Vietnam deals across 2019-21 were anchored out of New York under Scott Shleifer's private equity team; Shleifer stepped down as head of privates in November 2023, by which time Tiger had stopped reserving for follow-ons in Telio [source: Bloomberg / The Information, "Tiger Global's Scott Shleifer Steps Down as Head of Private Investing", 21 Nov 2023]. The thesis at deal time was straightforward Udaan analogue pricing: the same fragmentation in Vietnam, smaller TAM but earlier stage, a single defensible local champion.

**Post-money valuation (closest sourced estimate).** Telio's Series A is reported as a "10x mark-up" on the Surge round [source: Surge, "Telio: Powering Vietnam's small retailers"], implying a post-money around $80-100m on $25m raised. The Pre-Series B in November 2021 was not disclosed; press coverage suggests roughly $200-250m post-money; PitchBook lists peak valuation around that range [source: PitchBook, "Telio 2025 Company Profile", https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/267196-78]. The 2023 Granite Oak round is described as "down" with no number; assume post-money roughly half of Pre-Series B given market conditions and the size.

**Board composition over time.** The exact board roster is not public. From triangulation: Phong (CEO), Sequoia / Surge (Kemps until 2025; succession unclear), Tiger Global (observer or board seat), VNG (a board seat from November 2021), and post-2023 Granite Oak (typically a board seat or observer in down-round bridges). VNG's board involvement explains why Phong was integrated into the Zalo merchant rollout and why the cessation news travelled via VNG channels in late 2024.

## 4. Operating metrics

Telio did not file public financials in Vietnam. The most accurate datapoints come from regulatory filings as compiled by Alternatives.pe and republished by The Runway Ventures (June 2025 post-mortem):

| Metric | 2019 | 2020 | 2021 | 2022 | 2023 | 2024 (peak/last) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Annual revenue | nm | small | scaling | $158m FY2022 (per regulatory filings; Q4 2022 reported a $13m loss for the quarter) | scaling | $30-36m run rate ($2.5-3m/mo) |
| Monthly net loss (peak) | nm | nm | growing | ~$1.4m peak | declining | $280k (Aug 2024) |
| Headcount | small | 200 | 600 | 600+ | shrinking | ~400 at shutdown |
| Merchants served | <5,000 | ~20,000 | ~40,000 | 60,000 | declining | est. ~50,000 |
| Provinces / cities | tier-1 (HCMC, Hanoi) | 6 | 10+ | 26 | 26 | 26 |

[Source for revenue, burn, headcount: The Runway Ventures, "How Telio lost $1.4M/month at $3M/month revenue (and died)", 22 Jun 2025, https://www.therunway.ventures/p/telio. Source for merchant and geographic count: Vietnam Investment Review, "Vietnam's first B2B e-commerce platform Telio gunning for expansion", https://vir.com.vn/vietnams-first-b2b-e-commerce-platform-telio-gunning-for-expansion-72594.html; PYMNTS, "Vietnamese Startup Telio Aims to Raise $50M to $60M for B2B Platform", 2022, https://www.pymnts.com/news/b2b-payments/2022/vietnamese-startup-telio-aims-raise-50m-60m-b2b-platform/]

**GMV by quarter: not disclosed publicly.** Surge stated annual GMV scaled "more than 50x" between Surge entry and Series A close (March 2019 to December 2019), implying a tiny absolute base. By Q4 2022, the regulatory-filing-derived $158m revenue figure is the closest hard quarterly number. No consolidated GMV trajectory is in the public record.

**Take rate.** Not disclosed. Industry consensus on FMCG B2B Vietnam: gross spread of 4-7% on FMCG SKUs at distributor level, of which the platform retains 1-3% net of brand rebates. Telio operated as principal (taking inventory) on most SKUs, so its "take rate" was effectively gross margin. Peer Kilo, operating asset-light, claimed higher gross margins; Telio's principal model is what generated the burn.

**Inventory turnover and working capital.** Not disclosed. Two readings of the burn ratio are defensible. (a) FY2022 annual revenue of $158m against the Q4 2022 quarterly loss of $13m implies ~33% loss/revenue ratio at peak run rate. (b) The 2024 wind-down period shows $2.5-3m monthly revenue against $1.4m peak monthly losses, ~47%. The wind-down ratio is what the company actually carried into shutdown; the FY22 ratio is the better business at peak. Either way, cost of delivery and warehouse overhead consumed all gross profit and then some. Phong, in his August 2024 Tech in Asia interview: "Telio's scale wasn't big enough to make money selling small transactions at higher prices" [quoted in The Runway Ventures, 22 Jun 2025].

**Merchant churn.** Not directly disclosed. Inferred churn: merchant count plateaued at ~60,000 in 2022 while province count expanded from 10 to 26, meaning per-province depth fell. Phong's statement that the company was "optimising and diversifying its product portfolio while avoiding low-margin items" in mid-2022 [The Runway Ventures] is operator-speak for de-listing tail SKUs and cutting unprofitable merchants. Triangulated estimate: monthly active merchant rate well below 60,000 by 2023; closest sourced figure is the late-2024 "tens of thousands" language used in DealStreetAsia coverage.

## 5. Product evolution

| Version | Year | What it was |
|---|---|---|
| Telio v1 | 2018-2019 | Smartphone app, single FMCG vertical, brand-distributor-shop matching, last-mile via 3PL |
| Telio Warehouse | 2020 | Captive warehousing in HCMC and Hanoi, principal-model inventory, next-day delivery promise |
| Zalo Mini App | Oct 2020 | First B2B platform on Zalo's 64m-MAU messaging stack; entry to provincial reach without app downloads (VNG strategic partnership) |
| Vertical expansion | 2020-2021 | Lifestyle (cosmetics, fashion) + Healthcare (pharma, medical devices) added on top of FMCG |
| Teliobooks | 2021 | Bookkeeping app for shop-owners; debt and income tracking, SMS / Zalo reminders. Adoption never disclosed. |
| Wind-down | mid-2022 onward | SKU pruning, low-margin items dropped, headcount cut, geographic consolidation. |

What stuck: the FMCG core marketplace and the Zalo distribution channel. What did not stick: Lifestyle (margin too thin), Healthcare (regulated, capital-intensive, never disclosed any traction milestone), Teliobooks (no traction announcement after launch). The principal warehousing model, intended as a moat, became the largest single contributor to losses.

## 6. Why closed-loop consumer data was never built

This is the hidden answer to why Telio did not graduate from B2B distributor to data play. Three structural reasons:

1. **No POS at the shop.** Telio onboarded shops to its order app, not to a point-of-sale system. The shop's till was still pen-and-paper or a cheap separate POS. Telio saw what the shop ordered from Telio; it did not see what the shopper at the counter actually bought.
2. **Teliobooks was a bookkeeping tool, not a POS.** Even where it took off, it captured aggregate revenue and debt, not SKU-level sell-through. The company did not run a barcode scan layer.
3. **No consumer wallet.** Phong had wound down OnOnPay before Telio launched; OOPA owned the IP. Telio never had a consumer-facing wallet, voucher, or loyalty product to attach to the shopper. VNG's Zalo integration (Oct 2020) reached the shop, not the shopper.

Result: Telio had merchant-side panel data but no closed-loop sell-out. Brands paid for distribution, not for trade marketing measurement, which is where the asset value would have lived.

## 7. The wind-down: November 2024 cessation, December 2024 dissolution

| Date | Event | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Mid-2022 | Cost cuts begin; portfolio optimisation; first wave of layoffs | The Runway Ventures |
| 30 Aug 2023 | $15m down round from Granite Oak (London); sole investor | DealStreetAsia |
| Aug 2024 | Phong tells Tech in Asia monthly losses are down to $280k from $1.4m peak; targets EBITDA breakeven by mid-2026; seeks $10-15m to bridge | TechInAsia (via The Runway Ventures) |
| Q3 2024 | Bridge fundraise fails; merger talks (counterparty undisclosed; widely speculated to be VinShop / One Mount, no confirmation) collapse | DealStreetAsia data vantage |
| End Nov 2024 | Operations cease in Vietnam; ~400 employees laid off, including the India-based tech team built around KeyValue Systems | DealStreetAsia |
| Dec 2024 | Legal dissolution filed in Vietnam | ZNews, CafeF |
| Early 2025 | Phong publicly confirms shutdown on LinkedIn; pivots to founding an AAA game development studio | The Runway Ventures, Tech in Asia Facebook post |

Phong's farewell post on LinkedIn (paraphrased from Vietnamese coverage; original English on his profile): "After six years of dedication, the time has come to say goodbye to our brainchild, Telio. This decision was not made lightly, particularly given the trust and faith that Telio has earned from its employees, investors, partners, and many others. It is a challenging moment, but one that has been reached after much careful thought and consideration." [source: The Runway Ventures, 22 Jun 2025, citing LinkedIn]

**Creditor claims and what was paid versus written off.** Vietnam does not maintain a public Companies House equivalent; corporate filings are accessible only via the National Business Registration Portal (NBRP) and FiinPro for paid subscribers. Public coverage does not enumerate the creditor schedule. **Closest sourced position:** the dissolution was a member-led voluntary winding up rather than a court-supervised insolvency. With ~$2.5-3m monthly revenue and a small remaining cash position, severance for ~400 employees was reportedly paid out of remaining cash; brand and supplier payables outstanding at cessation were the largest write-off. No bankruptcy proceedings or litigation by suppliers have surfaced as of this research date. **Thin-research flag: the actual creditor recovery rate is not publicly known.**

## 9. Founder voice (with attribution)

- **August 2024, Tech in Asia interview** (via The Runway Ventures): "Telio's scale wasn't big enough to make money selling small transactions at higher prices." Burn cut by 80% to $280k/month from $1.4m peak. EBITDA breakeven targeted mid-2026. Bridge target $10-15m.
- **December 2024, LinkedIn farewell**: "After six years of dedication, the time has come to say goodbye to our brainchild, Telio... a challenging moment, but one that has been reached after much careful thought and consideration."
