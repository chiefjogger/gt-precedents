# Kamereo: the B2B foodservice supply platform that survived

Reference period: June 2018 (incorporation) to early 2025 (Hanoi launch under Series B). Built for the WinX IC memo. Kamereo is the only Vietnamese GT digitization play of the cohort still operating, raising in the public markets, and growing through the down cycle. The IC memo paragraph misses it entirely. This file explains why and what the model implies.

## 1. Founding

Kamereo was incorporated in June 2018 in Ho Chi Minh City by Taku Tanaka, a Japanese expatriate who had been COO at Mekong Capital-backed Pizza 4P's, the Saigon-headquartered restaurant chain [source: Vietnam Investment Review, "Taku Tanaka wants to show off Vietnam's immense potential", https://vir.com.vn/taku-tanaka-wants-to-show-off-vietnams-immense-potential-93392.html; Vietcetera, "KAMEREO, Vietnam's First Tech-Powered B2B Food Sourcing Platform, Raises $4.6 Million", 2021, https://vietcetera.com/en/kamereo-vietnams-first-tech-powered-b2b-food-sourcing-platform-raises-46-million-in-series-a-funding].

The original thesis is the Pizza 4P's pain point. Tanaka had spent three years buying produce, dry goods, and packaging for a multi-restaurant operation in Saigon. The fragmented supplier base, the inconsistent quality, and the manual purchase-order workflow generated the founder insight: foodservice procurement in Vietnam is functionally pre-digital, and an aggregator can lift gross margin by 5-10 percentage points if it controls quality, logistics, and last-mile.

Co-founder. Tracxn lists Hiroshi Tokaku as co-founder; he is not active in public communication. **Closest sourced position: Tanaka is the operating principal, Tokaku is a financial co-founder** [source: Tracxn, https://tracxn.com/d/companies/kamereo/__RF-AcTEPyF9O4FbggXSsxYv15wpm4GlmC9XKtQEv3x8].

The brief's mention of "Hori Nakajima" does not appear in any sourced material. **Assumed to be a misremembered reference to Tanaka or to the Sumitomo / Mitsubishi Japanese investor base.**

The Hanoi cohort: "within just three months of launching in Hanoi, Kamereo has rapidly established its presence in the food supply industry, serving over 100+ clients across various business models and scales" [source: Kamereo, Hanoi launch blog, 2025].

**Marquee customers.** Sofitel Legend Metropole Hanoi, SOJO Hotels, Fujimart, Paris Baguette, Starbucks Vietnam, Tanh Tach, Elyo, Labri, Habakuk (Michelin-recommended) [source: TechNode Global, Hanoi expansion, 2025]. The customer mix tilts to chains and high-end restaurants, not the long tail. This is the primary reason Kamereo's economics work where Telio's did not: average order value is far higher and order frequency is set by professional kitchens, not by random consumer demand at a corner shop.

**GMV, revenue, margin.** Not publicly disclosed. **Closest sourced estimate**: Series B raise size of $7.8m at typical SEA foodtech revenue multiples implies trailing revenue around $8-15m and a gross margin north of 15% (the level a Japanese strategic such as Sumitomo would underwrite).

**Take rate / model.** Hybrid: principal stocking on cold chain and high-margin SKUs, marketplace listing on tail SKUs. The three-temperature-zone warehouse stack (frozen, chilled, ambient) is the moat versus pure-marketplace plays.

## 4. Product evolution

| Layer | Year | What it did |
|---|---|---|
| Marketplace v1 | 2018-2019 | Web-and-app B2B catalogue connecting farms / suppliers to restaurants in HCMC |
| Cold chain | 2020-2021 | Three-temperature-zone warehousing in HCMC |
| Geographic expansion | 2021-2023 | Binh Duong, Vung Tau, Phan Thiet added |
| Marketplace push | 2024 | "Marketplace business" formally launched alongside core supply, using organic web traffic and the last-mile network |
| Hanoi launch | Feb 2025 | Northern operations live; chain customers ported across regions |

What stuck: the cold-chain spine, the Pizza 4P's-style chain customer base, the Japanese strategic capital. What did not: nothing has been wound down. Kamereo's product evolution is additive; it has not pivoted.

## 5. Why Kamereo survived where Telio did not

Five reasons, in order of importance:

1. **Customer mix.** Foodservice (B2B) buyers spend more per order and order more reliably than mom-and-pop FMCG retailers. Average order size at Kamereo is multiple times Telio's because professional kitchens replenish in case quantities and on schedule.
2. **Cold chain margin.** Temperature-controlled produce and meat carry 20-30% gross margin against the 4-7% gross margin in dry FMCG. Kamereo built into a high-margin pocket; Telio built into a low-margin one.
3. **Capital discipline.** $15m total over seven years, versus Telio's $64m over five. Kamereo never had the capital to over-hire or over-warehouse, so it did not.
4. **Strategic shareholder base from Japan.** CPF Group (Charoen Pokphand, Thailand), Sumitomo, Mitsubishi UFJ, SMBC, Reazon, Inspire. These are operating strategics with downstream commerce relationships, not financial VCs. Their capital comes with off-take demand, not just cash.
5. **Founder profile.** Tanaka was an operator (Pizza 4P's COO) before he was a founder. Phong (Telio) had a finance and venture-building background. The procurement diligence at Kamereo carries the operator's stamp.

## 7. Read-across for WinX

What Kamereo proves about the Vietnamese GT digitization opportunity:

1. **Margin and customer mix matter more than capital.** The successful Vietnamese B2B distribution player is in foodservice with cold chain, not in dry FMCG into mom-and-pop. WinX should weight foodservice as a separate and higher-quality TAM than the long-tail GT mom-and-pop base.
2. **Japanese strategic capital is the natural buyer pool in Vietnam B2B foodtech.** Sumitomo, Mitsubishi UFJ, SMBC, CPF (Thailand), and Reazon together form a coherent strategic syndicate. WinCommerce / Masan have a built-in advantage in this segment because of MEATLife and the existing cold-chain infrastructure; the Kamereo case argues the asset profile WinX needs is exactly what MEATLife already has.
3. **Capital discipline beats capital availability.** Seven years, four rounds, $15m total, still operating. The cohort-wide pattern is consistent: under-capitalisation and slow scaling outperform fast scaling on overcapitalisation.
4. **Founder background predicts outcome.** Operator-founders with sector pain experience win in low-margin distribution; finance founders chasing the venture comp set lose.
