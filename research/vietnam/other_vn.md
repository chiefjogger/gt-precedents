# Other Vietnam GT / MT digitization plays

A briefer scan of every other Vietnamese platform that touched general trade or modern trade digitization between 2018 and 2025. Built for the WinX IC memo. The IC memo's three-paragraph treatment names only Telio and VinShop; this file ensures nothing of relevance has been missed.

## 1. Kilo

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Founded | 2020, Ho Chi Minh City |
| Founder | Kartick Narayan, former CMO of Groupon, VP at Coupang, Chief Business Officer at Tiki |
| Thesis | B2B marketplace connecting wholesalers with MSMEs; multi-channel selling assistance via Kilo app, Facebook, Zalo |
| Capital | Pre-Series A: $5m, November 2021, lead Altos Ventures (per TechCrunch coverage) |
| Peak metric | 30,000 wholesalers and retailers on platform; stated target 1m businesses by year-end 2022 |
| Status | Operating but quiet since mid-2023; no follow-on funding round publicly disclosed since the 2021 round |

[Sources: TechCrunch, "Kilo, a Vietnamese B2B e-commerce platform for MSMEs, bags $5M pre-Series A", 14 Nov 2021, https://techcrunch.com/2021/11/14/vietnamese-b2b-e-commerce-platform-kilo-bags-5m-pre-series-a/; PYMNTS, "Vietnam's B2B Market Kilo Aims for 1M Customers Next Year", 2022, https://www.pymnts.com/news/b2b-payments/2022/vietnams-b2b-market-kilo-aims-for-1m-customers-next-year/]

What it tells the IC memo. Kilo went asset-light (no captive warehousing, multi-channel listing assistance) where Telio went principal. Kilo did not over-raise; it did not pivot publicly. The lower public visibility since 2023 is the signal: in the down market, the asset-light B2B model also struggles to scale because there is no moat and no inventory turn to monetise.

## 2. Loship (Lozi)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Founded | 2017 (delivery arm of Lozi, founded 2012) |
| Founder | Nguyen Hoang Trung (CEO), Vu Anh Tu (CTO) |
| Thesis | One-hour hyperlocal delivery and quick commerce, expanding from food delivery into B2B foodservice supply for restaurants |
| Capital | Pre-Series C August 2021: $12m co-led by BAce Capital (Ant Group affiliate) and Sun Hung Kai & Co; bridge round February 2021 with MetaPlanet Holdings (Skype co-founder Jaan Tallinn); cumulative ~$30m+ |
| Peak valuation | $100m at the August 2021 round (TechCrunch) |
| Stated ambition | NYSE IPO by 2024; explicitly framed as listing target |
| Status | Heavily downsized; reverted to debt-financing search by 2022; no public confirmation of full shutdown but operational footprint has materially contracted |

[Sources: TechCrunch, "Vietnamese on-demand e-commerce platform Loship raises $12M at a valuation of $100M", 3 Aug 2021, https://techcrunch.com/2021/08/03/vietnamese-on-demand-e-commerce-platform-loship-raises-12m-at-a-valuation-of-100m/; TechNode Global, "Vietnam's Loship raises $12M in pre-Series C funding", 2021, https://technode.global/2021/08/04/vietnams-loship-raises-12m-in-pre-series-c-funding-led-by-bace-capital-sun-hung-kai-co/; Nikkei via DealStreetAsia, "Vietnam delivery startup Loship said to be seeking debt financing", https://asia.nikkei.com/Spotlight/DealStreetAsia/Vietnam-delivery-startup-Loship-said-to-be-seeking-debt-financing; VIR, "Loship aims to file for IPO on New York Stock Exchange by 2024", https://vir.com.vn/loship-aims-to-file-for-ipo-on-new-york-stock-exchange-by-2024-86690.html]

What it tells the IC memo. Loship straddled GT and consumer delivery; the foodservice B2B leg never reached the depth Kamereo achieved. The 2021 NYSE IPO ambition collapsed when the SEA tech-listing window shut. **Closest sourced position on current status**: heavily contracted, debt-financed, no recent public funding round; no confirmed full dissolution as of search date. **Thin-research flag.**

## 5. MM Mega Market

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Origin | Acquired by Thai TCC Group from Metro Cash & Carry in January 2016; rebranded MM Mega Market Vietnam |
| Format | 21 Cash & Carry warehouses, 8 Food Service Depots, 6 sourcing platforms, 2 central warehouses across Vietnam |
| Role in GT digitization | Wholesale supplier to Vietnamese mom-and-pop shops; physical-format incumbent rather than digital platform |
| Status | Operating; the dominant traditional cash-and-carry wholesaler in Vietnam |

[Source: MM Mega Market Vietnam, About Company, https://mmvietnam.com/en/about-company/]

What it tells the IC memo. MM Mega Market is the analogue physical incumbent that any Vietnamese GT digitization platform competes with at the supply layer. It is also the visible counterargument to the digital-only platform thesis: a Thai-conglomerate-owned cash-and-carry chain has out-survived every venture-funded B2B FMCG platform in the country.

## 6. VinatexMart and other state-aligned distribution

The Vietnam Textile and Garment Group (Vinatex) and the state-influenced retail chains (Saigon Co.op, BRG, Hapro) operate variants of distribution but are not GT digitization plays in the venture-funded sense. They are mentioned because they appear in the broader Vietnamese B2B / MT landscape and are sometimes confused with the digital platforms in foreign coverage.

## 7. The "VietnamMM" and "On Group" references

Multiple searches against the names "VietnamMM" and "On Group" did not surface a venture-funded Vietnamese GT or MT digitization platform under those labels. Possible interpretations:

- "VietnamMM" likely refers to MM Mega Market Vietnam (covered above) rather than to a venture-funded platform.
- "On Group" likely conflates "One Mount" (covered separately) or "OnOnPay" (Phong's pre-Telio fintech, covered in telio.md).

**Closest sourced position: no separate venture-funded "VietnamMM" or "On Group" GT digitization play exists.** Treated as out of scope for this memo.

## 9. Cross-cohort observations

| Pattern | Telio | VinShop | Kamereo | Kilo | Loship | Other |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Customer focus | mom-and-pop FMCG | mom-and-pop FMCG | foodservice | mom-and-pop FMCG | quick commerce + foodservice | varies |
| Capital model | aggressive VC | corporate captive | discrete VC + Japanese strategic | small VC | mid-size VC | varies |
| Inventory model | principal | principal + 3PL | hybrid (cold chain principal) | asset-light marketplace | asset-light + last-mile | varies |
| Status as of 2025 | dissolved Dec 2024 | rebranded OneShop | operating | quiet since 2023 | heavily contracted | mixed |
| Capital efficiency at exit | $64m for ~$36m run rate | parent capex; rebrand | $15m total, growing | ~$5m disclosed | ~$30m+ | varies |

What this cross-section says about the cohort overall:

1. The mom-and-pop FMCG TAM has not yielded a self-sustaining business at any capital level, in any model.
2. The B2B foodservice TAM has yielded one: Kamereo.
3. The corporate-captive variant (VinShop) has reached the largest store count but has not produced a venture-style outcome.
4. The asset-light marketplace variant (Kilo) has not produced a venture-style outcome either.
5. The principal-warehouse variant (Telio) has produced an outright failure.

The IC memo's three paragraphs of Telio and VinShop coverage capture two of these five plays and inadvertently weight them as the totality of the cohort. The actual cohort has five plays, and the survivor profile (Kamereo) is the model the memo underweights.

## 10. Thin-research flags

- Loship's exact 2024-2025 status (still operating in some form vs. fully shut down): public sources do not converge.
- Selly's funding totals and current scale: thin and inconsistent in English-language press.
- Aemi's GMV and investor base: not consistently disclosed.
- Buymed's B2B distribution model is in pharma, not FMCG, and has not been benchmarked here.
- Smaller social-commerce plays (Mio, Hasaki for B2B beauty, others) excluded for thin-research reasons.
