# One Mount Group: deep narrative supplement

Companion to one_mount.md. Built for Dr. Quang's IC clearance read on the Vietnam GT digitization cohort. Coverage period: November 2019 incorporation through October 2025 OneShop relaunch and the August 2025 leadership rotation. The base file documents the four-pillar architecture, the shareholder evolution, the leadership timeline, and the January 2025 Layer-1 blockchain mandate. This file fills in the experiments at the holdco level, the voice layer, the pivot ladder, the aftermath of the August 2025 rotation, the merchant voice across the OneU/OneShop union, and the IC clearance read.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran (holdco level)

**Founding shareholder structure (Late 2019).** Dual-shareholder vehicle: Vingroup at 51.22%, Techcombank-aligned shareholders at the balance, with chairmanship held by Ho Anh Ngoc, brother of Techcombank chairman Ho Hung Anh, and CEO Nguyen Thi Diu, former Deputy CEO of Vingroup. The dynastic alignment to the Techcombank principals was deliberate from inception [source: ViettTimes, "Dich chuyen so huu tai One Mount Group", https://m.viettimes.vn/dich-chuyen-so-huu-tai-one-mount-group-post155669.html]. Charter capital ramped from VND 3,000bn (~US$130m) at inception to VND 4,047bn mid-2020 and VND 5,400bn mid-2021 [source: VnExpress International, "Digital platform One Mount Group restructures shares", 2022, https://e.vnexpress.net/news/companies/digital-platform-one-mount-group-restructures-shares-4444896.html]. Lesson: the dual-shareholder structure was unstable from formation; Vingroup's 51.22% mathematically gave it control, while Techcombank's family ties gave operating tilt. The Q1 2022 Vingroup divestment resolved the tension.

**Vingroup divestment (Q1 2022).** Vingroup fully divested its 51.22% stake; Techcombank-aligned shareholders absorbed it. The chairmanship moved to Nguyen Thi Thanh Thuy, wife of Ho Hung Anh. Vingroup's exit was simultaneous with its broader retail-asset divestment cycle (WCM and VinEco to Masan in late 2019, retreat from VinSmart phones, exit from VinFA pharma) [source: ViettTimes, "Vingroup rút lui, One Mount Group đang thuộc về ai?", 2022, https://viettimes.vn/vingroup-rut-lui-one-mount-group-dang-thuoc-ve-ai-post185645.html]. Lesson: the One Mount divestment was not a write-off; it was a clean shareholder restructuring. The implications for VinShop / OneShop are structural: from Q1 2022 onward, the platform sits inside a Techcombank-flavoured holdco rather than a Vingroup-flavoured one.

**OutSystems low-code rollout (October 2024).** Contractual partnership with OutSystems to power digital transformation across the operating units; press release confirmed adoption of the OutSystems platform across pillars [source: PR Newswire, "OutSystems Powers One Mount Group's Digital Transformation in Vietnam", Oct 2024, https://www.prnewswire.com/apac/news-releases/outsystems-powers-one-mount-groups-digital-transformation-in-vietnam-302256796.html]. Lesson: the technical re-architecture preceded the August 2025 CEO change by ten months. The platform unification work (which the OneShop relaunch operationalised at retailer-app level) started under Diu and was inherited by Hung.

**VinID rebrand to OneU (September 2024) and VinID Pay shutdown (May 2025).** VinID consumer loyalty (~12m users at 2024) was rebranded as OneU "lifestyle platform" with 300+ partner brands [source: Dan Tri, "VinID nâng cấp thành OneU", 7 Sep 2024, https://dantri.com.vn/kinh-doanh/vinid-nang-cap-thanh-oneu-nen-tang-phong-cach-song-chat-luong-20240907143652496.htm]. Eight months later, VinID Pay e-wallet was discontinued [source: VietnamBiz, "OneU dừng dịch vụ ví điện tử VinID Pay", 26 May 2025, https://vietnambiz.vn/oneu-dung-dich-vu-vi-dien-tu-vinid-pay-2025526152928408.htm]. Lesson: the consumer-side wallet leg of the closed-loop thesis (loyalty + wallet + B2B = sell-through visibility) was abandoned. This is the most-explicit signal that the original 2019-2020 four-pillar holdco thesis did not converge.

**State Layer-1 blockchain mandate (January 2025).** General Secretary To Lam at the 6th National Forum on Developing Vietnam's Digital Technology Enterprises tasked One Mount and one other organisation with building the "Make in Vietnam" Layer-1 blockchain. One Mount committed US$200-500m of capex [source: Bao Chinh Phu, 15 Jan 2025, https://baochinhphu.vn/one-mount-group-nhan-nhiem-vu-xay-dung-mang-blockchain-layer-1-make-in-vietnam-10225011516435619.htm; Thanh Nien, https://thanhnien.vn/one-mount-dau-tu-200-500-trieu-usd-xay-dung-mang-blockchain-layer-1-cua-viet-nam-185250115155946827.htm]. As of April 2026 the public record covers the announcement and the design criteria (high speed, scalability, security, advanced consensus mechanism); a testnet launch date has not been announced. Lesson: the mandate repositions One Mount as a state-aligned digital infrastructure vehicle, not a venture-funded commerce platform. The GT digitization business (VinShop / OneShop) becomes a feeder to the mother venture (digital infra), not the venture itself.

**August 2025 Techcombank-led leadership rotation.** Phung Quang Hung (former permanent Deputy CEO of Techcombank) replaced Diu as CEO from 1 August 2025; Diu rotated into Permanent Vice Chair of the One Mount Board, plus Vice Chair seats at Techcom Securities (TCBS) and Masterise Group [source: TheInvestor, "Former Techcombank exec appointed CEO of technology major One Mount Group", Aug 2025, https://theinvestor.vn/former-techcombank-exec-appointed-ceo-of-technology-major-one-mount-group-d16525.html; VnExpress, 1 Aug 2025, https://vnexpress.net/sep-cu-techcombank-lam-tong-giam-doc-one-mount-group-4921672.html]. Vietnam Finance reproduced Hung's CV: TCB joined 2007, permanent Deputy CEO from 2018 to 2025 [source: Vietnam Finance, "Ông Phùng Quang Hưng thay bà Nguyễn Thị Dịu làm CEO One Mount Group", https://vietnamfinance.vn/ong-phung-quang-hung-thay-ba-nguyen-thi-diu-lam-ceo-one-mount-group-d130470.html]. Lesson: the rotation moves One Mount from Vingroup operator lineage to Techcombank operator lineage, four years after the cap table moved.

**OneShop launch with MM Mega Market partnership (October 2025).** The relaunch was joint-keynoted by Le Thiet Bao (One Mount Distribution CEO) and Nguyen Duc Toan (MM Mega Market Vietnam CEO). MM Mega Market is the cash-and-carry arm of TCC Group (Thai), the largest Thai FMCG distributor in Vietnam [source: VietnamPlus, op cit]. Lesson: the relaunch consciously built a wholesale-cash-and-carry rail into the platform, mitigating the principal-warehouse capex problem that killed Telio. The Thai conglomerate wholesale tie-in is structurally analogous to Sumitomo's Fujimart tie-in for Kamereo.

## 2. Voice layer

**General Secretary To Lam (state-level voice on the blockchain mandate, 15 January 2025)**: framed One Mount's tasking as central to building national blockchain infrastructure; design criteria "high speed, exceptional scalability, optimal security and a consensus mechanism" with the platform as a "core foundation, facilitating operations, exploitation, interaction and interoperability across various types of blockchain networks" [source: Bao Chinh Phu, 15 Jan 2025, https://baochinhphu.vn/one-mount-group-nhan-nhiem-vu-xay-dung-mang-blockchain-layer-1-make-in-vietnam-10225011516435619.htm].

**Phung Quang Hung (CEO from 1 August 2025), inaugural statement, Vietnam.vn**: "Accepting the assignment at One Mount is not just a new position, but a call to serve at a higher level. My goal is to lead the group to develop its business on an excellent technology and data platform, ensuring seamless integration, turning One Mount into a solid launching pad, helping Techcombank and the entire HST [hệ sinh thái = ecosystem] to make a stronger breakthrough." Source: vietnam.vn, "Techcombank - One Mount rotates leadership, optimizes ecosystem strength", Aug 2025, https://www.vietnam.vn/en/techcombank-one-mount-luan-chuyen-lanh-dao-toi-uu-suc-manh-he-sinh-thai. The "launching pad for Techcombank" framing is the cleanest single-sentence positioning of One Mount's post-2025 role.

**Nguyen Thi Diu (CEO 2020 to 2025)**, retrospective from VIR profile, 2021: "Our objective is to contribute to the development of Vietnam's economy by setting up the technological foundation for businesses. This is expected to help them add value to their customers' experience with products and services that are at a more competitive price." Source: Vietnam Investment Review, "A closer look at One Mount Group", 2021, https://vir.com.vn/a-closer-look-at-one-mount-group-and-the-vision-to-build-vietnams-largest-digital-ecosystem-80387.html.

**TheInvestor editorial framing on the August 2025 rotation**: "The leadership rotation between the major units in the ecosystem is a strategic move to optimize management resources and enhance integration capability between finance, technology, and real estate." Source: TheInvestor, op cit.

**OutSystems Vietnam case-study positioning (October 2024)**: framed One Mount as "Vietnam's largest technological ecosystem that creates solutions along the entire value chain, starting with retail, distribution, real estate, and financial services" [source: PR Newswire, op cit].

**Glassdoor anonymous employee voice (109 reviews, 3.6/5 aggregate)**: positive themes ("team is super supportive"; compensation 3.8/5); negative themes ("Heavy workload, hierarchical management, unclear vision" in 5 reviews; "controversial culture and morality" in 3 reviews). Source: Glassdoor, https://www.glassdoor.com/Reviews/One-Mount-Group-Reviews-E4867862.htm.

**ITviec Vietnamese-language reviewer voice on One Mount (24 reviews aggregate)**: salary and team support positive; hierarchy and meeting culture flagged. Source: https://itviec.com/companies/one-mount-group/review.

**Vietnamese trade press editorial framing (CafeF, 16 January 2025, day after the blockchain tasking)**: "Thân thế One Mount Group" / "The identity of One Mount Group" piece characterised the group as the entity "founded by Vingroup and Techcombank, owning an app with 12 million users" assigned to invest up to $500m in blockchain. Vietnamese original: "doanh nghiệp được Tổng bí thư giao nhiệm vụ đầu tư 200-500 triệu USD xây mạng Blockchain Layer 1 Make in Vietnam." The framing in domestic press still attaches the Vingroup brand to One Mount even three years after Vingroup's exit.

## 3. The pivot ladder (holdco level)

| Order | Date | Trigger | Bet | Stuck or rolled back |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Late 2019 | Post-WCM divest reset of Vingroup tech strategy | Form One Mount as Vingroup x Techcombank tech ecosystem | Stuck through 2022 |
| 2 | Oct 2020 | First operating leg | VinShop B2B distribution | Stuck (rebranded OneShop 2025) |
| 3 | Tet 2021 | Need a moat | Techcombank merchant working-capital pipe | Stuck |
| 4 | 2021 to 2022 | Real estate ambition | OneHousing scale-up; charter capital VND 33bn to VND 620bn | Stuck |
| 5 | Q1 2022 | Vingroup retail/tech retreat | Vingroup divests 51.22%; Techcombank-aligned absorb | Stuck |
| 6 | 2022 | Three-pillar pivot validation | Continue VinShop, VinID, OneHousing under new shareholder structure | Stuck |
| 7 | Sep 2024 | VinID brand fatigue | Rebrand VinID to OneU as lifestyle platform | Partial; e-wallet leg killed eight months later |
| 8 | Oct 2024 | Technical platform integration need | OutSystems low-code rollout | Stuck |
| 9 | Jan 2025 | State digital sovereignty agenda | Layer-1 blockchain mandate, $200-500m commitment | In progress |
| 10 | May 2025 | OneU monetisation review | Discontinue VinID Pay e-wallet | Done; ratifies abandonment of closed-loop thesis |
| 11 | 1 Aug 2025 | Strategic Techcombank consolidation | Phung Quang Hung from TCB to One Mount CEO | Stuck |
| 12 | Late Oct 2025 | Decree 70 e-invoicing wave; five-year VinShop mark | OneShop relaunch with POS, e-invoicing, voice ordering, MM Mega Market partnership | Just-launched; outcome pending |

The biggest "what didn't stick" inside the One Mount stack is the closed-loop consumer data layer that the four-pillar architecture was designed to deliver. The Vingroup divestment in Q1 2022 severed the supermarket POS access (which moved to Masan with WCM in late 2019); the May 2025 e-wallet shutdown ratified the consumer-side break. The remaining moats are (a) the Techcombank merchant credit pipe, (b) the OneHousing real-estate stack, (c) state-aligned blockchain infrastructure, (d) the OneShop B2B distribution rail. None of these directly closes the consumer-to-merchant data loop that was the original thesis.

## 4. Aftermath

**CEO transition**. Diu's rotation is operating, not departure: she retains Vice Chair seats across One Mount, TCBS, and Masterise Group [source: VnExpress, 1 Aug 2025, op cit]. Hung is the operational CEO; Diu the institutional bridge into Techcombank's broader investment franchise. The structure resembles a holdco vice-chair model rather than a clean succession.

**Senior team**. Le Thiet Bao continues as CEO of One Mount Distribution. The October 2025 OneShop launch was led by Bao with Hung not visibly in the press cycle, suggesting an operating-team continuity rather than top-to-bottom restructure.

**Ho Anh Ngoc (founding chair, brother of TCB chairman) and Nguyen Thi Thanh Thuy (current chair, wife of TCB chairman)**: both remain in board roles per the public record [source: ViettTimes, 2022, op cit]. The dynastic alignment to the Techcombank principal household persists across the rotation.

**Engineering and ops staff**. No public layoff notice has accompanied the rotation. The Glassdoor review density (109 reviews to date) suggests a stable mid-1000s headcount across One Mount Distribution. The OneShop relaunch (rebrand, not replatform) implies the engineering team carried forward.

**Capital recovery on VinID Pay shutdown (May 2025)**. End-user balances reportedly migrated or refunded; no public creditor schedule. The wallet was the consumer-side leg of the closed-loop thesis, and its closure is the single most-explicit admission that the thesis did not work in its original form.

**Physical assets**. VinShop branded warehouses migrating into OneShop; the VinShop merchant app rebranded as OneShop with the same Google Play package ID. The OneHousing real-estate stack continues as a separate operating unit. The blockchain build is announcement-stage; no physical or technical asset disclosures yet.

**Capital recovery at parent level**. None required; One Mount is operating, parent shareholders (Techcombank-aligned) hold at parent equity. The $200-500m blockchain commitment is operating capex over the build cycle, sourced from parent capital. No external priced rounds have been reported.

**State alignment narrative**. State press (Bao Chinh Phu, Tien Phong, Thanh Nien) consistently frame the January 2025 blockchain tasking as a national-capability commitment. This framing strengthens through 2026; competitor press (e.g., Vietnamese fintech commentary) treats the blockchain tasking as a strategic moat, not a commercial venture.

## 5. Customer voice

**Joint platform structural problem**. One Mount's customer voice splits across three audiences: OneU consumers (12m+ historical, post-merger; e-wallet users now displaced), VinShop / OneShop merchants (120k+ at OneShop launch), and OneHousing real-estate buyers (separate cohort). The consolidated voice corpus is fragmented; no single platform comment thread captures the merchant-and-consumer joint view.

**Merchant voice through OneShop launch**. The October 2025 launch press carried operating-merchant testimonials filtered through MM Mega Market and tax-authority partnership angles, not direct shopkeeper quotes [source: VietnamPlus, op cit; vietnam.vn OneShop Synergy piece, 2025, https://www.vietnam.vn/en/oneshop-hop-luc-cong-nghe-tai-chinh-nguon-hang-de-thuc-day-ban-le-viet]. Thin-research flag: a journalist's visit to a OneShop pilot store has not surfaced.

**Consumer voice through OneU**. The Dan Tri 7 September 2024 piece on the VinID-to-OneU rebrand framed the upgrade as a "lifestyle platform with 300+ partner brands" without surfacing direct consumer testimonials [source: Dan Tri, op cit]. The VietnamBiz 26 May 2025 piece on the VinID Pay e-wallet shutdown carried complaint-style framing on the abrupt closure but no quoted consumer voice was admissible as IC voice.

**Glassdoor and ITviec employee voice (already cited above)** is the most consistent public voice corpus on One Mount as an operating organisation. The aggregate 3.6/5 Glassdoor rating with 67% would-recommend is mid-tier for a Vietnamese tech employer.

**Comparative context: Vietnamese press still cites VinShop / OneShop as the leading FMCG B2B distributor against the Telio shutdown narrative**. The dominant trade-press positioning since Q4 2024 is that One Mount won the VinShop-vs-Telio race by virtue of structural staying power, even if not by IPO-eligible economics.

## 6. The IC clearance read

**Consumer truth.** One Mount bet on three atomic units of demand simultaneously: the Vietnamese consumer through VinID/OneU, the Vietnamese mom-and-pop shopkeeper through VinShop/OneShop, and the Vietnamese homebuyer through OneHousing. Each is a permanent atomic unit. The error in the original thesis was treating the integration of the three as additive rather than multiplicative; the cross-unit data flywheel never materialised. The May 2025 e-wallet shutdown ratified the disintegration. The OneShop relaunch in October 2025 implicitly accepts that the platform competes on the merchant side alone, with finance-rail integration via Techcombank and goods-rail integration via MM Mega Market, but without consumer sell-through visibility.

**Product truth.** Five years of corporate capital reached 120k merchants on the VinShop side, 12m users on the OneU side, and a small priced-deal real-estate base on the OneHousing side. Each pillar shipped product. The pillars did not integrate end-to-end. The OneShop October 2025 rebrand added POS, voice ordering, and e-invoicing, demonstrating that the original 2020-2024 product was sub-feature relative to current regulatory and merchant needs. This is a product-truth failure relative to the holdco's launch ambition; it is a product-truth pass relative to a single-pillar B2B distributor benchmark.

**Equity transferability.** Holdco-architected, single-pillar in delivery. The four pillars do not cross-sell into each other in a way that would justify the holdco multiple. The cross-pillar synergy that would have enabled holdco transfer value (e.g., to a Thai or Japanese strategic acquirer) has not materialised. The state-aligned blockchain tasking creates a separate transfer-value question: is One Mount transferable at all, given its national-infrastructure positioning?

**Value creation sizing.** No external priced round at parent. "Next unicorn" framing in 2021 press never validated. Closest sourced peak valuation: $1bn implied, never confirmed. Of the four pillars, only the Techcombank financing pipe and OneHousing arguably approach a $500m to $1bn standalone-pillar value mark; the two together inside One Mount Group are illiquid relative to their underlying economics. No backer has reached the venture-grade $500m to $1bn bar; parent-equity-held value sits closer to a Vietnamese conglomerate-segment multiple.

**Competitive dynamics.** The structural gap One Mount targeted (integrated retail + finance + real-estate ecosystem against the Vietnamese consumer and merchant base) is real but contested. The August 2025 leadership rotation and the October 2025 OneShop relaunch confirm One Mount is consolidating around Techcombank as the structural advantage. Masan's structural advantage (WinCommerce modern-trade footprint, MEATLife cold chain, MCH brand portfolio) sits in adjacent rather than directly competitive territory: Masan owns the modern-trade plus consumer-brand stack; One Mount owns the GT-distribution plus consumer-loyalty stack. The competitive question for WinX is whether to compete with One Mount on the GT distribution rail, partner with it (e.g., MCH brands listing on OneShop), or build alongside.

**Learnability.** Masan's IC could develop conviction on a partnership with One Mount that listed MCH and MEATLife SKUs on OneShop while WinX builds independent shop-POS rails through WinCommerce structural assets. Masan's IC could not develop conviction on a clone-of-One-Mount four-pillar holdco play; the corporate-funded incumbent has run that play for five years and the pillars do not integrate. The state-aligned blockchain tasking is a learnability constraint: Masan's IC framework does not stretch cleanly to state-tasked digital infrastructure decisions.
