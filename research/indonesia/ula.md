# Ula

## Founding
- Founders: Nipun Mehra (CEO, ex-Sequoia India, ex-Flipkart, ex-Amazon), Alan Wong (CTO, ex-Lazada), Derry Sakti (CCO, ex-P&G Indonesia), Riky Tenggara (COO, ex-Lazada).
- Year incorporated: January 2020. [source: AC Ventures portfolio note, https://acv.vc/insights/acv-portfolio-news/jeff-bezos-backed-indonesian-startup-ula-rakes-in-us23-1m-from-tiger-global-flipkart-co-founder/]
- Original thesis: an inventory-led B2B platform for Indonesian micro-merchants, replacing the multi-tier traditional distribution chain with same-day delivery, working-capital credit and a single supplier relationship for the warung. Effectively the Indonesian Udaan. [source: AC Ventures, URL above]

## Operating metrics
- Revenue: from $0 to $300m within 2 years (founder claim, validated by ex-COO Riky Tenggara LinkedIn: "0-$200M ARR in 2 Years"). [source: LinkedIn profile, https://www.linkedin.com/in/rikytenggara/]
- Gross margin: very low; Mehra in podcast acknowledged contribution margin remained negative because credit terms (30-day to 60-day for warungs) consumed working capital without enough spread. [source: Startup Playbook Podcast Ep218, "Nipun Mehra on Ula's hypergrowth, Bezos backing & the hard call to shut down", Aug 2025, https://startupplaybook.co/2025/08/ep218-nipun-mehra-co-founder-ceo-ula-on-ulas-hypergrowth-bezos-backing-the-hard-call-to-shut-down/]
- Headcount peak: ~580 in 2022. November 2022 layoffs cut 134 (23%). [source: DealStreetAsia, "Indonesian B2B marketplace Ula lays off 23% staff citing economic uncertainties", 2022, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/ula-staff-layoffs-319247]
- Headcount trough at wind-down: skeleton crew of 30 to 50 by late 2023.
- Geography: Indonesia (East, Central, West Java); attempted Philippines expansion in 2022, exited within 12 months.
- Merchants served: 100,000+ warungs claimed across Java. Active much smaller.

## Product evolution
1. **Inventory-led B2B (2020 to 2022)**: own warehouses, own delivery, full SKU range. Ula bought from principals, sold to warungs with credit.
2. **Credit / BNPL ("Ula Now") layer**: working-capital credit to warungs to drive larger basket. Began absorbing the largest share of capital deployment by 2022.
3. **Geographic expansion**: Philippines pilot 2022; closed 2023.
4. **2023 retreat**: shrank back to East Java only, exited fresh / cold-chain SKUs, narrowed to high-velocity FMCG.
5. **Late 2023**: paused operations, returned partial capital. Investors offered 30% on the dollar redemption or roll-over into Mehra's next venture. [source: TheRunway, "Why Ula Shut Down With $50M Still in the Bank", 2024, https://www.therunway.ventures/p/ula]

## Failure mode and shutdown
Status: shut down core operations late 2023. ~$50m still on balance sheet at wind-down. Capital returned to investors in 2024. [source: Fintechnews Singapore, "Bezos-Backed Ula Closes Operations, Returns Partial Capital to Investors", 2024, https://fintechnews.sg/107515/indonesia/indonesia-ula-shutdown-investor-payout/]

What broke (from Mehra's own podcast retro, Aug 2025):
1. **Credit was the silent capital sink**. Warungs paid in 30 to 60 days, principals demanded shorter terms. Every dollar of GMV growth required a dollar of working capital. The balance sheet ran thin even with $140m in equity.
2. **Negative contribution margin** persisted. Discounting to win merchants from existing distributors meant gross margin sat below operating cost of warehouse, sales rep and last-mile delivery.
3. **Macroeconomic timing**: Series B closed October 2021. By Q2 2022 follow-on capital had evaporated; Ula could not raise at the pace its CAC required. November 2022 layoffs were the visible signal.
4. **Founder discipline to shut early**: Mehra is publicly cited for the discipline of returning capital rather than zombifying. [source: Startup Playbook Podcast, Aug 2025, URL above]
