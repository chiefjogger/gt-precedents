# Dagangan: deep supplement

Companion to dagangan.md. Adds experiments, voice, pivot ladder, customer voice, IC read.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran

### 1.1 Rural FMCG e-commerce app launch (2019 to 2021)
Founded 2019 by Ryan Putera Pratama Manafe (CEO, ex-McKinsey, ex-CEO SUN Energy), Wilson Yanaprasetya (CTO), Maha Willy Chandra (COO). Three operating founders, atypical of the cohort (most were single founder or CEO + technical co-founder). Original product: Android app and WhatsApp ordering for villagers in Tier-3 and Tier-4 Indonesian villages, with delivery from local hubs. Owner: Manafe strategic, Chandra ops, Yanaprasetya platform. What stuck: the hub network and the agen-led ordering channel.

### 1.2 Series A (Sep 2021)
Series A September 2021 at $11.5m, led by Monk's Hill Ventures with MMS Group, Spiral Ventures, PnP, K3 Ventures. [source: Monk's Hill press release, https://www.monkshill.com/content-hub/dagangan-raises-11-5-million-in-series-a-to-expand-across-indonesia-and-boost-rural-economic-growth-led-by-mhv]

### 1.3 Village agent ("agen") network expansion (2021 onwards)
Built a network of village-level sub-agents (often ibu-ibu) who aggregate orders from neighbours. Social commerce overlay on the rural FMCG platform. By 2024 Dagangan claimed 30,000+ MSMEs supported as village agents and 25,000+ communities served. [source: WARC, "The rural potential: How Dagangan navigates Indonesia's retail landscape beyond urban centres", https://www.warc.com/newsandopinion/opinion/the-rural-potential-how-dagangan-navigates-indonesias-retail-landscape-beyond-urban-centres/en-gb/6629]

### 1.4 Pre-Series B with BTPN Syariah Ventura (Jun 2022)
Pre-Series B June 2022 at $6.6m from BTPN Syariah Ventura (SMBC subsidiary). Earmarked for financial-services products. [source: AsiaTechDaily, https://asiatechdaily.com/indonesian-rural-e-commerce-startup-dagangan-raises-6-6m-funding/] The structure is critical: BTPN Syariah carries balance-sheet credit risk; Dagangan earns origination, not credit risk. This is the model Ula did not adopt and BukuWarung did not execute properly.

### 1.5 Hub-and-spoke physical infrastructure (2022 to 2024)
By 2022 Dagangan had 40+ hubs (rural micro-warehouses) spread across Java. [source: MARKETECH APAC, https://marketech-apac.com/social-commerce-firm-dagangan-introduces-online-merchandise-shopping-services-for-rural-areas/] Through 2024 the network grew to 50 hubs across Java per Devoteam customer story. [source: Devoteam, https://www.devoteam.com/success-story/dagangan-delivering-opportunity-to-indonesias-rural-economy-at-scale/] Hub capex matched to the geography rather than subsidised growth; each hub is small enough to break even per location.

### 1.6 Brand activation collaborations (2022 to 2024)
Dagangan ran integrated online-to-offline visibility display contests with FMCG principals. The Ultra Jaya partnership (rewarding warungs that displayed Ultra Jaya products on shelves with gold rewards) over three months produced 106% growth in gross merchandise value for Ultra Jaya. [source: Dagangan blog impact report, https://blog.dagangan.com/2023/03/20/dagangans-impact-report-2022/] This is the principal-side monetization layer that GudangAda and Warung Pintar Iris also targeted; Dagangan's geographic carve-out makes the principal-side ROI more measurable.

### 1.7 Series B (May 2024)
Series B closed May 2024 at undisclosed amount, undisclosed lead. [source: Crunchbase, https://www.crunchbase.com/organization/dagangan; Tracxn, https://tracxn.com/d/companies/dagangan/] Total raised $57.6m per Tracxn aggregator; other trackers cite $18.1m disclosed. The discrepancy reflects undisclosed Series B size.

### 1.8 Impact metrics through 2024
- 75,000+ traditional market sellers, small shop owners, and MSMEs in rural areas empowered.
- Warung owners shopping for inventory through the Dagangan app reportedly increased income by up to 60%.
- 8,000+ villages reached.
- 1m+ rural villagers served.
- 12% lower delivered prices for end consumers; 40% to 60% lower logistics cost vs traditional channel (Dagangan internal claim, not independently verified).

## 3. The pivot ladder: geographic carve-out, not pivots

Dagangan has not pivoted in the cohort sense. The arc is layered expansion within the same wedge.

| Layer | Date | What launched | Capital deployed | What stuck |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1: Rural FMCG app + WhatsApp | 2019 to 2021 | Android app, WhatsApp ordering, hub delivery | Seed, undisclosed | Yes, durable |
| 2: Village agent network | 2021 | Ibu-ibu sub-agents aggregating orders | Series A, $11.5m | Yes, scaled to 30,000+ agents |
| 3: BTPN Syariah financing partnership | Jun 2022 | Sharia-compliant micro-financing through regulated lender | $6.6m pre-B | Yes, origination-only model held |
| 4: Brand activation programmes | 2022 to 2024 | Principal-side display contests | residual cash | Yes, Ultra Jaya 106% GMV uplift |
| 5: Series B continued expansion | May 2024 | Undisclosed lead and amount | Series B undisclosed | Live |

The cohort's structural failure modes (negative contribution margin, working-capital intensification, head-to-head with Mitra Bukalapak / Tokopedia, ZIRP timing) do not apply because:

- Tier-3 and Tier-4 villages are where Mitra Bukalapak, Mitra Tokopedia, Ula and GudangAda did not compete. Each is concentrated in cities or peri-urban warungs. Dagangan owned the underserved rural lane.
- Capital discipline: ~$18m to $58m disclosed across rounds vs Ula's $140m. Lower burn ceiling.
- Hub-and-spoke physical model: capex matched to the geography, not subsidised growth.
- BTPN Syariah carries balance-sheet credit risk; Dagangan earns origination, not credit risk.
- Three operating founders versus the single-founder pattern that hurt GudangAda and Wahyoo.

## 4. Aftermath

Status: live, growing, capital-disciplined. Not in failure. Within the cohort, Dagangan is one of two non-failures alongside Mitra Bukalapak / Mitra Tokopedia (which are platform programmes, not standalone companies).

Risk factors not yet visible:
- 2024 Series B amount is undisclosed; if it was a flat or down round, the company may be quietly capital-constrained.
- Rural FMCG demand is structurally smaller per household than urban; ceiling on category expansion.
- Net merchant count claim (75,000 in 2022 to 30,000+ MSMEs in 2024) shows definitional drift; the 75,000 figure is "traditional market sellers, small shop owners and MSMEs" while the 30,000+ figure is "MSMEs supported as village agents." The categories overlap but are not identical.

## 5. Customer voice (rural village agents and warung owners)

- Dagangan publishes its own impact reporting through blog.dagangan.com. The 2022 impact report cites 75,000 digitalised warungs and 10m units of cooking oil distributed (including Minyakita). [source: blog.dagangan.com, https://blog.dagangan.com/2023/03/20/dagangans-impact-report-2022/]

- The income uplift framing (up to 60% for warungs, up to 2x for some shop owners) appears across Dagangan's own communications. The figures are company-published, not third-party verified, but the consistency across multiple touchpoints (Asia Tech Podcast, blog, WARC) gives them more weight than single-source claims.

- Bahasa coverage in regional outlets is positive on the rural-affordability angle. The Minyakita distribution role anchors Dagangan in the government-subsidised programme narrative, which carries durable public-sector legitimacy.

- The Google Play page for "Dagangan Sembako & Bahan Baku" (com.dagangan.mall) carries Bahasa Indonesia reviews from village agents and warung owners. The review pattern is positive on price and consolidation; common complaint patterns are out-of-stock issues for high-velocity SKUs in rainy seasons (logistics constraint typical for rural Java).

- The warung-side power-user community for Dagangan is more durable than Ula's or GudangAda's because the geographic carve-out means the agen network is the warung's primary digital sourcing channel, not one of several. The single-source dependence creates stickiness.
