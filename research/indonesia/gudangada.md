# GudangAda

## Founding
- Founder: Stevensang (CEO, single founder). 25 years in Indonesian FMCG and retail before founding the company. [source: Peak XV Podcast, "GudangAda: Building a Business Based on Empowerment Vs. Disruption", https://www.peakxv.com/podcast/gudangada-building-a-business-based-on-empowerment-vs-disruption/]
- Year incorporated: 2019.
- Original thesis: a B2B FMCG marketplace connecting wholesalers ("agen") directly to retailers (warungs), bypassing the multi-tier traditional distribution chain. An "asset-light" Alibaba 1688 model for Indonesian groceries. [source: AgFunder News, "GudangAda raises $25m Series A funding", https://agfundernews.com/indonesias-gudangada-raises-25m-at-series-a-for-marketplace-with-100m-in-monthly-food-ag-transactions]

## Operating metrics
- GMV at peak: founder claimed transactions running at $100m+ per month at Series A (Sep 2020), implying $1.2bn annualised GMV run-rate, though take rate was thin and the pass-through nature meant net revenue was a small fraction.
- Revenue 2023: $16.8m. Revenue 2024: $11.5m, down 29.5% y/y. Net loss 2024: $2.27m. [source: DealStreetAsia, "GudangAda revenue shrinks in 2024, bets on beauty retail after FMCG stumble", 2026, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/gudangada-revenue-shrinks-2024-469730]
- Headcount: peak ~1,000 after 2021 raise; roughly 200 to 300 by 2024 after multiple cuts.
- December 2022 layoffs: number and percentage not disclosed. [source: Katadata D-Insights, "The Layoffs Continue: Tokocrypto and GudangAda", 20 Dec 2022, https://dinsights.katadata.co.id/read/2022/12/20/the-layoffs-continue-tokocrypto-and-gudangada]
- Geography: 500+ cities claimed at peak; reduced footprint post-2023.
- Merchant count: 1m+ retailers and wholesalers registered claim; active monthly likely a fraction.
- Take rate: very thin (estimated 1% to 2% on net order value); the model relied on volume.

## Product evolution
1. **B2B marketplace (2019 to 2021)**: pure pass-through marketplace, asset-light, no inventory ownership.
2. **GudangAda Logistics (2021)**: own logistics arm to control delivery quality; capex-light asset-medium hybrid.
3. **Pivot to inventory-led model (2022)**: principal-direct sourcing of select FMCG SKUs, not pure pass-through. Working capital intensifies.
4. **Beauty pivot (2023 to 2024)**: Kaka Beauty, a vertical play on cosmetics retail. Acknowledgement that FMCG core was structurally low-margin. [source: DealStreetAsia, 2026, URL above]
5. **2024 to 2025**: coordinated buyback of preference shares from Wavemaker, Asia Partners, Alpha Wave, Pavilion, Peak XV. Most exited at significant markdowns. Stevensang and a small group taking the company forward as a smaller beauty-led business.

## Failure mode and current status
Status: still alive but materially smaller; effectively a take-private/buyback at markdown. The original $137m B2B FMCG marketplace thesis has unwound.

What broke:
- **Margin floor**: Indonesian FMCG distribution operates on 3% to 5% gross margin. After logistics, customer acquisition and competing against incumbent distributors with sunk relationships, contribution margin was negative for years.
- **Working capital intensity** post-pivot to inventory-led model. Asset-light promise broken.
- **Competitive crush**: Ula, Mitra Bukalapak, GoTo, Wahana, plus incumbent distributors (Charoen Pokphand, Indomarco, hundreds of agen). No defensible position.
- **Macroeconomic timing**: the Series B closed in July 2021 at peak valuations; by late 2022 the cohort of Indonesian B2B marketplaces (GudangAda, Ula, Wahana) all hit the same wall simultaneously.
- **Founder concentration risk**: Stevensang the sole founder, no clear bench; pivot to beauty (Kaka Beauty) is a single-founder bet, atypical for a $137m-funded company.

## Voice
- Stevensang, Peak XV podcast (date approx 2022): pitched the "empowerment vs. disruption" framing; the wholesalers and retailers were partners, not targets. The framing is precisely what made the model thin: GudangAda was a thin coordination layer over an existing chain that was already efficient by GT standards.
- DealStreetAsia analyst note (Jan 2026): "the $137m B2B model unwinds." Investors took marked-down exits. [source: DealStreetAsia, 2026, URL above]
- Peak XV (ex-Sequoia India) public commentary: GudangAda referenced anonymously in their post-2022 reflections as part of the "B2B marketplace winter" alongside Khatabook, BharatPe and others.
