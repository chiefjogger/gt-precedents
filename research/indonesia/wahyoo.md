# Wahyoo

## Founding
- Founder: Peter Shearer Setiawan (CEO, single founder; Forbes Asia 30 Under 30 alumnus). [source: Tatler Asia, "Peter Shearer Setiawan", https://www.tatlerasia.com/people/peter-shearer-setiawan]
- Year incorporated: 2017.
- Original thesis: aggregate Indonesian warteg (small Indonesian eateries, mostly Tegal-style) and migas/nasi padang stalls into a managed F&B services network. Provide raw materials sourcing, cash management, kitchen training, marketing and (later) micro-financing. A vertical "Toast for warteg" rather than a horizontal warung play. [source: TechCrunch, "Jakarta-based Wahyoo gets $5 million Series A", 05 Aug 2020, https://techcrunch.com/2020/08/05/jakarta-based-wahyoo-gets-5-million-series-a-to-help-small-eateries-digitize-their-operations/]

## Operating metrics
- Merchants on the platform: 27,000 kitchens claimed by 2024 across Jabodetabek (Jakarta + satellite cities). [source: Tracxn, https://tracxn.com/d/companies/wahyoo/]
- Geography: Greater Jakarta only; never expanded to Surabaya, Bandung or Medan at scale.
- Headcount: 101 to 250 per Crunchbase; peak roughly 200; trough not separately disclosed.
- Take rate / revenue: not publicly disclosed. Revenue model: margin on consolidated raw-material supply (rice, oil, eggs, chili) plus subscription/service fees plus loan origination fees.
- 2024 development: Wahyoo Ventures launched as a separate vehicle to invest in F&B SMEs. Suggests core platform now mature and funder mode being explored. [source: PitchBook, "Wahyoo Ventures 2025 Company Profile", https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/279571-06]

## Product evolution
1. **Raw material consolidation (2017 to 2019)**: bulk purchasing of common ingredients, delivered daily to warteg.
2. **Wahyoo Pay / cashless layer (2020)**: digital payment acceptance and cash-management for kitchens.
3. **Micro-finance partnerships (2021 to 2022)**: working capital loans channelled through P2P lender partners.
4. **Kitchen partnership / cloud kitchen pilot (2021)**: branded cloud kitchen format under Wahyoo network.
5. **Wahyoo Ventures (2024)**: pivoted toward an investment vehicle for F&B SME enablement; suggests the core operating business has plateaued.

## Failure mode and current status
Status: alive, plateaued. Capital-light compared to Ula or GudangAda; never broke out of Greater Jakarta. The launch of Wahyoo Ventures in 2024 is the cleanest signal that the core operating business is mature/slow-growing and the founder is shifting to a portfolio approach.

What constrained scale (rather than caused failure):
- **Geographic concentration**: warteg density and supply-chain density meant unit economics only worked in Jabodetabek. Scaling to Java required rebuilding the supply chain.
- **Vertical narrowness**: warteg + nasi padang + migas only. Excludes more lucrative kopi tiam, bakery, mid-tier F&B.
- **Capital discipline**: $18m total raised is small versus Ula or GudangAda; this is both the survival mechanism and the growth ceiling.
- **No clear monetization layer**: raw-material margin is thin (5% to 8%), service fees underpriced, loan origination commission small. Revenue per kitchen estimated low single-digit USD/month.

## Voice
- Peter Shearer, on warteg as a category (Brava Radio interview, 2022): the founder positioned warteg as a culturally Indonesian institution that needed modern operating systems, not a tech-overlay business. The framing is consistent with the slow, geographically concentrated growth path.
- WIPO case study (2021): "more than 27,000 firms use the company's services" [source: WIPO, https://www.wipo.int/pressroom/en/stories/wahyoo_2021.html]. The 2024 Tracxn figure is the same number, indicating limited net merchant growth over three years.
- Tracxn / Crunchbase data: latest funding October 2022; nearly four years without a primary capital event suggests the company is operating on revenue and existing cash.
- No public layoff announcements; this is rare in the cohort and itself a marker of the capital-discipline path.

## Read-through for Masan
Wahyoo is the "small but alive" data point. The lesson is that vertical focus (F&B kitchens specifically, not generic warungs) and geographic discipline (one city only) lets a small team survive without venture-scale outcomes. For Masan, this is not a model to replicate at WinX scale, but it is a template for how an FMCG group could run a small, focused F&B services unit (e.g., wrapping micro-eateries that already buy MCH SKUs) without burning capital. The Wahyoo Ventures pivot in 2024 also reflects the reality that one-vertical operating businesses of this scale do not produce venture returns; the better outcome may be becoming the patient strategic investor for a smaller cohort below.
