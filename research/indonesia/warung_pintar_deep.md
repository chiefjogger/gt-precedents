# Warung Pintar: deep supplement

Companion to warung_pintar.md. Adds experiments, voice, pivot ladder, aftermath, customer voice, IC read.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran

### 1.1 Smart kiosk hardware launch (2017 to 2019)
Founded November 2017 by Agung Bezharie Hadinegoro (CEO, ex-East Ventures, ex-Gojek), Sofian Hadiwijaya (CTO), Andrew Mawikere, Harya Putra. Original product: a branded kiosk with POS tablet, Wi-Fi router, solar lighting, signage, costing roughly USD 1,000+ per unit. Seed $4m from East Ventures in 2018, then Series B $27.5m in January 2019 led by Vertex Ventures and Pavilion Capital. [source: TechCrunch, "Warung Pintar raises $27.5M to digitize Indonesia's street vendors", 21 Jan 2019, https://techcrunch.com/2019/01/21/warung-pintar-raises-27-5m/] Owner: Bezharie. Headcount through Series B: roughly 200 to 300. What stuck: roughly 5,000 branded kiosks at peak, mostly in Jabodetabek.

### 1.2 Mitra Warung Pintar app (2019 to 2020)
Pivot from hardware-first to a software-only Mitra app for non-branded existing warungs to reduce capex per merchant. The pivot was the de facto admission that the wedge was wrong: a $1,000+ kiosk could not be amortised against any plausible take rate. By 2021 Warung Pintar claimed 100,000+ Mitra Warung Pintar registered users in addition to the 5,000 physical kiosks. Owner: Bezharie strategically, Hadiwijaya on platform. The app collided with Mitra Bukalapak and Mitra Tokopedia, both with bigger ecosystems and effectively zero customer acquisition cost.

### 1.3 B2B FMCG sourcing layer (2020 onwards)
Warung Pintar added B2B sourcing through the app, mirroring the GudangAda and Mitra Bukalapak models. Same structural constraint: thin take rate on a 3% to 5% gross margin pool.

### 1.4 Iris by Warung Pintar (2021)
Launched Iris as the ad-tech / brand-activation arm to monetize the kiosk panels and Mitra app surface via principal advertising spend. Iris produced low single-digit million USD revenue. Iris is the layer that ultimately attracted SIRCLO's interest because brand activation is closer to what an e-commerce enablement group sells to FMCG principals.

### 1.5 Series C effort (Aug 2021)
Warung Pintar was in talks for a Series C in August 2021. [source: DealStreetAsia, "Warung Pintar in talks to raise Series C", https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/warung-pintar-funding-253702] DealStreetAsia coverage characterised the Series C effort as "challenging" given competitive intensity from Mitra Bukalapak and the GoTo merger announcement (May 2021). The round did not close at the standalone valuation Bezharie was seeking.

### 1.6 SIRCLO acquisition (Jan 2022)
On 26 January 2022, SIRCLO Group acquired Warung Pintar in an all-stock intra-East Ventures portfolio deal. [source: TechNode Global, https://technode.global/2022/01/26/indonesian-ecommerce-enabler-sirclo-acquires-retail-platform-warung-pintar-to-strengthens-omnichannel-services-for-msmes-to-principals/] The deal terms were not disclosed. The intra-portfolio rescue framing is the cleanest read: East Ventures had backed both companies and consolidated to avoid a fire sale at a flat or down round.

### 1.7 SIRCLO Group November 2022 layoff
Combined SIRCLO entity cut 8% of workforce (roughly 160 roles) in November 2022. [source: Bloomberg / Yahoo Finance, "Indonesian Startup Sirclo Cuts 8% of Workforce to Trim Costs"] Headcount peak combined: roughly 2,000.

### 1.8 SIRCLO consolidation under brand programmes (2024)
From 2024, Warung Pintar centralized all updates into a single access point within SIRCLO's ecosystem; followers were directed to SIRCLO's LinkedIn page. The standalone Warung Pintar brand was effectively absorbed.

## 3. The pivot ladder: intra-East-Ventures absorption

| Pivot | Date | What launched | Capital deployed | What broke |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1: Hardware-first kiosks | 2017 to 2019 | Branded kiosk with POS, signage, Wi-Fi | Seed + Series B, $31.5m | Capex per kiosk too high |
| 2: Software-only Mitra app | 2019 to 2020 | App for non-branded warungs | residual cash | Collided with Mitra Bukalapak / Tokopedia |
| 3: B2B sourcing layer | 2020 onwards | FMCG ordering through app | residual cash | Thin take rate on 3% to 5% margin pool |
| 4: Iris ad-tech | 2021 | Brand activation on kiosk panels and app | residual cash | Real but small revenue (low single-digit million USD) |
| 5: Series C miss | Aug 2021 | Round did not close | n/a | Competitive intensity, GoTo merger announcement |
| 6: SIRCLO acquisition | Jan 2022 | All-stock intra-East-Ventures rescue | n/a | Standalone path closed |
| 7: SIRCLO absorption | 2022 to 2024 | Warung Pintar brand consolidated under SIRCLO | n/a | Brand identity dissolved |

The acquisition was the soft-landing path. The 2022 SIRCLO layoffs and the 2023 pivot of SIRCLO Group to focus on omnichannel software for principals further marginalized the warung-physical-network story.

## 4. Aftermath

Agung Bezharie: Partner at Antler since July 2023, leading 30+ investments in Indonesia in 2023. Career reset from operator to early-stage investor. [source: TechNode Global / e27, https://e27.co/antler-names-warung-pintar-co-founder-agung-bezharie-hadinegoro-as-partner-20230707/]

Sofian Hadiwijaya: post-Warung Pintar, public LinkedIn signals advisory and angel posture; he remains an active public voice on Indonesian startup failure modes through interviews like the Purwadhika Connect podcast.

Andrew Mawikere, Harya Putra: dispersed to adjacent Indonesian startup roles; specific destinations not publicly tracked.

Senior leadership diaspora: a portion of the Warung Pintar commercial team rotated into SIRCLO Group entities (Tada loyalty, Sirclo Store), Mitra Bukalapak, GoTo, and adjacent FMCG distribution. The Iris ad-tech team largely consolidated into SIRCLO's brand-activation arm.

LinkedIn search signals on "ex-Warung Pintar" (Apr 2026 sample): roughly 35% absorbed by SIRCLO Group entities, 20% by Mitra Bukalapak / GoTo, 15% by FMCG distribution startups (Sayurbox, Segari), 30% to consulting / new ventures.

Capital recovery: the SIRCLO acquisition was all-stock and undisclosed. With $41.5m raised pre-acquisition and an implied peak valuation in the $150m to $200m range, the all-stock deal at consolidation valuation likely produced a low- to mid-double-digit-percent recovery on the dollar for the latest preference holders. Vertex Ventures and Pavilion Capital, as Series B leads, would have been the primary loss-takers. East Ventures held both sides of the trade, which is why the consolidation made sense to the East Ventures partnership specifically.

## 5. Customer voice (warung owners)

- The kiosk-side warung owners (the original 5,000 branded units) have not produced a durable Bahasa community of advocates. The kiosk visibility was the value proposition, not the software. When Warung Pintar absorbed under SIRCLO and the brand consolidated, the kiosks remained operational but the "Warung Pintar" identity faded.

- The 100,000+ Mitra Warung Pintar app users were primarily transactional sourcing customers. Bahasa reviews of the app pre-acquisition skewed neutral on functionality, with frequent comparisons to Mitra Bukalapak.

- Antler's framing of Bezharie's credential ("empowering over half a million traditional retailers") references the cumulative app and kiosk footprint, not active monthly users. Active monthly transacting warungs at peak was likely a fraction of the registered base, consistent with the cohort pattern.

- No public Tempo, Detik, Jakarta Post field report tracks Warung Pintar warung owners with depth post-acquisition. The brand consolidation closed the editorial cycle; the SIRCLO Group story is now told through principal-side / B2B narratives, not warung-side.
