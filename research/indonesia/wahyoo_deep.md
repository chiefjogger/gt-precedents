# Wahyoo: deep supplement

Companion to wahyoo.md. Adds experiments, voice, pivot ladder, aftermath, customer voice, IC read.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran

### 1.1 Raw material consolidation launch (2017 to 2019)
Founded 2017 by Peter Shearer Setiawan, single founder, Forbes Indonesia 30 Under 30 alumnus. The original product: bulk-buy of common warteg ingredients (rice, oil, eggs, chili, vegetables) delivered daily to kitchens in Jabodetabek. Wahyoo bought from wholesalers and resold to warteg with a small margin (5% to 8%). Owner: Shearer. Headcount through seed close: roughly 40. What stuck: this was the durable core of the business through 2024.

### 1.2 Series A and Wahyoo Pay (Aug 2020)
Series A August 2020 at $5m, led by Intudo Ventures with Amatil X (Coca-Cola Amatil's CVC), Kinesys Group, Arkblu Capital, Indogen Capital, Selera Kapital, Gratyo Universal Indonesia, and Isenta Hioe. [source: TechCrunch, "Jakarta-based Wahyoo gets $5 million Series A", 05 Aug 2020, https://techcrunch.com/2020/08/05/jakarta-based-wahyoo-gets-5-million-series-a-to-help-small-eateries-digitize-their-operations/] Coca-Cola Amatil participation is the structural tell: an FMCG principal saw warteg as a beverage distribution channel. Wahyoo Pay launched as the cashless payment acceptance layer for kitchens. Owner: Shearer.

### 1.3 Micro-finance partnerships (2021 to 2022)
Working capital loans channelled through P2P lender partners. Wahyoo did not carry credit risk on balance sheet; it earned origination commission. The structure mirrors what Dagangan later built with BTPN Syariah Ventura. The micro-finance layer scaled modestly; revenue per kitchen from loan origination remained low single-digit USD per month.

### 1.4 Cloud kitchen / kitchen partnership pilot (2021)
Branded cloud kitchen format under the Wahyoo network. Specifics on outlet count and budget are not in primary sourcing; the pilot did not break out as a separate disclosed business line through 2022 to 2024.

### 1.5 Series B (Oct 2022)
Series B October 2022 at $6.5m, led by Global Brain Corporation with AC Ventures. [source: Tracxn / Techgenyz] Total raised reached roughly $18m disclosed (Tracxn) or $24.6m (PitchBook variant; the discrepancy reflects undisclosed extension tranches). Headcount peak approximately 200 around Series B close.

### 1.6 Geographic discipline (2017 to 2024)
Wahyoo never expanded outside Greater Jakarta at scale. Surabaya, Bandung, Medan were not opened. The 27,000 kitchens claimed by 2024 are concentrated in Jabodetabek. [source: Tracxn, https://tracxn.com/d/companies/wahyoo/] By WIPO's 2021 case study the figure was already 27,000 firms; the 2024 Tracxn figure is the same number, indicating limited net merchant growth over three years.

### 1.7 Wahyoo Ventures (2024)
Launched Wahyoo Ventures as a separate vehicle to invest in F&B SMEs. [source: PitchBook, "Wahyoo Ventures 2025 Company Profile", https://pitchbook.com/profiles/company/279571-06] The pivot to a portfolio investment vehicle is the cleanest signal that the core operating business has plateaued and the founder is shifting to a fund-of-fund posture below the operating layer.

### 1.8 No public layoff announcements
Wahyoo has no public layoff coverage in the cohort tracking (Ecommurz, Katadata D-Insights). This is rare in the cohort and itself a marker of the capital-discipline path. The company appears to have managed headcount through attrition rather than cuts.

## 4. Aftermath

Peter Shearer: still founder and CEO of Wahyoo. Now also operating Wahyoo Ventures. Career trajectory still operating; not exited.

Senior leadership: small team, no public diaspora signal at the senior leadership level. Mid-level operations likely rotate within the F&B services and cloud kitchen ecosystem.

Capital position: the absence of public layoffs and the $6.5m Series B in October 2022 (closed in the late-ZIRP window) suggest the company is operating on revenue plus residual cash. Roughly four years without a primary capital event by Apr 2026 indicates the business is self-sustaining at a small scale.

LinkedIn search signals on "ex-Wahyoo": thin. The company's small headcount (peak ~200) and Jabodetabek concentration mean ex-employees disperse into local F&B (Hangry, Mangkokku, ESB), Indonesian fintech (Xendit, Flip), and traditional FMCG distribution.

## 5. Customer voice (warteg owners)

- Wahyoo's warteg-side warmth in Bahasa coverage is meaningfully stronger than the rest of the cohort. The Merry Riana Show, Hidden Masters, MLDSPOT and Brava Radio coverage consistently frame Shearer's relationship with warteg owners as a partnership.

- The "naik kelas" framing (warteg leveling up) is a recurring theme in Bahasa coverage, with warteg owners on camera in YouTube interviews describing the income uplift from the Wahyoo platform. Specifics on income uplift are not standardised; Wahyoo has not published a formal Dagangan-style 40% to 60% number.

- The lack of a Reddit or Kaskus thread tracking Wahyoo specifically is consistent with the cohort. Warteg owners are not online community participants in the same way urban professionals are; the customer voice is captured through video and Bahasa print rather than text-based social media.

- The Coca-Cola Amatil X investment is the structural endorsement of warteg as an FMCG distribution channel. The Amatil-side voice is confined to the corporate venture announcement.
