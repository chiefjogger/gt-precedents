# BukuWarung

## Founding
- Founders: Chinmay Chauhan (former co-founder, now exited operating role) and Abhinay Peddisetty (CEO).
- Year incorporated: 2019; YC cohort S20 (Summer 2020), often referred to as "W20-era" because the seed round closed April 2020. [source: TechCrunch, "Meet BukuWarung, the bookkeeping app built for Indonesia's 60 million micromerchants", 02 Jul 2020, https://techcrunch.com/2020/07/02/meet-bukuwarung-the-bookkeeping-app-built-for-indonesias-60-million-micro-merchants/]
- Original thesis: a Khatabook-style mobile ledger for Indonesia's 60 million micro-merchants, on top of which BukuWarung would layer payments, then credit. [source: TechCrunch, 02 Jul 2020, URL above]

## Operating metrics
- Revenue 2022: $0.99m. Revenue 2023: $1.7m, up 72% y/y. [source: DealStreetAsia, "Indonesian fintech firm BukuWarung slashes losses by 75% in 2023", 2024, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/bukuwarung-earnings-2023-408041]
- Net loss 2022: $25.3m, up 33% from $19m in 2021. Employee benefits in 2022 jumped 176% to $13.8m. [source: DealStreetAsia, "Indonesian fintech BukuWarung's losses widen in 2022 despite revenue jump", 2023, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/bukuwarung-earnings-2022-363854]
- Net loss 2023: cut by 75% versus 2022, implying roughly $6m. [source: DealStreetAsia, 2024, URL above]
- Headcount: peak roughly 400+ in 2022 (Glassdoor, employee LinkedIn signal); cut to ~211 by 2024 per Crunchbase. Trough estimate ~200 to 250.
- Geography: Indonesia primary; small India tech hub (Bukuwarung India Private Limited, Bengaluru).
- Merchant count claimed: 9m+ registered MSMEs as of 2022 (founder claims), monthly active much smaller; not independently verified.
- Take rate: not publicly disclosed.

## Product evolution
1. Launch: free SMS/Android digital ledger (catatan utang). Khatabook clone.
2. 2021: BukuWarung Pay, QRIS payments, digital products (PPOB pulsa, electricity, water bills). Mirror of Indian Khatabook plus Paytm.
3. 2022 reset: payments scaled back due to fraud and thin take. Pivot toward MSME lending with regulated lending partners; insurance and savings stubs added.
4. 2023 to 2024: cost cut, focus on lending economics, attempts to embed accounting + working capital lending bundle. What stuck: ledger + PPOB; lending remained sub-scale.

## Failure mode and current status
Status: alive but plateaued. Not shut. Reported 75% loss reduction in 2023 indicates path to break-even but on a much smaller revenue base ($1.7m) than the $250m valuation implied.

What broke:
- Layoffs in November 2022: 100 employees, roughly 25% of staff. CEO Abhinay Peddisetty: "We have made the difficult decision after exploring every possible avenue to remain sustainable in the last six months." [source: StartupNews/DealStreetAsia, "BukuWarung lays off 100 employees", 25 Nov 2022, https://startupnews.fyi/2023/10/03/bukuwarungs-losses-swell-33-in-2022/]
- Fraud in payments forced retreat from the Paytm playbook. Lending requires KYC, collections infrastructure and capital that a SaaS-priced ledger app does not generate.
- ZIRP timing: Series A landed June 2021 at peak; product had 6 months to find rev model before macro turned.
- Glassdoor employee reviews from Nov 2022 onwards reference "abrupt layoffs", "no clear monetization", "leadership cycling priorities every quarter".

## Voice
