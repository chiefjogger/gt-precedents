# Mitra Bukalapak

## Status
Operating. Treated as a comp, not a failure case. Mitra Bukalapak is the warung-partner programme inside PT Bukalapak.com Tbk (IDX: BUKA), launched in 2017. After Bukalapak's January 2025 decision to phase out physical-goods marketplace, Mitra has effectively become the company's primary operating engine.

## Founding
- Programme launch: 2017 inside Bukalapak.
- Bukalapak founders (parent company): Achmad Zaky, Nugroho Herucahyono, Fajrin Rasyid (founded 2010).
- Original Mitra thesis: convert existing warungs into Bukalapak-affiliated points-of-presence for digital products (PPOB pulsa, electricity, BPJS, virtual goods) and B2B FMCG sourcing. Use the warung as a distribution channel, not as a customer.

## Capital
Mitra is not separately funded. Bukalapak parent IPO'd on IDX in August 2021 at IDR 850 (raised IDR 21.9 trillion / approx $1.5bn USD). Stock has since fallen more than 85% from IPO.

## Operating metrics
- Registered Mitra: 16.8m as of March 2023, up from 13.1m March 2022. [source: PR Newswire / Bukalapak press, 2023, https://en.prnasia.com/releases/apac/behind-mitra-bukalapak-s-success-in-harnessing-the-power-of-indonesia-s-micro-businesses-402408.shtml]
- Q1 2023: Mitra accounted for 46% of group Total Processing Value (TPV).
- FY2024-25: marketplace and Mitra revenues at parity following marketplace shutdown.
- Active warungs transacting monthly: ~1.8m (FY2025 disclosure framing).
- Income uplift for warung owners (company claim): up to 3x.
- Financials: Bukalapak parent net loss IDR 1.55 trillion (~$95m USD) in 2024, up 12% y/y from IDR 1.38 trillion in 2023. [source: DealStreetAsia, "Indonesian e-commerce firm Bukalapak's losses rise 12% in 2024 amid spiralling costs", https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/indonesian-e-commerce-firm-bukalapaks-losses-rise-12-in-2024-amid-spiralling-costs-435103]
- 2025 turnaround: Bukalapak swung to profit in 2025 with revenue up 46%. [source: DealStreetAsia, "Bukalapak swings to profit in 2025 as revenue jumps 46%", https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/bukalapak-earnings-2025-475812]
- Q4 2024: revenue up 7% q-o-q; marketplace revenue +21% q-o-q. [source: TNGlobal, "Bukalapak reports 7 percent q-o-q increase in revenue in Q4 of 2024", 19 Mar 2025, https://technode.global/2025/03/19/indonesian-e-commerce-firm-bukalapak-reports-7-percent-q-o-q-increase-in-revenue-in-q4/]

## Product evolution and current strategy
1. **2017 to 2019**: PPOB (digital goods) layer for warungs.
2. **2020 to 2022**: B2B FMCG sourcing through Mitra app; warung orders FMCG SKUs from Bukalapak's wholesaler-network and gets next-day delivery.
3. **2023**: Mitra take rate raised; promotional subsidies cut.
4. **January 2025**: parent shuts physical-goods marketplace entirely (less than 3% of revenue). Group becomes a Mitra + virtual goods operator. [source: KrAsia, "Indonesia's Bukalapak pivots to digital services, ends physical goods sales", https://kr-asia.com/indonesias-bukalapak-pivots-to-digital-services-ends-physical-goods-sales]
5. **2025**: profitable swing, revenue +46% per FY2025 disclosure. Mitra is the surviving and now profitable operating asset.

## What they got right
- **Native distribution**: Mitra was layered onto an existing 100m+ user marketplace. Customer acquisition cost on the Mitra side was effectively the warung walk-in.
- **Digital goods first, FMCG second**: PPOB has near-zero working capital and 1% to 3% take rate at scale. FMCG sourcing was a layer added once the warung relationship was established.
- **Take rate discipline**: company raised take rates and cut subsidies in 2023 to 2024 ahead of macro pressure; this is what produced the 2025 profitability swing.
- **Refusing to defend the marketplace**: the January 2025 shutdown of the physical-goods marketplace is the cleanest example of management focusing on what works (Mitra + virtual goods) and killing what does not (3P marketplace competing against Shopee, Tokopedia, TikTok Shop).
- **Public market discipline**: as IDX-listed, capital allocation is reported quarterly; this forced honest accounting that private players (Lummo, Ula) deferred.

## What is unresolved
- TikTok Shop and Shopee bypassing the Mitra channel and reaching warungs directly through livestream ordering is a structural threat.
- Take rate ceiling: with FMCG margin pool at 3% to 5%, Mitra's revenue per warung is structurally capped.
- 2024 net loss still rising y/y; 2025 profit swing driven partly by one-offs and partly by cost reduction.
