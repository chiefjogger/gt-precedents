# Dagangan

## Founding
- Founders: Ryan Putera Pratama Manafe (CEO, ex-McKinsey, ex-CEO SUN Energy), Wilson Yanaprasetya (CTO), Maha Willy Chandra (COO).
- Year incorporated: 2019 (some sources cite 2018; Pitchbook and Crunchbase both anchor to 2019).
- Original thesis: a hub-and-spoke rural e-commerce platform for FMCG and daily essentials in Tier-3 and Tier-4 Indonesian villages, where formal modern trade has zero coverage. Order via Android app or village agent; delivery from local micro-warehouses ("hubs") within 24 hours. [source: TNGlobal, "Indonesia social e-commerce platform Dagangan raises $11.5M Series A led by Monk's Hill Ventures", 21 Sep 2021, https://technode.global/2021/09/21/indonesia-social-e-commerce-platform-dagangan-raises-11-5m-series-a-led-by-monks-hill-ventures/]

## Operating metrics
- Villages reached: 8,000+ across Java (claim, 2024). [source: WARC, "The rural potential: How Dagangan navigates Indonesia's retail landscape beyond urban centres", https://www.warc.com/newsandopinion/opinion/the-rural-potential-how-dagangan-navigates-indonesias-retail-landscape-beyond-urban-centres/en-gb/6629]
- Communities served: 25,000+.
- Hubs: 40+ rural micro-warehouses (2022 figure). [source: MARKETECH APAC, https://marketech-apac.com/social-commerce-firm-dagangan-introduces-online-merchandise-shopping-services-for-rural-areas/]
- MSMEs supported: 30,000+ local sellers / village agents.
- Customers: 1m+ rural villagers served.
- Cost reduction claim: 12% lower delivered prices for end consumers; 40% to 60% lower logistics cost vs traditional channel (Dagangan internal claim, not independently verified).
- Income uplift to village agents: 40% to 60% per founder claims.
- Take rate: not publicly disclosed.
- Headcount: not publicly disclosed; Crunchbase signal points to 200 to 500.

## Product evolution
1. **Rural FMCG e-commerce app (2019 to 2021)**: Android app + WhatsApp ordering for villagers. Delivery via local hubs.
2. **Village agent network (2021)**: ibu-ibu / village-level sub-agents who aggregate orders from neighbours. Social commerce overlay.
3. **Pre-B financial services (2022 onwards)**: micro-financing for village agents and warungs in network, structured through BTPN Syariah Ventura partnership. Sharia-compliant.
4. **Series B 2024**: continued rural expansion; founders publicly target 10x growth in two years.

## Failure mode and current status
Status: live, growing, capital-disciplined. Not in failure. Within the cohort, Dagangan is one of two non-failures alongside Mitra Bukalapak / Mitra Tokopedia (which are platform programmes, not standalone companies).

Why it has avoided the cohort failure modes (interpretation):
- **Geographic carve-out**: Tier-3/4 villages where Mitra Bukalapak, Mitra Tokopedia, Ula and GudangAda did not compete. Each is concentrated in cities or peri-urban warungs. Dagangan owned the underserved rural lane.
- **Capital discipline**: ~$18m disclosed across rounds vs Ula's $140m. Lower burn ceiling.
- **Hub-and-spoke physical model**: capex matched to the geography, not subsidised growth. The 40+ hubs are small enough to break even per location.
- **Sharia-compliant micro-financing partnership**: regulated lender (BTPN Syariah) carries balance-sheet risk; Dagangan earns origination, not credit risk.
- **Founder mix**: COO Maha Willy Chandra runs operations; Ryan Manafe brings consulting / operating discipline; CTO Wilson Yanaprasetya runs platform. Three operating founders versus the single-founder pattern that hurt GudangAda and Wahyoo.

Risk factors not yet visible:
- 2024 Series B amount is undisclosed; if it was a flat or down round, the company may be quietly capital-constrained.
- Rural FMCG demand is structurally smaller per household than urban; ceiling on category expansion.
