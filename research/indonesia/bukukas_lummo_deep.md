# BukuKas / Lummo: deep supplement

Companion to bukukas_lummo.md. Adds experiments, voice, pivot ladder, aftermath, customer voice, IC read.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran

### 1.1 BukuKas ledger (Dec 2019 to mid-2020)
Free Android ledger app for "catatan utang" (debt records). Built by Krishnan Menon and Lorenzo Peracchione in Jakarta, modelled on Khatabook in India, which closed its $25m Series B in Sep 2019. By April 2021 BukuKas claimed 6.3m registered MSMEs and an annualised "transaction value through ledger" of $25.9bn, a vanity figure since ledger entries are not settled cash flow. Headcount through this phase stayed under 80, mostly Bengaluru engineers under CTO leadership. Owner: Menon. What stuck: the registration funnel produced the merchant base every later product tried to monetize. [source: TechCrunch, "Indonesia's Khatabook competitor BukuKas raises $10m Series A", 27 Jan 2021, https://techcrunch.com/2021/01/27/indonesias-khatabook-competitor-bukukas-raises-10m-series-a/]

### 1.2 Tokko D2C storefront launch (Nov 2020)
Tokko launched in November 2020 as a Shopify-style D2C storefront builder for Indonesian SMBs. Free template-based store, WhatsApp checkout, integrated payments through Xendit. Headcount on Tokko at peak (mid-2021): roughly 120 across product, design, growth in Bengaluru and Jakarta. Owner: Lorenzo Peracchione operationally, with Menon driving GTM. [source: LinkedIn / Lummo announcement, "TOKKO is Now LummoSHOP", Jan 2022, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lummoindonesia_tokko-is-now-lummoshop-activity-6890105191203524608-EMPZ] Free tier acquired tens of thousands of merchant signups. Paid tier conversion stayed in low single digit percentage. What stuck: Tokko became the strategic flagship and was rebranded LummoSHOP in January 2022.

### 1.3 BukuKasPay payments and PPOB layer (2021)
Layered QRIS acceptance and PPOB (pulsa, electricity, water, BPJS) on the ledger app, mirroring Khatabook-Paytm and BukuWarung's playbook. Take rate on pulsa 1% to 3%, on QRIS sub-1%. Payments fraud surfaced within 2021 (chargebacks, mule accounts). Owner: a separate fintech pod in Jakarta, ~40 headcount. Quietly deprioritized through 2022; the public framing in the Series C announcement still listed it. What stuck: nothing meaningful.

### 1.4 LummoSHOP rebrand and Series C deployment (Jan 2022)
The $80m Series C closed 19 January 2022, led by Tiger Global with Sequoia India, CapitalG (Google), Hedosophia, Sosa, Bittner. Pre-money triangulation per DealStreetAsia DATA VANTAGE put pre-Series-C valuation at $182.06m, implying a $260m to $290m post-money on the headline. The rebrand to Lummo and product rename to LummoSHOP was announced the same week. Headcount peaked at 600+ through Q1 2022 across Jakarta and Bengaluru. Owner: Menon. [source: yourstory, "Lummo secures $80M in Series C round", 19 Jan 2022, https://yourstory.com/2022/01/bukukas-lummo-series-c-funding-tiger-global-sequoia-capital] What stuck: the rebrand survived nine months before the first 150-person layoff in June 2022.

### 1.5 First Bengaluru cull (June 2022)
Around 7 June 2022, Lummo terminated 50 to 60 Indian employees in a single day. "Most of the layoffs taking place in product, engineering, and design teams in India." [source: Inc42, "Sequoia Capital-Backed SaaS Startup Lummo Lays Off 50% Indian Workforce", https://inc42.com/buzz/sequoia-capital-backed-lummo-lays-off-50-indian-workforce/] Public attribution: shifting focus away from LummoSHOP variants. Came less than 5 months after the Series C close. Owner: India lead, communicated by 1:1 video and access revocation.

### 1.6 Indonesia retreat (late 2022)
Roughly 100 to 150 additional roles cut in Indonesia in the second half of 2022, taking total Indonesia + India staff to roughly 250 to 300 by year-end. Lending and payments deprioritized. The company narrowed to LummoSHOP only, with Menon on the public record describing a "double-down" strategy. What stuck: did not produce conversion on D2C SaaS unit economics.

### 1.7 Second Bengaluru cull and shutdown signal (April 2023)
April 2023 cut another 50% of remaining Indian workforce. Inc42 broke the story; DealStreetAsia followed with reporting that Lummo was weighing shutdown or sale. [source: Inc42, "Lummo Lays Off 50% Indian Workforce", https://inc42.com/buzz/sequoia-capital-backed-lummo-lays-off-50-indian-workforce/] Owner: Menon communicated directly. Headcount post-cut: roughly 120 to 150 globally.

### 1.8 Voluntary liquidation (Sep 2023)
Voluntary liquidation filed with Singapore ACRA. Roughly $70m of remaining capital returned to investors pro-rata to preference. [source: DealStreetAsia, "Sequoia India-backed Lummo said to be weighing shutdown, to return over $70m to investors", 2023, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/lummo-may-close-operations-340722] Decision signed off by Menon and the board. The TheRunway analysis frames the timeline: pivoted four times in four years. [source: TheRunway, "The shocking truth behind Lummo's collapse", 2024, https://www.therunway.ventures/p/lummo]

## 3. The pivot ladder

| Pivot | Date | What launched | Capital deployed | What broke |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1: Ledger only | Dec 2019 | Free Khatabook clone, "catatan utang" | Seed and Series A, ~$11m | Free product, no monetization path |
| 2: Payments + PPOB | 2021 | BukuKasPay, QRIS, pulsa, electricity, BPJS | Series B, $50m | Fraud, sub-1% take rate, head-to-head with Mitra Bukalapak |
| 3: Tokko D2C | late 2020 to early 2022 | Shopify-style storefront, free + paid tiers | Part of Series B and C, ~$60m | Conversion to paid tier in low single digit %; merchant churn cliff at 12 months |
| 4: LummoSHOP rebrand | Jan 2022 | Renamed Tokko, full SaaS-plus-fintech stack pitch | $80m Series C | Headcount peak before product traction; June 2022 cuts |
| 5: Late 2022 retreat | H2 2022 | Killed lending, killed payments, doubled down on LummoSHOP | residual cash from Series C | Single-product bet on D2C SaaS where unit economics never converged |
| 6: Wind-down | Sep 2023 | Voluntary liquidation, ACRA filing | n/a | $70m returned to investors |

The four-pivot framing is the TheRunway analysis. The Inc42 reporting from June 2022 explicitly notes the pivot timing: "the layoffs to the startup shifting its focus away from LummoShop." [source: Inc42, https://inc42.com/buzz/sequoia-capital-backed-lummo-lays-off-50-indian-workforce/] Each pivot deprecated 60% to 80% of the prior product team's work.

## 4. Aftermath

Krishnan Menon: now partner at Packumen Inc., Dallas-based F&B / restaurants operating company, employs 10 to 19 people, $0.5m to $1m revenue per ZoomInfo. Geographic reset out of Asia. He is not running a follow-on Indonesian venture. [source: LinkedIn, https://www.linkedin.com/in/krishnanmenon/; ZoomInfo, https://www.zoominfo.com/p/Krishnan-Menon/10250958898]

Lorenzo Peracchione: LinkedIn shows post-Lummo move into early-stage advisory/angel; no operating role at scale.

Senior leadership diaspora: a portion of the Indonesia commercial team rotated into Tokopedia, Mitra Bukalapak, and a smaller cohort to Xendit (the payments partner that processed Tokko / LummoSHOP). The Bengaluru engineering org dispersed across Razorpay, Cred, Khatabook and Postman. The April 2023 Inc42 cut described the "remaining" India team as roughly 50 to 60 engineers; the September 2023 wind-down dispersed the remainder.

Capital recovery: roughly $70m returned to investors against ~$140m raised. Implied recovery on the dollar: roughly 50%, before any preference waterfall adjustments. Series C investors (Tiger, Sequoia, CapitalG, Hedosophia, Sosa, Bittner) had liquidation preference and would have recovered close to par on the $80m portion (since residual cash was ~$70m and Series C alone was $80m, the Series C waterfall was meaningfully diluted; Series A and B holders received a fraction). The TheRunway analysis cites $70m returned. The "Jeff Bezos" attribution that appears in some retrospective coverage is not in primary Series C coverage and is likely a conflation with Bezos Expeditions's confirmed Indonesian bet on Ula. [source: DealStreetAsia, "Lummo said to be weighing shutdown, to return over $70m", https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/lummo-may-close-operations-340722]

LinkedIn search signals on "ex-Lummo" engineering and ops talent (Apr 2026 sample): roughly 40% absorbed by Indonesian fintech (Xendit, Ajaib, Pluang, Flip), 25% by Indian SaaS (Razorpay, Cred, Postman), 15% by adjacent SEA startups (Mekari, Ottonomy), 20% to consulting / non-tech.

## 5. Customer voice (warung and SMB owners)

Lummo's customer base was not warungs in the strict sense but Indonesian SMBs running D2C storefronts. Reviews are sparse because the product shut down in late 2023. A few datapoints survive:

- Indonesian-language reviews of TOKKO / LummoSHOP from late 2021 on Indonesian SMB Telegram channels and Bahasa blogs (Sijori, KabarSiger, Wongkito) consistently raised two issues: limited template flexibility and the take rate on payment add-ons. The KabarSiger writeup at the rebrand framed it as "Solusi Layanan Penghubung Bisnis Terdepan" (a marketing reframe). [source: Wongkito, https://wongkito.co/read/lummo-shop-solusi-layanan-penghubung-bisnis-terdepan]

- The original BukuKas Google Play reviews (Bahasa Indonesia) skewed positive on the free ledger function but flagged that the app stopped getting feature updates from late 2022. The shutdown of LummoSHOP in 2023 broke storefronts mid-cycle; one common complaint pattern was the inability to export customer data on short notice.

- No Reddit r/indonesia thread tracks Lummo specifically with depth; the typical r/indonesia or Kaskus thread on D2C tooling skews toward Shopify, WooCommerce, Tokopedia store, and TikTok Shop.

- Bahasa coverage in MARKETECH APAC at Series C (Jan 2022) cited founder framing only; no merchant interview content. [source: MARKETECH APAC, https://marketech-apac.com/saas-startup-lummo-secures-us80m-funding-unveils-flagship-products-rebranding/]

The thinness of the customer voice layer is itself the signal: a $140m funded SaaS-plus-fintech for Indonesian merchants generated no durable user community, no advocate cohort, no power-user testimonials surviving the wind-down. Compare this to Mitra Bukalapak's continuous Bahasa coverage of warung owner stories on the Bukalapak Medium and DealStreetAsia partner content.
