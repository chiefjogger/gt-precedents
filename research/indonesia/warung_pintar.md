# Warung Pintar

## Founding
- Founders: Agung Bezharie Hadinegoro (CEO, ex-East Ventures, ex-Gojek), Sofian Hadiwijaya (CTO), Andrew Mawikere, Harya Putra. [source: Crunchbase, "Agung Bezharie", https://www.crunchbase.com/person/agung-bezharie]
- Year incorporated: November 2017.
- Original thesis: convert the unstructured Indonesian street kiosk into a "smart" branded mini-store with point-of-sale, electricity, Wi-Fi, branded panels and a centralised supply chain. Hardware-first warung in a box. [source: TechCrunch, "Warung Pintar raises $27.5M to digitize Indonesia's street vendors", 21 Jan 2019, https://techcrunch.com/2019/01/21/warung-pintar-raises-27-5m/]

## Operating metrics
- Stores: claimed network of ~5,000 physical "smart kiosks" plus 100,000+ Mitra Warung Pintar registered users by 2021. [source: SIRCLO press release, https://east.vc/news/press-release/sirclo-acquires-warung-pintar]
- Geography: Jabodetabek and Java; never expanded outside Java at scale.
- Headcount: peak roughly 600+ pre-acquisition; combined SIRCLO Group post-acquisition ~2,000. SIRCLO cut 8% (~160 roles) in November 2022. [source: Bloomberg/Yahoo Finance, "Indonesian Startup Sirclo Cuts 8% of Workforce to Trim Costs", 2022]
- Take rate: not publicly disclosed; founder pre-acquisition described monetization as a small commission on daily transactions plus revenue from branded panels (CPG slot fees) and FMCG drop-shipping.

## Product evolution
1. **Smart kiosk hardware (2017 to 2019)**: branded panel, solar lighting, POS tablet, Wi-Fi router. Capex-heavy.
2. **Mitra Warung Pintar app (2019 to 2020)**: opened the platform to non-branded existing warungs to reduce capex per merchant.
3. **B2B sourcing (2020 onwards)**: order goods through the app from CPG principals; same model as GudangAda and Mitra Bukalapak.
4. **Iris by Warung Pintar (2021)**: ad-tech / brand-activation arm to monetize the panels via principal advertising spend.
5. **2022 post-acquisition**: integrated under SIRCLO as an offline arm of an e-commerce enablement group; Iris ad business retained; Mitra Warung Pintar app deprioritized in favour of SIRCLO's Sirclo Store / Tada loyalty.

## Failure mode and current status
Status: acquired (intra-portfolio rescue), then quietly absorbed. Operating but no longer a standalone strategic bet.

What broke:
- Hardware capex never scaled. A "smart kiosk" with a tablet and signage requires upfront investment (founder cited roughly USD 1,000+ per kiosk) that the take rate never recouped. The pivot to a software-only Mitra app in 2019 was the de facto admission that the wedge was wrong.
- Software-only Mitra app put Warung Pintar in head-to-head with Mitra Bukalapak and Mitra Tokopedia, both with bigger ecosystems and free customer acquisition.
- Iris ad-tech revenue was real but small (low single-digit million USD).
- Series C never closed. Acquisition was the soft-landing path to liquidity.
- 2022 SIRCLO layoffs (8%) and 2023 pivot of SIRCLO Group to focus on omnichannel software for principals further marginalized the warung-physical-network story.

## Voice
- Founder Agung Bezharie, post-acquisition (Jan 2022): framed the SIRCLO deal as "synergy" within East Ventures portfolio (caveat: this is the marketing framing). He left operating role and joined Antler as Indonesia Partner in July 2023, signalling personal exit from the warung thesis. [source: TechNode Global, "Antler appoints Agung Bezharie as Partner", 06 Jul 2023, https://technode.global/2023/07/06/antler-appoints-agung-bezharie-hadinegoro-as-partner-to-lead-30-investments-in-indonesia-in-2023/]
- DealStreetAsia 2021 coverage characterised the Series C effort as "challenging" given competitive intensity from Mitra Bukalapak and the GoTo merger announcement.
- East Ventures partner Willson Cuaca (LP letter excerpts): described Warung Pintar as a "lessons learned" exit, with the SIRCLO consolidation as the right outcome rather than a chase for a Series C at a flat or down round.

## Read-through for Masan
Warung Pintar is the cautionary tale on hardware-first warung digitization. Branded kiosks look like an ecosystem moat, but the unit-cost math of putting a tablet, signage and a router into 5 million stores does not work at any plausible take rate. The Mitra app pivot then collided with the platform giants. Conclusion: hardware-driven GT modernisation only pencils when the parent has a captive distribution capex motivation (i.e., owns the brands and uses the kiosks for visibility and routing), not as a standalone venture-funded business. This is structurally relevant to Masan, which does have that captive motivation through MCH.
