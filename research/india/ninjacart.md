# Ninjacart

## Founding

Founded 2015 in Bengaluru by Thirukumaran Nagarajan (CEO), Kartheeswaran K K, Ashutosh Vikram, Sharath Loganathan, and Vasudevan Chinnathambi. Original thesis (May 2015): a 60-minute fresh-fruit-and-vegetable B2C delivery service. Pivoted within four months (September 2015) to B2B fresh produce supply chain, sourcing from farmers and supplying to kiranas, restaurants, and grocers.

## Product evolution

- May 2015: B2C 60-minute fresh produce delivery.
- Sep 2015: Pivot to B2B; sourced from farmers, sold to kirana / horeca.
- 2017-2020: Built proprietary supply-chain infra: collection centers, sorting hubs, predictive demand routing.
- 2020-2021: 200 layoffs in Bengaluru, Chennai, Mumbai, Hyderabad, attributed officially to "performance and integrity issues" but coincident with cost mode (Inc42).
- 2022-2024: Premium fruits and vegetables tier launched; Walmart-Flipkart strategic partnership opens grocery-supply role.
- 2024: B2B fulfilment-as-a-service for retailers and supermarkets becomes a meaningful contributor.

What stuck: the farmer-to-retailer fresh produce backbone. What was abandoned: the B2C consumer-facing thesis (within months of founding).

## Voice

Founder Thirukumaran (BusinessToday Magazine cover, Jan 2024): the company is now "looking to have a legacy of its own" rather than be acquired into a Walmart/Flipkart play.

Inc42 layoff coverage (2020): senior management sources said the company had been in "cost-saving mode" for months despite the official "performance and integrity" framing.

Deccan Founders 2025 framing: "Why Ninjacart Might Be India's Most Underrated Tech-Driven Unicorn?" notes the company has not gone public with the unicorn label despite operational scale [source: Deccan Founders, https://deccanfounders.com/2025/23/editor_picks/ninjacart-india-agritech-supply-chain/].

## Current status

Operating. FY24 revenue INR ~2,000 Cr (74% YoY growth), losses narrowing to INR 260 Cr, peak valuation USD 815 Mn at the 2022 round. Walmart and Flipkart are anchor strategic shareholders. No fresh up-round since Jan 2022; the FY24 financial trajectory points to continued capital need before profitability.

## Failure mode (graveyard read: pivoted-and-survived)

Ninjacart's "failure" is the original B2C thesis (which lasted 4 months). The B2B fresh produce pivot has worked in revenue terms but has yet to demonstrate net profitability. Fresh produce is a structurally low-margin, high-spoilage category; the path to durable profitability is conditional on either (a) layering value-added services on top (financing, branded farmer programs) or (b) further consolidation under a strategic owner (Walmart-Flipkart). The 2020 layoffs and the absence of an up-round since 2022 are the warning lights; the FY24 revenue growth and loss reduction are the offset.
