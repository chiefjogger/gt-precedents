# Ninjacart: deep supplement

This file extends `ninjacart.md`. Numbers in the base file are not re-litigated.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran

**1.1 The four-month B2C pivot (May to Sep 2015).** Ninjacart launched in May 2015 as a 60-minute fresh-fruit-and-vegetable B2C delivery service in Bengaluru. Within four months the founding team (Thirukumaran Nagarajan, Kartheeswaran Palanisamy, Ashutosh Vikram, Sharath Loganathan, Vasudevan Chinnathambi) abandoned the consumer model and pivoted to B2B supply from farmers to kiranas, restaurants and grocers [source: BusinessToday, "Ninjacart: The start up which made agritech look cool", 8 Jan 2024, https://www.businesstoday.in/magazine/deep-dive/story/ninjacart-the-start-up-which-made-agritech-look-cool-is-now-looking-to-have-a-legacy-of-its-own-412227-2024-01-08]. Per the YourStory podcast retrospective, the team ran "13 different experiments" in the first 500 days of the B2B pivot to figure out the unit economics of fresh produce sourcing, sorting and delivery [source: YourStory, "Podcast: Thirukumaran Nagarajan on the first 500 days", https://yourstory.com/2020/09/podcast-accel-ninjakart-thirukumaran-nagarajan]. Read: the only company in the cohort that pivoted away from consumer and survived.

**1.2 Walmart-Flipkart strategic anchor (Dec 2019 to Jan 2022).** Walmart and Flipkart led a USD 145 Mn Series D in Dec 2019, followed-on in Oct 2020 (Series E), and led the Series F extension in Jan 2022 that pushed Ninjacart to USD 815 Mn post-money [source: Entrackr, "Ninjacart's valuation crosses $815 Mn", Jan 2022, https://entrackr.com/2022/01/ninjacart-valuation-crosses-815-mn-following-walmart-and-flipkart-led-round/]. Strategic logic: Walmart's grocery procurement under Sameer Aggarwal (Walmart India CEO at the time) needed an India fresh-produce backbone. Closest sourced estimate of Walmart Foundation involvement: the Walmart Foundation's separate India CSR work on smallholder farmers ran in parallel but the Ninjacart equity is from Walmart corporate / Flipkart Group, not the Foundation. Thin-research flag on Vivek Astaire specifically; this name does not surface in surfaced public reporting on Walmart India or Walmart Foundation India leadership in the 2019 to 2022 window.

**1.3 The 2020 to 2021 layoffs (200 cut).** Inc42 reported Ninjacart let go ~200 employees across Bengaluru, Chennai, Mumbai and Hyderabad in 2020. The company framed it as "performance and integrity issues" but senior sources told Inc42 the company had been in "cost-saving mode for months" [source: Inc42, "Exclusive: Ninjacart Lays Off 200 Employees", https://inc42.com/buzz/ninjacart-employee-layoffs/]. This is the same pattern as Udaan's 2022 cuts, with the same dual-narrative (performance versus cost) that the trade press has called out across the cohort.

**1.4 Premium fresh tier and sub-product family launches (2022 to 2024).** Ninjacart layered Ninja Mandi (for traders), Ninja Global (export-import), Ninja Kirana (retailers), Ninja Kisaan (farmers), and a premium fruits and vegetables tier targeted at upper-mid kirana, modern trade, and HoReCa segments [source: businessmodelcanvastemplate.com / Ninjacart product page; StartupTalky, "Ninjacart Success Story", https://startuptalky.com/ninjacart-success-story/]. The product proliferation was the response to the same pressure Udaan faced: the core fresh produce P&L is structurally low margin (1 to 3% net) and only differentiated tiers can lift unit economics.

**1.5 The 2023 business divestments.** The base file frames the question of "what specifically Ninjacart sold off in 2023". Surfaced public reporting confirms FY23 gross revenue crossed INR 1,212 Cr and FY24 hit INR 2,082 Cr with losses narrowing 20% to INR 260 Cr [source: YourStory, "Agritech startup Ninjacart's revenue surges 74% to Rs 2,002 Cr", Oct 2024, https://yourstory.com/2024/10/agritech-startup-ninjacart-revenue-loss-fy24-fulfillment-business; Entrackr, "Ninjacart's gross revenue crosses Rs 1,600 Cr in FY23", https://entrackr.com/2023/10/ninjacarts-gross-revenue-crosses-rs-1600-cr-in-fy23/]. Specific divestment of Unnati, Citymall, or Frischkart is not surfaced as a Ninjacart action in public reporting; these are separate companies, not Ninjacart subsidiaries. Thin-research flag on the framing of "Ninjacart sold off in 2023". The narrower truth surfaced in reporting is sub-product simplification (consolidation toward Ninja Kirana and Ninja Mandi) rather than entity sales.

**1.6 Fulfilment-as-a-service for retailers and supermarkets (2024).** By FY24 Ninjacart's B2B fulfilment-as-a-service for organized retailers and modern-trade supermarkets had become a meaningful contributor to the 74% YoY revenue jump. The company began offering its sourcing, cold chain and last-mile capabilities as a paid-for stack to third parties, in effect offering Walmart / Flipkart's grocery procurement stack to other modern retailers [source: YourStory FY24 update].

## 2. Voice layer

**Thirukumaran Nagarajan, CEO and co-founder, on farmer revenue uplift:** "We buy directly from the farmers and they make 15-20 percent more revenue as the inventory risk belongs to Ninjacart once we source the produce from them." [source: YourStory / SeedToScale, https://www.seedtoscale.com/people/thirukumaran-nagarajan]

**Thirukumaran Nagarajan, on the food standards thesis:** "We wanted to increase the standard of the food that an Indian consumes. Right now if you ask an average Indian, s/he does not know the origin of the food." [source: YourStory]

**Thirukumaran Nagarajan, on Ninjacart's posture (BusinessToday cover, Jan 2024):** the company is "looking to have a legacy of its own" rather than be acquired into a Walmart or Flipkart play [source: BusinessToday Magazine, 8 Jan 2024].

**Mr. Naveen, farmer in Kondlahalli village, Karnataka (working with Ninjacart since 2019):** "Since working with Ninjacart, selling the harvested produce has been a very comfortable experience." Earlier, transporting a ton of brinjal to Hosur and Bathalapalli markets cost up to INR 5,000 per trip; now he saves INR 70,000 to INR 80,000 per season on transportation [source: Ninjacart Blogs, "How Ninjacart made Trade Experience Better for Farmers", https://ninjacart.com/blog/how-ninjacart-made-trade-experience-better-for-farmers/].

**Inc42 layoff coverage source (2020):** senior management sources said the company had been in "cost-saving mode" for months despite the official "performance and integrity" framing.

**Glassdoor product manager review (21 Aug 2024):** Ninjacart is "On a downhill trend, silent layoffs happening." [source: Glassdoor, https://www.glassdoor.com/Reviews/ninjacart-Reviews-E1322421.htm]

**Glassdoor headline data (2024 to 2025):** 3.5 of 5 stars, 1,321 reviews, 67% would recommend; work-life balance scored 3.1 of 5 [source: Glassdoor].

## 4. Aftermath (Ninjacart is operating; tracking trajectory of founders and key staff)

**Thirukumaran Nagarajan (CEO, co-founder).** Continues as CEO. Public framing leans toward independence rather than Walmart-Flipkart absorption [source: BusinessToday Magazine cover, Jan 2024].

**Other co-founders (Kartheeswaran Palanisamy, Ashutosh Vikram, Sharath Loganathan, Vasudevan Chinnathambi).** Surfaced reporting does not provide updated roles. Thin-research flag.

**Walmart and Flipkart strategic relationship.** The Walmart Foundation India work on smallholder farmers operates separately from the equity stake in Ninjacart. Vivek Astaire as a named Walmart Foundation executive is not surfaced in public reporting on the Ninjacart equity track. The corporate Walmart contact through 2019 to 2022 was Sameer Aggarwal (Walmart India CEO at the Best Price acquisition) and Judith McKenna (Walmart International CEO). Thin-research flag on the specific Foundation-side relationship name.

**The original kirana app team.** Ninjacart's "Ninja Kirana" app team is part of the active business; not dispersed. Glassdoor 2024 reviews mention "silent layoffs" but the LinkedIn-traceable trail of departures is moderate, with engineering staff landing at competitor agritech (DeHaat, Otipy, WayCool) and Bengaluru's broader B2B SaaS market. Comprehensive trail not assembled. Thin-research flag.

**The Walmart-Flipkart capital position.** As of the Series F extension Jan 2022, Walmart and Flipkart are anchor strategic shareholders. No fresh up-round since Jan 2022; the absence is itself the data point. The FY24 revenue trajectory points to continued capital need before profitability; closest sourced estimate is that an FY26 round at flat-to-down valuation is the most plausible outcome unless margins flip materially.

## 5. Customer voice (kirana owners and farmers specifically)

The Ninjacart customer voice has two distinct sides because the company sits between farmers and kiranas/HoReCa.

**Farmer side.** Mr. Naveen (Karnataka) is the cleanest documented case: ~INR 70,000 to INR 80,000 transportation savings per season, plus 15 to 20% revenue uplift from removing middlemen. Per company-published impact materials, the average farmer working with Ninjacart sees a 20% revenue boost after eliminating intermediaries [source: Ninjacart Impact Report 2023, https://www.ninjacart.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/NinjacartImpactReport2023.pdf; FocusAgriTech, "Ninjacart: Transforming the agri-trade ecosystem", https://focusagritech.com/agri-startups/ninjacart/].

**Kirana / HoReCa side.** The kirana voice on Ninjacart is narrower than on Udaan or Jumbotail because Ninjacart is fresh-produce only, not the full FMCG basket. The benefit cited in trade press is reliability of supply through the cold chain and consistent grading; the friction is that Ninjacart's pricing is not always cheaper than the wet-market arbitrage that a Bengaluru or Hyderabad kirana can capture in pre-dawn mandi visits. Independent kirana NPS data on Ninjacart is not publicly disclosed. Thin-research flag.
