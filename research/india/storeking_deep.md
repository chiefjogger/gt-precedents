# StoreKing: deep supplement

This file extends `storeking.md`. Numbers in the base file are not re-litigated.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran

**1.1 The 2012 to 2015 tablet kiosk in rural kirana model.** Sridhar Gundaiah and Govardhan Krishnappa launched the assisted-commerce kiosk in 2012 at LocalCube Commerce Pvt. Ltd. The mechanic: rural kirana acts as e-commerce kiosk where the storekeeper helps a walk-in customer order from a 50,000+ SKU catalog displayed on a tablet, with delivery routed to the store. The original thesis assumed rural smartphone penetration would remain low and that an "assisted" intermediation step in the village kirana would persist as a structural opportunity [source: YourStory, "StoreKing, a Rs 100 crore e-commerce company that million South Indian villagers buy from", May 2015, https://yourstory.com/2015/05/storeking-e-commerce-company].

**1.2 The Axiata Series B (Jun 2016, USD 16 Mn).** Malaysia telco Axiata Digital led the only meaningful late-stage round StoreKing closed. Post-money implied USD 60 to 90 Mn band. No subsequent up-round through 2025 [source: YourStory, "Rural e-commerce company StoreKing expands its reach", Jun 2016, https://yourstory.com/2016/06/storeking-funding/amp]. Total raised: USD 43.7 Mn over 16 rounds [source: Tracxn, https://tracxn.com/d/companies/storeking/].

**1.3 The 2016 to 2018 inventory-to-marketplace pivot.** Founder Sridhar Gundaiah's own framing: "It took me a while to discern the limitations to growth as a stockist." The shift from holding inventory to running a digital marketplace recovered working capital but reduced the gross margin per order, requiring volume to compensate. Volume did not arrive at the original projection.

**1.4 Rural FMCG distribution layer (2019 to 2021).** StoreKing positioned itself as the rural distribution arm for FMCG majors: HUL, Wipro, Dabur, Godrej, Marico, Reckitt, ITC, Himalaya. The B2B distribution P&L became the operating beach-head once the consumer-assisted-commerce thesis weakened [source: StartupTalky, "How StoreKing is connecting Rural Kirana Stores to Suppliers", https://startuptalky.com/storeking-success-story/].

**1.5 The April 2021 modern retail store plan.** StoreKing announced plans for modern retail stores in rural India [source: BusinessToday, Apr 2021, https://www.businesstoday.in/current/corporate/rural-distribution-company-storeking-to-launch-modern-retail-stores-in-rural-india/story/437897.html]. Execution did not scale to the announcement; the rural retail store rollout did not become the headline-changing milestone the press cycle implied.

**1.6 SK ROS (StoreKing Retail Operating System), 2023 to 2024.** Repositioned as a SaaS layer that turns a kirana shelf into "infinite aisle" in 5 minutes. Layered insurance distribution as a secondary revenue stream [source: Inc42, "Exclusive: StoreKing Plans Insurance Foray To Increase Penetration In Rural India", https://inc42.com/buzz/exclusive-storeking-plans-insurance-foray-to-increase-penetration-in-rural-india/]. SK ROS is the company's third repositioning in 12 years.

**Trade press read (YourStory and Entrepreneur cover narrative, multiple years):** rural was "underserved" but the operational cost of serving it (last-mile, low order density, low ticket sizes) consistently squeezed unit economics; StoreKing survived by repositioning multiple times rather than reaching scale.

## 4. Aftermath (StoreKing is operating; tracking founder and trajectory rather than dispersal)

**Sridhar Gundaiah (founder, CEO).** Continues as CEO. Open Magazine and BusinessToday profiles position him as a contrarian who chose Bharat (rural India) over urban e-commerce.

**Govardhan Krishnappa (co-founder).** Public role and current status not detailed in surfaced reporting. Thin-research flag.

**Axiata Digital.** Strategic anchor since Series B Jun 2016. Subsequent rounds not publicly led by Axiata; relationship status post-2020 not detailed in surfaced reporting. Thin-research flag.

**Engineering and ops staff dispersal.** StoreKing has not had a public layoff round comparable to Udaan, Dunzo or Ninjacart. Glassdoor data is thin (sub-100 reviews); the company sits in the "stalled rather than dead" quadrant. Thin-research flag on the engineering trail.

## 5. Customer voice (kirana owners specifically)

The rural kirana voice on StoreKing is less covered than urban platforms because the customer base sits in tier-3, tier-4 and village India where vernacular reporting dominates and English-language search trail is sparse.

Composite themes from surfaced StartupTalky, YourStory and Open Magazine reporting:

(a) Trust and assistance value (2012 to 2018). Rural kirana customers used the StoreKing kiosk to access SKUs they would otherwise not see; the kirana owner earned a small commission per order. Closest sourced estimate of typical kirana commission: 2 to 5% of the consumer ticket, but no precise number disclosed.

(b) Smartphone penetration substitution (2018 onward). As Reliance Jio's data tariff dropped and smartphone penetration in rural India surged, the assisted-commerce premise eroded. Customers who would have used a StoreKing kiosk now ordered directly on Amazon, Flipkart or Meesho. The kirana's role shifted from primary order-taker to occasional fallback.

(c) FMCG distribution reliability (2019 onward). Rural kiranas working with StoreKing as a B2B distribution partner reported reasonably reliable supply for the eight FMCG majors (HUL, Wipro, Dabur, Godrej, Marico, Reckitt, ITC, Himalaya). The proposition is conventional rural distribution, not a digital differentiation story.

(d) SK ROS adoption (2023 to 2024). Adoption data on the SaaS layer is not publicly disclosed. Thin-research flag.
