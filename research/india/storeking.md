# StoreKing

## Founding

Founded 2012 in Bengaluru (legal entity LocalCube Commerce Pvt. Ltd.) by Sridhar Gundaiah (CEO) and Govardhan Krishnappa. Original thesis: rural kirana stores in tier-3, tier-4 and village India lacked access to e-commerce SKUs; turn the kirana into an "assisted commerce" point-of-sale where the storekeeper helps a walk-in customer order from a 50,000+ SKU catalog, with delivery routed to the store.

[source: YourStory, "StoreKing ; a Rs 100 crore e-commerce company that million South Indian villagers buy from", May 2015, https://yourstory.com/2015/05/storeking-e-commerce-company]

## Product evolution

- 2012-2015: Tablet-based assisted-commerce model; village kirana acts as e-commerce kiosk.
- 2016-2018: Pivoted from inventory-holding stockist model to a digital marketplace (Sridhar's own framing: "took me a while to discern the limitations to growth as a stockist").
- 2019-2021: Rural distribution layer for FMCG majors (HUL, ITC, Dabur etc.).
- 2021: Plans for modern retail stores in rural India (BusinessToday, Apr 2021).
- 2023-2024: Launched "SK ROS" (StoreKing Retail Operating System) repositioning as a SaaS layer that turns a kirana shelf into "infinite aisle" in 5 minutes.
- 2024: Insurance distribution foray to deepen rural vertical (Inc42).

What stuck: rural FMCG distribution as a B2B beach-head; what did not scale to expectations: the consumer-facing assisted-commerce thesis (rural smartphone penetration overtook the original kiosk premise).

[source: Inc42, "Exclusive: StoreKing Plans Insurance Foray To Increase Penetration In Rural India", https://inc42.com/buzz/exclusive-storeking-plans-insurance-foray-to-increase-penetration-in-rural-india/]

## Voice

Founder Sridhar Gundaiah, profile in Open Magazine: "Call of the Countryside" framing positions him as a contrarian who chose to chase Bharat (rural India) rather than urban e-commerce [source: Open Magazine, https://openthemagazine.com/features/business/sridhar-gundaiah-call-of-the-countryside].

The narrative contrast in YourStory and Entrepreneur coverage: rural was "underserved" but the operational cost of serving it (last-mile, low order density, low ticket sizes) consistently squeezed unit economics; StoreKing survived by repositioning multiple times rather than reaching scale.

## Current status

Operating, modest scale relative to peers. INR 361 Cr revenue FY25 (Tracxn). Privately held; Series B vintage; no fresh marquee round disclosed since 2016 Axiata round. Effectively an FMCG rural distribution business plus a SaaS layer; the original e-commerce-into-villages thesis has effectively been retired.

## Failure mode (graveyard read: stalled rather than dead)

StoreKing is the case where a 12-year journey produced a ~INR 360 Cr revenue business with modest growth, no recent marquee round, and a thesis (assisted rural commerce) that smartphone penetration largely solved on its own. It did not blow up; it did not break out. Capital efficiency is materially better than Udaan or ShopX (USD 43.7 Mn raised against an operating business), but the venture-scale outcome that the early rounds priced in did not materialize.
