# Dunzo / Dunzo Daily

## Founding

Founded 2014 in Bengaluru by Kabeer Biswas (CEO), Ankur Aggarwal, Dalvir Suri, and Mukund Jha. Started as a WhatsApp-group-based personal-task-running service; layered hyperlocal delivery and merchant logistics. Original consumer-task thesis: deliver anything from anywhere within a city in under an hour.

## Product evolution

- 2014-2018: WhatsApp-task delivery in Bengaluru and select metros.
- 2019-2020: Merchant-first B2B push (Dunzo Merchant Services, DMS) onboarding kiranas and small businesses for last-mile.
- Aug 2021: Launched Dunzo Daily, the 19-minute quick-commerce vertical for groceries and essentials, in direct competition with Blinkit, Zepto, and Swiggy Instamart.
- 2022: Series F closed with Reliance Retail anchoring; positioning as last-mile arm of JioMart.
- 2023: Founders began departing (Dalvir Suri, Mukund Jha, Ankur Aggarwal). Lightrock, Reliance, and Google nominees vacated board seats August 2023.
- 2024: Salaries unpaid for many months; multiple layoff rounds; CEO Biswas attempting to stitch a rescue round.
- Aug 2024: Biswas told staff via email that the fundraising "had hit a roadblock".
- Jan 2025: Biswas joined Flipkart's Minutes (quick-commerce vertical); Dunzo app and website went offline.
- FY25: Reliance Retail formally wrote off the entire INR 1,645 Cr (USD 200 Mn) investment.

What stuck: the merchant logistics arm DMS at peak. What broke everything: the Dunzo Daily quick-commerce burn (heavy advertising and dark-store CapEx with no path to per-order profitability).

## Failure mode

Reliance write-off ranks among India's largest tech write-offs alongside Prosus' USD 500 Mn on Byju's and InfoEdge's INR 276 Cr on 4B Networks (Outlook Business). Direct cause: Aug 2024 fundraise hit a wall, payroll defaulted, creditors filed at NCLT, founders exited. Underlying cause: Dunzo Daily entered a category (10-15-minute delivery) where Blinkit, Zepto, and Instamart already had product-market fit and warchests; a Series F valuation of USD 775 Mn became uninvestable when the unit economics on a 19-minute drop did not improve fast enough to absorb the customer-acquisition cost.

## Voice

Kabeer Biswas, employee email (12 Aug 2024): the ongoing fundraising process had "hit a roadblock", with management "unable to close the transaction" [source: Inc42, "How Dunzo Lost Its Edge"].

Storyboard18 (Jan 2025): "Dunzo app and website shut down after cofounder Kabeer Biswas joins Flipkart" [source: Storyboard18, https://www.storyboard18.com/brand-marketing/dunzo-app-and-website-shut-down-after-cofounder-kabeer-biswas-joins-flipkart-53316.htm].

Inc42 retrospective frame: Dunzo's downfall was a sequence of capital-mistimed bets (DMS scale-up, then Daily, then Reliance partnership) where each pivot required more capital than the last and the quick-commerce category never afforded a profitable per-order economics window before the funding climate turned.

## Current status

Wound down. App and website offline since Jan 2025. CEO Biswas at Flipkart Minutes. NCLT proceedings underway. Reliance investment written off in FY25 reporting.

## Failure mode (graveyard read)

Dunzo is the most expensive single failure in the kirana-adjacent cohort excluding the wholesaler graveyard, because Reliance's USD 200 Mn was strategic (intended to wire JioMart kirana orders to Dunzo last-mile) and the strategic partner could not save it. Lessons: (1) a hyperlocal delivery layer cannot subsidize a 10-minute quick-commerce vertical out of consumer pricing; (2) strategic capital does not protect against per-order unit economics; (3) the kirana-as-merchant value (DMS) was real but too thin to fund the rest of the business.
