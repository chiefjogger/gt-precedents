# Amazon Easy Stores / Amazon Distribution (B2B India)

## Founding / launch

Amazon launched its Amazon Easy Stores program (consumer-facing kirana franchise that helps walk-in customers order on Amazon.in) and a separate B2B wholesale arm (initially "Amazon Wholesale", later rebranded as Amazon Distribution / Amazon Business) in India over 2018-2020. Easy Stores was the consumer-touchpoint kirana franchise. Amazon Distribution / Wholesale was the B2B kirana-supply arm that warehoused FMCG and re-supplied small retailers in select Karnataka cities (Bengaluru, Mysuru, Hubballi).

[source: Amazon India newsroom, "Amazon Easy Stores bridge digital divide", https://www.aboutamazon.in/news/small-business/amazon-easy-stores-bridge-digital-divide-for-indias-shoppers-in-smaller-towns-cities]
[source: TechCrunch, "Amazon partners with thousands of mom-and-pop stores in India", 18 Jan 2020, https://techcrunch.com/2020/01/18/amazon-india-kirana-neighborhood-stores/]

## Capital and ownership

Internal Amazon spend rather than venture rounds. Amazon committed an estimated USD 227 Mn to the Indian B2B / kirana supply arm before shutdown (Finshots / industry reporting). Amazon India overall has cumulatively committed USD 6.5-7 Bn+ to the country since 2014.

[source: Finshots, "Amazon's experiment with Kirana stores has failed!", https://finshots.in/archive/amazons-foray-into-b2b-ecommerce/]

## Operating metrics

Operating-level disclosure is limited because the program was inside Amazon and therefore not separately reported.
- Easy Stores: hundreds to low thousands of kirana franchise touchpoints across smaller cities; specific scale not disclosed.
- B2B Wholesale: warehouses in Bengaluru, Mysuru, and Hubballi only; never a national network.
- Customer base for B2B: not disclosed. Target was kiranas and small retailers in the three Karnataka cities.
- Take rate / merchant churn: not disclosed.

## Product evolution

- 2018-2019: Amazon launches the Easy Stores consumer-touchpoint kirana franchise.
- 2019-2020: Amazon Wholesale opens warehouses in Karnataka, supplies FMCG to small retailers.
- Late 2022: Amazon shuts the B2B kirana wholesale arm. Closure attributed to (a) negative unit economics, (b) regulatory pushback from Confederation of All India Traders (CAIT) and Union Ministers around foreign-funded e-commerce displacing local kiranas, (c) competitive squeeze from Reliance JioMart and Walmart-Flipkart.
- 2023-2025: Amazon shutters several other India verticals (Amazon Food, Amazon Distribution, Amazon Academy) as a broader rationalization.
- Easy Stores (consumer-facing kirana program) continues at reduced scale but is no longer marketed as a strategic initiative.

[source: Inc42 / FreepressJournal, "As Amazon shuts 3rd vertical in India, here are setbacks", https://www.freepressjournal.in/business/as-amazon-shuts-3rd-vertical-in-india-here-are-setbacks-that-have-hit-the-e-commerce-giant]

## Voice

Vivek Tyagi (LinkedIn industry post, Dec 2022): "Amazon's experiment with Kirana stores has failed!" [source: LinkedIn, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tyagivivek_amazons-experiment-with-kirana-stores-has-activity-7003987680480964608-gONo].

Outlook Business framing: regulator and political environment turned hostile to Amazon's kirana B2B in 2022, with the Indian government "picking a side" toward small retailers; the resulting policy uncertainty made the cost-to-scale unattractive [source: Outlook Business, "Amazon's India Troubles", https://www.outlookbusiness.com/corporate/amazons-india-troubles-in-giant-e-commerce-vs-small-kirana-has-the-government-picked-a-side].

PolicyCircle frame: kirana stores have proved unexpectedly defensible against organized e-commerce in India because of (a) credit-on-the-counter relationships, (b) sub-1km proximity, and (c) ability to deliver to door at no marginal labor cost.

## Current status

Amazon Distribution / Amazon Wholesale (the B2B kirana supply arm) was shut down in late 2022. Amazon Easy Stores continues to operate as a low-key consumer-facing kirana program but has been deprioritized; the strategic narrative around kirana digitization has been wound down. Amazon Business (B2B for SMEs and offices, distinct from kirana supply) continues separately.

## Failure mode (graveyard read)

The most well-capitalized strategic player walked away from the B2B kirana opportunity within ~3 years of meaningful investment. Three combined drivers per public reporting: (1) FDI / e-commerce regulatory pressure and political optics around foreign capital displacing kiranas; (2) negative unit economics on the Bengaluru/Mysuru/Hubballi pilot, with no clear bridge to scale profitability; (3) Reliance Jio Kirana program (WhatsApp-anchored, distribution-funded by Reliance Retail) competing without needing to clear an internal Amazon investment hurdle. The Amazon shutdown is informative because it removes the "you need more capital and a bigger brand" hypothesis. Amazon had both, and the answer was still no.
