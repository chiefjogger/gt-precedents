# Flipkart Wholesale / Walmart B2B India

## Founding / launch

Flipkart Group launched Flipkart Wholesale (a digital B2B marketplace targeting kiranas and MSMEs) in July 2020, simultaneously acquiring 100% of Walmart India Private Limited, which operated the Best Price cash-and-carry warehouse-club business since 2009. Lead architects: Adarsh Menon (Flipkart Wholesale head, ex-Flipkart Group VP), Sameer Aggarwal (Walmart India CEO at acquisition), Kalyan Krishnamurthy (Flipkart Group CEO), Judith McKenna (Walmart International CEO).

[source: Walmart corporate, "Walmart's Majority-Owned Flipkart Launches Wholesale Business", 23 Jul 2020, https://corporate.walmart.com/news/2020/07/23/walmarts-majority-owned-flipkart-launches-wholesale-business-to-help-small-businesses-in-india-source-directly-from-manufacturers-and-producers]

## Capital

Internal Walmart-Flipkart capital, not separately disclosed. Best Price was an established loss-making asset on Walmart India's books at the time of transfer; the Inc42 reading at acquisition was that Flipkart "plans to consolidate loss-making Walmart ops" [source: Inc42, "After Acquisition, Flipkart Plans To Consolidate Loss Making Walmart Ops", https://inc42.com/buzz/after-acquisition-flipkart-plans-to-consolidate-loss-making-walmart-ops/]. Cumulative Walmart investment in Flipkart Group: USD 16 Bn (2018) plus follow-on funding rounds.

## Operating metrics at acquisition

| Metric | Value (2020) | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Best Price stores | 28 cash-and-carry warehouse clubs | Walmart |
| Best Price member base | 1.5 Mn+ kiranas / MSMEs | Walmart |
| Years of brand operation | 12 years (since 2009) | Walmart |
| Categories | grocery, FMCG, apparel, mobile (later) | Flipkart |

## Product evolution

- Jul 2020: Best Price acquisition + Flipkart Wholesale digital platform launch.
- 2020-2022: Tried to digitize the 1.5 Mn member base into the new app-based marketplace; layered grocery and apparel; offered short-term credit (INR 5,000-2 lakh, 14-day interest-free) to kirana buyers.
- 2023: Reorganization. All Flipkart Wholesale employees outside core merchandising and operations were folded back into Flipkart Group functions. Effectively a re-absorption rather than a clean shutdown.
- 2024: Best Price physical stores continue to operate; the Flipkart Wholesale app continues; the standalone "Wholesale" brand has been reduced to a subsidiary signal.

[source: Inc42 / Indian Retailer, "Flipkart Wholesale Plans to Reorganize and Refine Business Operations", https://www.indianretailer.com/news/flipkart-wholesale-plans-to-reorganize-and-refine-business-operations.n12533]

## Voice

Walmart corporate communication at launch (2020) framed Flipkart Wholesale as the digital channel to extend the Best Price franchise into millions of kiranas; the language of "transform marketplace" was the strategic pitch.

BusinessToday (Jul 2020): "Flipkart Wholesale vs Reliance JioMart: The battle lines are drawn to win India's kiranas" framed it as a two-horse race against Jio [source: BusinessToday, https://www.businesstoday.in/latest/corporate/story/flipkart-launches-of-flipkart-wholesale-aims-to-transform-kirana-retail-268037-2020-07-23].

By 2023 the press tone had shifted: Indian Retailer described the move as "reorganize and refine"; in practice headcount was redistributed to other Flipkart functions, suggesting standalone wholesale economics did not deliver.

## Current status

Continues to operate but rationalized. The standalone Flipkart Wholesale brand has been folded operationally into Flipkart Group. Best Price physical stores still trade. The Best Price Flipkart Wholesale app is live on Google Play and the App Store. No clean shutdown announcement; the path was absorption, not closure.

## Failure mode (graveyard read: did not break out)

Walmart-Flipkart spent over a decade and a meaningful capital line trying to convert a 1.5 Mn-member cash-and-carry business into a digital kirana B2B marketplace, and the operational outcome (per the 2023 reorganization) is that the standalone economics did not justify a standalone team. The same forces that defeated Amazon's B2B arm (kirana behavioral inertia, regulatory pressure, FDI optics, competition from Reliance) applied. Flipkart Wholesale's outcome is more graceful than Amazon's because the Best Price physical infrastructure has residual value as an MSME and kirana cash-and-carry channel, and because Walmart's broader Flipkart investment thesis carried it. But the kirana digitization piece specifically did not become the standalone profit engine that the 2020 launch implied.
