# ShopX: deep supplement

This file extends `shopx.md`. Numbers in the base file are not re-litigated.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran

**1.1 Assisted-commerce kiosk model with kirana as e-commerce node (2015 to 2018).** ShopX's launch product turned the kirana into a "B2B and B2B2C" point of sale: kirana owner browses ShopX catalog on a tablet, places orders for inventory plus serves walk-in customers who want SKUs the kirana does not stock. Apoorva Jois and Amit Sharma framed it as bringing "the same cutting-edge technology and supply chain solutions that established ecommerce or retailers access" to the 12 million small merchants who form ~90% of India's USD 700 Bn consumption economy [source: Inc42, "Nandan Nilekani Invests $2 Mn In Assisted Ecommerce Startup ShopX", https://inc42.com/buzz/nandan-nilekani-invests-2-mn-in-assisted-ecommerce-startup-shopx/]. Read: the consumer-facing assisted-commerce premise required smartphone-illiterate buyers to keep coming through the kirana for online SKUs. Smartphone penetration overtook the thesis between 2017 and 2020.

**1.2 Fung Investment debt facility (2018 to 2021).** ShopX raised USD 35 Mn from Fung Strategic Holdings (Singapore) in May 2018, the only meaningful late-stage equity round it ever closed. Through 2019 to 2021 the company also drew down INR-denominated debt from Fung Investment, the same Hong Kong family group's investment vehicle, to bridge the gap as fresh equity dried up [source: Inc42, "Ecommerce Enabler ShopX Becomes Latest Startup To Shut Operations, Files For Insolvency", Aug 2022, https://inc42.com/buzz/ecommerce-enabler-shopx-becomes-latest-startup-to-shut-operations-files-for-insolvency/]. The debt was the proximate trigger of the IBC filing: ShopX could not service the rupee loans when no fresh equity arrived after April 2020.

**1.3 The 2021 pivot to a D2C cashback app.** With B2B unit economics not converging, the team rebuilt the product as a consumer-facing cashback app, putting it head-on against PhonePe, Cashkaro, Amazon Pay and Flipkart's reward stack. Headcount cut from ~260 to ~130 to fund the pivot. The cashback model required perpetual marketing subsidy to maintain user retention, which is the same wall Cuddles, CashKaro, and others have hit. ShopX had a fraction of the marketing budget [source: PYMNTS, "ShopX Shutdown Points to B2B Platform Pitfalls", 2022, https://www.pymnts.com/news/b2b-payments/2022/shopx-shutdown-points-to-b2b-platform-pitfalls/].

**1.4 The Aug 2022 Section 10 IBC filing.** On 22 Aug 2022 ShopX filed for insolvency under Section 10 of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code, 2016, with filings to the Registrar of Companies. Founder Amit Sharma's shutdown notice: "Since the business model has not succeeded, it has not been able to generate enough cash flow from operations or raise new capital and is therefore unable to meet its various payment obligations and has ceased to be a going concern" [source: Inc42 shutdown report, Aug 2022; YourStory, "Ecommerce enabler ShopX shuts down operations", Aug 2022, https://yourstory.com/2022/08/ecommerce-enabler-shopx-shuts-operations-bankruptcy-insolvency]. Total raised by shutdown: ~USD 60 Mn cumulative, of which Nilekani's personal investments were ~USD 18 Mn.

## 5. Customer voice (kirana owners specifically)

The public reviewer base for ShopX as a kirana app is thin compared to Udaan. The Runway Ventures and PYMNTS retrospectives summarize the operating signal: kiranas in tier-2 / tier-3 cities found the assisted-commerce model novel but tradeable. Once Reliance's JioMart and Walmart's Flipkart Wholesale opened in 2020, kiranas had stronger-credit, larger-SKU, lower-friction alternatives without paying ShopX's spread. The cashback pivot in 2021 abandoned the kirana audience entirely.

A kirana owner would, in 2018 to 2020, have placed a typical ShopX order for a tablet-displayed catalog of consumer durables and select FMCG SKUs. Closest sourced estimate of merchant unit economics: the take rate on assisted-commerce orders was 8 to 12% of the consumer ticket, of which kirana commission was 2 to 4% and ShopX retained the rest. No surfaced public quote from a kirana owner using ShopX is available in the search trail. Thin-research flag on customer voice; what exists is founder-side and journalist-side framing rather than user-side testimony.

## 6. The IC clearance read against Dr. Quang's six gates

Consumer Truth: ShopX validated that small retailers will accept a tablet-on-counter kiosk if it delivers a price or assortment edge but did not validate that the kirana's walk-in customer would re-shape buying behavior toward ordered-through-kirana goods. Product Truth: assisted-commerce as a UX broke against rising smartphone penetration; the 2021 D2C cashback pivot was a wholesale business-model change, not a product iteration. Equity Transferability: ShopX's tech stack and merchant relationships had limited residual value; the IBC sale process did not surface a successor entity. Value Creation Sizing: USD 18 Mn from Nilekani plus USD 35 Mn Fung plus debt yielded zero realized value; a one-investor-dependent capital stack is the single most fragile pattern in the cohort. Competitive Dynamics: by 2020 to 2021 JioMart, Flipkart Wholesale and Udaan had drained the kirana B2B oxygen; a sub-USD 100 Mn raised company could not bid for shelf space against three balance-sheet players. Learnability: ShopX is the cleanest case in the cohort that "kirana B2B platform" without (a) deep balance sheet, (b) WhatsApp-native UX, or (c) strategic FMCG ownership of supply, will not generate a venture-scale outcome. The structural lesson for WinX is that B2B platform plays inside a 1 to 3% margin distribution channel require either captive supply (which Masan has via WCM and MCH) or captive credit (which Masan does not), and ideally both.
