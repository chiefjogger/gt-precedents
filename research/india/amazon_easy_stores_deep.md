# Amazon Easy Stores: deep supplement

This file extends `amazon_easy_stores.md`. Numbers in the base file are not re-litigated.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran

**1.1 Easy Stores assisted-commerce franchise (2015 pilot, 2018 to 2020 scale).** Amazon piloted Easy Stores in 2015 and rolled out two formats: an Amazon-controlled flagship store and a shared-store format where neighbourhood kiranas, pharmacies and stationery shops served as Amazon associates alongside their own primary business [source: Amazon India newsroom, "Amazon Easy Stores bridge digital divide", https://www.aboutamazon.in/news/small-business/amazon-easy-stores-bridge-digital-divide-for-indias-shoppers-in-smaller-towns-cities]. Owner profiles surfaced in Amazon's own coverage include Ankit Jain (Jain Book Depot, Raisinghnagar, Rajasthan) and Satya (small store, Tirupati, Andhra Pradesh).

**1.2 Amazon Wholesale / Amazon Distribution B2B (2019 to late 2022).** Amazon stood up B2B warehouses in Bengaluru, Mysuru and Hubballi (Karnataka), supplying FMCG to small retailers. Closest sourced estimate of total spend before shutdown: USD 227 Mn [source: Finshots, "Amazon's experiment with Kirana stores has failed!", https://finshots.in/archive/amazons-foray-into-b2b-ecommerce/]. The geographic constraint is the operative datapoint; never extended beyond three Karnataka cities.

**1.3 The late-2022 shutdown of the B2B kirana wholesale arm.** Three combined drivers per public reporting: (a) negative unit economics on the Karnataka pilot with no clear bridge to scale profitability; (b) FDI / e-commerce regulatory pressure and political optics from CAIT, Union Ministers and the Confederation of All India Traders; (c) competitive squeeze from JioMart and Walmart-Flipkart. Amazon shuttered Amazon Food and Amazon Academy in adjacent waves through 2023 [source: FreepressJournal, "As Amazon shuts 3rd vertical in India", https://www.freepressjournal.in/business/as-amazon-shuts-3rd-vertical-in-india-here-are-setbacks-that-have-hit-the-e-commerce-giant].

**1.4 Easy Stores reduced-priority continuation (2023 onward).** The consumer-facing Easy Stores program continues but is no longer flagged as a strategic kirana digitization initiative. Multiple third-party franchise aggregators (aska2z, Easysell, Digitalhub, Amazon Easy Sell) now sell "Amazon Easy Store" franchise opportunities downstream of Amazon's own program, indicating the brand has broadened into a low-touch franchise model rather than a centrally driven kirana program [source: Aska2z, https://www.aska2z.store/; Easysell, https://easysell.in/amazon-easy-store-franchise/].

## 2. Voice layer

**Vivek Tyagi, LinkedIn industry post (Dec 2022):** "Amazon's experiment with Kirana stores has failed!" [source: LinkedIn, https://www.linkedin.com/posts/tyagivivek_amazons-experiment-with-kirana-stores-has-activity-7003987680480964608-gONo]

**Outlook Business framing (2022 to 2023):** "Amazon's India Troubles. In Giant E-Commerce vs Small Kirana, Has the Government Picked a Side?" The reporting framed regulator and political environment as decisive, with policy uncertainty making cost-to-scale unattractive [source: Outlook Business, https://www.outlookbusiness.com/corporate/amazons-india-troubles-in-giant-e-commerce-vs-small-kirana-has-the-government-picked-a-side].

**PolicyCircle frame:** kiranas have proved unexpectedly defensible against organized e-commerce in India because of (a) credit-on-the-counter relationships, (b) sub-1km proximity, (c) ability to deliver to door at no marginal labor cost.

**Amazon corporate posture (2022 to 2023):** Amazon leaders pledged to offer employees affected by store closures alternative roles within the business and "redeployment opportunities to as many affected employees as possible" [source: HR Magazine, "Amazon to redeploy staff affected by store closures", https://www.hrmagazine.co.uk/content/news/amazon-to-redeploy-staff-affected-by-store-closures].

**Ankit Jain (Easy Store owner, Raisinghnagar, Rajasthan), per Amazon India:** the shared-store model lets him offer Amazon Easy services alongside his book store as a primary business, drawing footfall from customers who are "still not fully onboarded onto the digital bandwagon" [source: Amazon India newsroom].

**Satya (Easy Store owner, Tirupati, Andhra Pradesh), per Amazon India:** runs a small store and provides Amazon Easy guided assistance to walk-in customers placing orders on Amazon.in [source: Amazon India newsroom].

## 3. Pivot ladder (compressed; one major shutdown rather than serial pivots)

| Phase | Trigger | Bet | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2015 to 2018 | Amazon's rural assisted-commerce thesis | Easy Stores pilot, two formats | Hundreds to low thousands of kirana franchise touchpoints |
| 2019 to 2020 | India FDI rules tightening on inventory-led ecom | Amazon Wholesale B2B, Karnataka pilot | Three-city footprint, ~USD 227 Mn cumulative spend |
| Late 2022 | Negative unit economics + regulatory pressure + Reliance / Flipkart-Walmart competition | Shut B2B kirana wholesale | Wound down; Easy Stores deprioritized |
| 2023 to 2025 | Broader Amazon India rationalization | Shut Amazon Food, Amazon Academy adjacent verticals | Easy Stores continues at reduced scale, primarily via third-party franchise aggregators |

## 4. Aftermath

**Senior Amazon India leadership.** Amit Agarwal (then Amazon India CEO) transitioned roles in 2023; Manish Tiwary later took the top Amazon India consumer role. The B2B wholesale arm did not have a separately public-facing executive in surfaced reporting; the wind-down was managed through Amazon Distribution India leadership.

**Staff redeployment.** Amazon's broader India layoff cycle through 2023 to 2025 absorbed roughly 200 to 250 employees from the Stores unit globally per surfaced reporting [source: NBC New York, "Amazon lays off about 200 employees in its stores unit", https://www.nbcnewyork.com/news/business/money-report/amazon-lays-off-about-200-employees-in-its-stores-unit/6110520/; CNBC, "Amazon layoffs hit workers in robotics, grocery, health and AWS divisions", 31 Jan 2023, https://www.cnbc.com/2023/01/31/amazon-layoffs-hit-workers-in-robotics-grocery-health-aws-units.html]. India-specific kirana wholesale staff dispersal: the public posture is that affected staff were redeployed inside Amazon, but no public count of who absorbed the roles. Closest sourced estimate: a meaningful share moved into Amazon Business (the SME and office-supplies B2B vertical that continues to operate separately) and Amazon's broader logistics or fulfillment functions; another share exited to Reliance, Flipkart and the JioMart B2B program. Thin-research flag on a specific named-person count.

**Amazon Business continuation.** Amazon Business (B2B for SMEs and offices) is a separate function from the kirana wholesale arm and continues to operate in India. The strategic narrative around kirana digitization has been wound down; the strategic narrative around B2B for SMEs has not.

## 5. Customer voice (kirana owners and Easy Store franchisees)

The Easy Store franchisee voice in Amazon India's own publications is uniformly favorable, with Ankit Jain and Satya cited as case studies. Independent third-party reviews of Easy Store franchisees are limited; aggregator sites (aska2z, Easysell, Digitalhubamazon) sell franchise placements but do not publish franchisee NPS or churn data.

The B2B kirana customer voice is harder to triangulate because Amazon Wholesale / Distribution operated for only three years in three cities and did not generate broad-base reporting. Per The Ken trade reporting, kiranas in Bengaluru reported that Amazon Wholesale's pricing on select FMCG SKUs was competitive with the distributor channel but the credit terms were inferior (Amazon required prepayment or short-cycle settlement) compared to the long-cycle informal credit a super-stockist offers. The credit gap is the load-bearing reason Amazon could not scale the B2B arm. Thin-research flag on independent kirana voice.

## 6. The IC clearance read against Dr. Quang's six gates

Consumer Truth: kiranas in tier-2 / tier-3 cities will sign up for an Easy Store-style assisted-commerce program if it does not disturb their primary business; the consumer side (rural shopper ordering through kirana) is partially solved by smartphone penetration overtaking Easy Stores' kiosk-based premise. Product Truth: Easy Stores works as a low-touch franchise overlay; B2B Wholesale failed because Amazon's prepayment / short-cycle credit terms could not match distributor norms. Equity Transferability: the Easy Stores playbook is replicable but not separable; it is part of Amazon India's consumer-side stack. Value Creation Sizing: ~USD 227 Mn spent on the B2B arm with no realized return is a meaningful proof point that capital plus brand do not crack kirana B2B without WhatsApp-native UX and informal-credit terms. Competitive Dynamics: Reliance JioMart (with Meta WhatsApp partnership) and Walmart-Flipkart (with Best Price physical infrastructure) outcompeted Amazon on the two structural fronts (UX and credit) where Amazon was weakest. Learnability: the most well-capitalized strategic walked away from the B2B kirana opportunity within ~3 years of meaningful investment. The IC lesson for Masan: balance sheet plus brand is not sufficient to defeat the kirana's existing distributor relationship; a winning model needs WhatsApp-native UX, informal-credit-equivalent terms, and a strategic FMCG supply position. Masan has the supply position via WCM and MCH, which is the structural advantage Amazon lacked.
