# ShopX

## Founding

Founded August 2015 in Bengaluru by Apoorva Jois and Amit Sharma (CEO; engineering background, MBA UCLA). Original thesis: provide a B2B mobile and web platform that lets kirana stores and SME retailers in tier-2/3 cities order goods directly without leaving the shop, with logistics and procurement support layered in.

[source: Inc42, "Ecommerce Enabler ShopX Becomes Latest Startup To Shut Operations, Files For Insolvency", 2022, https://inc42.com/buzz/ecommerce-enabler-shopx-becomes-latest-startup-to-shut-operations-files-for-insolvency/]

## Operating metrics

Operating disclosure was thin. Pre-shutdown reporting:
- Headcount: ~260 at peak in 2019; cut by 50% (130 employees) in 2021 pivot; remaining staff laid off Aug 2022.
- Geographic footprint: tier-2 / tier-3 focus, never a full national network.
- Merchant count and GMV: not publicly disclosed; signals point to a few thousand active retailers (peak), well below Udaan or Jumbotail scale.
- Take rate: not disclosed.

## Product evolution

- 2015-2018: B2B kirana procurement app + offline distribution.
- 2021: Pivoted to D2C cashback app (B2C), realizing B2B unit economics did not work.
- 2022: Failed to raise fresh capital; defaulted on rupee debt to Fung Investment.
- 22 Aug 2022: Filed for insolvency under Section 10 of the IBC, 2016. All employees laid off.

## Failure mode

Three documented breaks: (1) the underlying B2B FMCG distribution P&L is low-single-digit margin, and ShopX as a thin-layer enabler captured an even smaller slice; (2) the 2021 pivot to a B2C cashback app put it in head-on competition with Amazon, Flipkart and PhonePe with a fraction of the marketing budget, and the cashback model required perpetual subsidy; (3) Fung-issued INR loans came due before either business showed cash flow. Founder Amit Sharma stated in the shutdown notice that "since the business model has not succeeded, it has not been able to generate enough cash flow from operations or raise new capital and is therefore unable to meet its various payment obligations and has ceased to be a going concern" [source: Inc42 shutdown report, Aug 2022, headline above].

## Voice

Financial Express on X (22 Aug 2022): "ShopX, a startup backed by Nandan Nilekani, has shut down and declared bankruptcy because its business model did not work" [source: Financial Express on X, 22 Aug 2022, https://x.com/FinancialXpress/status/1561942549111394304].

PYMNTS analysis: "ShopX Shutdown Points to B2B Platform Pitfalls", noted the structural problem: kirana B2B requires either deep working capital pockets to hold inventory at near-distributor cost, or merchant scale large enough to extract advertising / fintech revenue. ShopX had neither [source: PYMNTS, 2022, https://www.pymnts.com/news/b2b-payments/2022/shopx-shutdown-points-to-b2b-platform-pitfalls/].

The Runway Ventures retrospective (post-mortem): "How ShopX lost the game and went from $100M to bankruptcy" attributes the collapse to over-reliance on a single overseas debt source and an inability to scale GMV before the pivot capital ran out [source: The Runway Ventures, https://www.therunway.ventures/p/shopx].

## Current status

Wound up. Insolvency proceedings under IBC 2016 commenced August 2022. No successor entity.
