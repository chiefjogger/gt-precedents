# Udaan: deep supplement

This file extends `udaan.md`. Numbers in the base file are not re-litigated here. The focus is on the experiments, the voice layer, the post-hoc career trail, the kirana customer voice, and the IC clearance read.

## 1. Specific experiments the company ran

**1.1 The case-pack catalog (2017 to 2019).** Udaan's launch SKU shape mirrored the Walmart B2B Sam's Club mental model: minimum order quantity at a case (12 to 48 units of an FMCG SKU), priced at distributor-equivalent rates. The unit-economics logic was clean on paper because case picks are fast, single-line dispatches with low variable warehouse cost. The behavioral failure surfaced inside 18 months. Per The Ken's trade reporting, kiranas re-ordered roughly 80% of the same 200 to 400 SKUs every 7 to 14 days and rarely needed full cases of slower-moving SKUs. By 2020 Udaan had broken case packs in select categories. Jumbotail used the cracked case as a wedge in 2019 to 2020, reaching 90% sub-case order share on FMCG [source: The Ken, "Udaan is still not done with kiranas", https://the-ken.com/tradetricks/udaan-is-still-not-done-with-kiranas/].

**1.2 UdaanCapital and the Northern Arc co-lending experiment (Apr 2022).** UdaanCapital (the Hiveloop Capital NBFC) had been disbursing on its own balance sheet since Dec 2018. The Apr 2022 partnership with Northern Arc Capital was the explicit attempt to turn Udaan's transaction data into a scaled co-lending engine: ticket sizes INR 10,000 to INR 25 lakh, target merchant pool of 50,000 kiranas, credit rails wired into the order flow as "CreditBuy" [source: Business Standard, "udaanCapital ties up with Northern Arc Capital for financing 50,000 kiranas", 26 Apr 2022, https://www.business-standard.com/article/companies/udaancapital-ties-up-with-northern-arc-capital-for-financing-50-000-kiranas-122042600616_1.html]. The disbursement run-rate climbed to roughly INR 8,000 Cr per annum across ~150,000 kiranas by FY24 [source: udaanCapital company materials, https://udaancapital.com/financial-institutions]. The structural ceiling was the NBFC capital base: Hiveloop Capital's net portfolio sat at INR 359 Cr at March 2024 with PAR 90 of 0.5% but a write-off rate of 4.9% [source: ICRA, "Hiveloop Capital Private Limited: Ratings reaffirmed", https://www.icra.in/Rating/ShowRationalReportFilePdf/129776]. Read: the credit arm scaled the loan book but never threw off enough net interest margin to subsidize the marketplace P&L.

**1.3 Private label staples push (2020 onward, accelerating 2024).** Udaan launched private-label SKUs in staples and apparel from 2020, claiming gross margin of 18 to 22% versus 8 to 12% on national-brand SKUs. The 2024 revenue run-rate for private-label brands grew 250% YoY against the broader 65% YoY GMV growth, and Udaan delivered 2.45 billion FMCG units in calendar 2024 [source: Indian Startup Times, "Udaan Raises $114M in Series G, Sharpens Focus on FMCG, HoReCa as IPO Prep Intensifies", 2 Jun 2025, https://www.indianstartuptimes.com/news/udaan-raises-114m-in-series-g-sharpens-focus-on-fmcg-horeca-as-ipo-prep-intensifies/]. Vaibhav Gupta has framed private label as the "essential strategy to drive margin improvements" in IPO-cycle messaging [source: Business Standard, "Udaan raises $114 mn from M&G Investments, Lightspeed", 2 Jun 2025, https://www.business-standard.com/companies/news/udaan-raises-114-mn-from-m-g-investments-lightspeed-ahead-of-planned-ipo-125060200488_1.html]. Read: the experiment is real, the contribution is small relative to the INR 18,200 Cr GMV base, and it is the load-bearing margin story for the IPO pitch.

**1.4 Pharma and HoReCa category vertical experiments (2019 to 2021).** Udaan opened pharma B2B in 2019 and a HoReCa (hotel-restaurant-catering) vertical in 2020 to 2021. Pharma did not scale because the regulated drug distribution channel locks margin and inventory liability inside CDSCO-licensed distributors, and Udaan's marketplace model could not absorb the regulatory overhead. HoReCa was de-emphasized post-2022 cost mode and only re-elevated in 2024 to 2025 as a profitability-friendly category alongside FMCG [source: Indian Startup Times, 2 Jun 2025]. Read: category sprawl was a 2020 to 2021 capital-cycle decision; the 2024 framing collapses Udaan back to FMCG plus HoReCa.

**1.5 Reverse flip from Singapore to India for the IPO (2025 to 2026).** Udaan secured NCLT approval to consolidate its tech, logistics and wholesale trading entities under Hiveloop Ecommerce. CEO Vaibhav Gupta told ET in early 2026 the redomiciliation from Singapore to India would commence within weeks, targeting a listing in 9 to 18 months [source: India IPO News, "IPO-bound Udaan to kickstart reverse flip to India in weeks: CEO Vaibhav Gupta", https://www.indiaipo.in/news/detail/bound-udaan-to-kickstart-reverse-flip-to-india-in-weeks-ceo-vaibhav-gupta]. The corporate restructuring is itself an experiment in tax and regulatory positioning that PhonePe, Razorpay, Pine Labs and Groww also ran with mixed cost outcomes. Read: this is housekeeping for the BSE/NSE filing window.

**1.6 Headcount compression as a deliberate program (2022 to 2024).** Udaan ran four discrete layoff waves: Jun 2022 (180), Nov 2022 (350), Dec 2023 (120 to 150 immediately after the Series E close), and rolling through FY24. Net headcount went from ~11,500 in Oct 2023 (LinkedIn) to ~1,600 post-cuts [source: BusinessToday, "Udaan lays off 150 employees days after securing $340 mn", 18 Dec 2023, https://www.businesstoday.in/entrepreneurship/story/udaan-lays-off-150-employees-days-after-securing-340-mn-report-409837-2023-12-18; Inc42, "Udaan Fires 350 Employees In Second Wave Of Layoffs This Year", 2022, https://inc42.com/buzz/udaan-fires-350-employees-in-second-wave-of-layoffs-this-year/]. Vaibhav Gupta's public framing: burn cut by 80% in two years and 40% per year for three. Read: the layoff sequence is the most decisive operating experiment Udaan ran. Capital efficiency improved; growth disappeared.

## 5. Customer voice (kirana owners specifically)

The kirana voice on Udaan is bimodal in public reviews and offers a tighter operational read than the founder press cycle. Positive reports anchor around (a) ability to access pan-India SKUs without driving to a wholesaler, (b) credit lines via UdaanCapital that bypass distributor terms, and (c) price-discovery transparency on FMCG. Negative reports anchor around (a) settlement disputes after promised offers, (b) app reliability, and (c) customer-service unresponsiveness when an order goes wrong [source: Trustpilot, https://www.trustpilot.com/review/udaan.com; MouthShut, https://www.mouthshut.com/product-reviews/udaan-reviews-926003038].

The Ken's trade reporting through 2023 to 2024 framed the structural kirana objection as behavioral. The kirana places ~80% of weekly orders against a known 200 to 400 SKU set with a known person on WhatsApp. Switching to an app means typing, scrolling, and forgoing the soft credit and personal trust attached to the existing distributor relationship. Per the same reporting, JioMart, Reliance Mitra, ElasticRun and individual super-stockists have all migrated to WhatsApp ordering, which dilutes Udaan's core UX moat to the point that the company's defensibility narrative now leans on private label and category breadth, not workflow [source: The Ken, "Udaan is still not done with kiranas"].

Closest sourced estimate of unit-level kirana economics on Udaan: a kirana store owner could bulk-buy a two-tube combo of Colgate MaxFresh through JioMart at INR 115 versus INR 154 from traditional distributors (a 25% gap) [source: Logistics Insider, "JioMart joins hands with Whatsapp to propel Kirana stores", https://www.logisticsinsider.in/jiomart-joins-hands-with-whatsapp-to-propel-kirana-stores-into-an-online-retail-platform/]. Udaan's price gap is in the same band per company materials, but does not dominate the distributor + credit + returns + relationship bundle at the order-by-order decision point.

**Thin-research flag.** First-person Reddit threads from kirana owners using Udaan are not surfaced in public English-language search; vernacular language sources (Hindi, Kannada, Tamil) likely host more granular discussion. Glassdoor, Trustpilot, and MouthShut are the load-bearing public reviews.

## 6. The IC clearance read against Dr. Quang's six gates

Consumer Truth: Udaan validated that kiranas need credit and price transparency but failed to validate that kiranas want an app-first ordering UX. The category truth (FMCG distribution is INR 1 to 3% net margin, not a venture-scale return) was always present and the Series E down round priced it correctly. Product Truth: the case-pack catalog broke against sub-case demand; UdaanCapital scaled but at sub-prime credit losses; private label is real but small. Equity Transferability: Udaan's tech stack (warehousing, credit underwriting, ad-tech) is portable to any FMCG distributor and the IPO bookrunners will rely on this read. Value Creation Sizing: a USD 18,200 Cr GMV base at 1 to 3% net distributor margin caps EBITDA upside; the IPO pitch needs private label and HoReCa to flex margin. Competitive Dynamics: WhatsApp-first ordering by JioMart, ElasticRun and the entrenched super-stockist channel has neutralized Udaan's app-UX advantage; the residual moat is the warehouse and credit footprint. Learnability: the four documented layoff waves and the eight-year time-and-money cost (USD 1.6 Bn cumulative equity, peak headcount 11,500) demonstrate that the kirana app thesis is not capital-light, not behaviorally aligned, and not a category Masan should fund into without a WhatsApp-first interaction model wired to FMCG and credit P&L.
