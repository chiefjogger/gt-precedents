# Udaan

## Founding

Udaan was founded in late 2016 by three former Flipkart executives: Vaibhav Gupta (Business Finance and Analytics), Sujeet Kumar (President of Operations, supply chain, logistics) and Amod Malviya (CTO). The original thesis: India's USD 700 Bn B2B retail trade ran through 25-30 layers of intermediaries (super-stockist, distributor, sub-distributor, wholesaler), pricing was opaque, credit was informal, and a single nationwide app-first marketplace could compress the chain. Initial categories: lifestyle (apparel, footwear), electronics, staples, FMCG.

[source: YourStory, "Behind 26 months and 8 years of grit: Sujeet Kumar", Oct 2018, https://yourstory.com/2018/10/behind-26-months-8-years-grit-hardwork-got-us-unicorn-status-sujeet-kumar-co-founder-udaan]

## The 2023 down round in detail

Lead: M&G plc (UK insurer / asset manager, USD ~370 Bn AUM), with participation from existing investors Lightspeed and DST Global. Headline size USD 340 Mn, but the round combined fresh primary equity with conversion of existing convertible notes that had been issued during the 18-month interim funding gap. Effective fresh primary cash was materially below the headline figure (closest sourced estimate: convertible-note conversion plus debt clean-up made up a meaningful share, exact split not publicly disclosed). Valuation cut 43.5% from USD 3.1 Bn (Jan 2021) to USD 1.7-1.8 Bn post-money. The cut came against a backdrop of FY23 GMV collapsing 43.1% YoY (INR 9,900 Cr to INR 5,629 Cr), evidencing that the prior valuation reflected a growth trajectory that did not materialize.

[source: DealStreetAsia, "Indian B2B marketplace Udaan in talks to raise $100m at flat valuation", 2024, https://www.dealstreetasia.com/stories/udaan-funding-4-417035]

## Product evolution

Launch products (2017): apparel, electronics, staples sold case-pack via app, with 24-48 hr delivery. Layered on:
- 2018: UdaanCapital (working-capital credit to merchants, NBFC route via Hiveloop Capital).
- 2019-20: Pharma, fresh, HoReCa categories.
- 2020-21: Private label staples and apparel (gross margin 18-22% vs 8-12% on national SKUs per company materials).
- 2022: Ad-tech monetization (sponsored listings, in-app banner inventory) targeted at FMCG brand budgets.
- 2024-25: AI demand-forecasting tools for retailer SaaS layer.

What stuck: FMCG and staples (the volume base), private label, UdaanCapital. What did not scale: pharma B2B, fresh and produce (operationally hard, undercut by Ninjacart/Jumbotail in fresh), advertising revenue (estimated INR 120 Cr in FY25 against an ~INR 18,000 Cr GMV; sub-1% take, well short of marketplace ad benchmarks of 3-5%).

## Why WhatsApp-based ordering disrupted the app-first model

Udaan's bet: kirana owner downloads app, browses 3-5 Mn SKU catalog, places order, gets delivered. Problem documented in The Ken trade reporting: kiranas do not browse. They re-order ~80% of the same 200-400 SKUs every 7-14 days from a known person. WhatsApp ordering (which JioMart, Reliance Mitra, ElasticRun, distributor reps, and the established super-stockist now all use) replicates that re-order conversation with zero training cost. Apps require literacy, data, and behavioral change; WhatsApp does not. Jumbotail's leadership stated 90% of FMCG orders placed on its platform are sub-case quantities (5 units of a biscuit, not 1 case): the case-pack assumption baked into Udaan's app workflow excluded the long tail of small kirana orders.

[source: The Ken (Trade Tricks), "Udaan is still not done with kiranas", https://the-ken.com/tradetricks/udaan-is-still-not-done-with-kiranas/]

## Inventory and warehousing model

Udaan ran a 1P-heavy hybrid: it took inventory on its books for fast-moving SKUs, ran 200+ regional FCs via UdaanExpress, and used LTL trucking with mixed fleet for the kirana drop. Working capital intensity was the structural problem: with 98.5% of GMV in traded goods at low single-digit gross margin, every INR 100 of GMV needed INR 12-18 of working capital and ~INR 4-6 of fixed cost (warehouse + delivery). Marketplace pure-plays in B2B (Jumbotail's earlier model, ElasticRun) ran lower asset intensity but had identical thin margins.

## Why advertising never scaled

The pitch to investors from 2019 onward: 3 Mn merchants on platform, FMCG brands will pay for visibility, ads will become the high-margin revenue layer that subsidizes the thin-margin distribution layer. Reality, per FY25 disclosures: ad and platform monetization estimated INR 120 Cr against INR 18,200 Cr GMV (~0.66% take). Reasons publicly reported and inferable: kirana decision-maker is the kirana owner not the consumer (so brand consumer-targeting budgets do not flow); FMCG trade marketing budgets already committed to the distributor/super-stockist channel they cannot afford to alienate; merchant in-app dwell time is order-and-leave, not browse-and-discover. Compare against Amazon ads at ~7-8% take.

## Udaan Capital: what it tried, why it underperformed

Thesis: 30+ million Indian retailers are credit-thin; we have transaction data, we can underwrite. Hiveloop Capital (NBFC) commenced lending Dec 2018. Expansion via co-lending with bank/NBFC partners (Northern Arc partnership 2022). Ticket sizes INR 10,000 to INR 25 lakh, short tenor. Performance constraint: write-off rate 4.9% as of March 2024, against an unsecured MSME segment that requires 6-8% gross yield over cost of funds to clear. Net portfolio of INR 359 Cr at FY24-end is small relative to USD 1.6 Bn of equity raised by parent. UdaanCapital remained a feature attached to the marketplace, not an independent profit pool that could underwrite the marketplace's losses.

## Current status

Operating; targeting 2026 IPO at the BSE/NSE; flat USD 1.8 Bn valuation; Vaibhav Gupta as CEO since Sep 2021 (replaced Sujeet Kumar at the helm). FY24 revenue INR 5,706.6 Cr, net loss INR 1,674 Cr, EBITDA margin -37.1%. Path-to-profitability narrative is the IPO pitch; full-group EBITDA breakeven targeted for ~mid-2026 per CEO guidance.

## Failure mode (graveyard read)

The kirana-app premise broke on three load-bearing assumptions: (1) merchants would adopt an app-first ordering UX (WhatsApp re-order won the behavior war); (2) marketplace ad take would scale to 3-5% (capped at <1%); (3) credit arm could throw off enough margin to subsidize distribution (constrained by NBFC capital and credit losses). The structural FMCG distribution P&L (1-3% net distributor margin) cannot absorb tech-startup unit cost. Udaan survived because it raised USD 1.6 Bn before the funding tap closed, cut 80-85% of headcount, and shrank GMV by half rather than burning through cash chasing the original thesis. The 2023 down round is the financial admission that the unicorn era thesis was overpriced.
