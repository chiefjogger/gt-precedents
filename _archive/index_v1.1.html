V. The four assets retail media requires

No Asian venture had all four. WinX is the first.

A retail media platform monetises four assets at once: a modern-trade footprint that anchors impression density, a general-trade wedge that captures the long tail, first-party FMCG brand ownership that funds the first dollar of advertising, and identified POS receipts that close the attribution loop. The cohort matrix shows which players had which assets at meaningful scale.

The closest Asian analogues had three of four (JioMart kirana programme, Mitra Bukalapak post-marketplace shutdown). Both reached profitability only as parent-funded programmes, not as venture-funded standalones. WinX is the first Vietnamese platform with the full configuration.

VIII. Strategic alignment

WinX sits inside the Group strategy and inside Techcombank's published commitment.

The cohort post-mortems argue WinX has the four assets the failed cohort lacked. The April 2026 Masan AGM frames the same four assets as the Consumer Operating System. The January 2026 Techcombank investor deck names the merchant-finance pattern WinX inherits and asks the bank to scale. Both institutional anchors are public, sourced, and structurally aligned with the v17 thesis.

The graveyard is the strongest argument for WinX, not against it.

The cohort post-mortems map cleanly onto the four-assets framework. Every venture that died lacked at least one asset. WinX is the first Vietnamese platform with all four.
