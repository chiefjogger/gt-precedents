# China Community Group Buying (社区团购): 2020 to 2022 Collapse

**Purpose.** Comparator for the WinX GT Digitization strategy memo. China is the largest non-Vietnam, non-India, non-Indonesia parallel because it deployed the most capital, most publicly, against a problem set adjacent to ours: digitizing fresh produce flow into low-income, fragmented neighbourhood demand. The failure was loud enough to leave a public record of what specifically broke. The disanalogies matter as much as the parallels and are flagged at the end.

**Reading note on the data.** Where multiple sources gave different numbers for the same metric, the version retained is the one with the most specific provenance (named investor, dated round, named regulator). Where verifiable triangulation was not possible, the entry is marked "Closest sourced estimate" and the source is named.

---

## 1. Sector overview

**The product, in one paragraph.** Community group buying (社区团购) was a next-day-pickup grocery model. A neighbourhood "team leader" (团长, tuanzhang), typically the owner of a corner convenience store, an apartment-block resident, or a courier station operator, ran a WeChat group of 100 to 500 households. Customers ordered fresh produce, eggs, frozen meat, and packaged FMCG by 23:00 the previous day; the platform aggregated demand at the city node, picked the next morning, trucked to the neighbourhood pickup point by 15:00, and the tuanzhang earned a 10 to 15 percent commission on goods sold from her shop [source: Sixth Tone, "The Shake-Out of China's Community Group-Buying Market", 24 Aug 2022, https://www.sixthtone.com/news/1010034]. The category-defining headline price was the RMB 0.99 head of cabbage and the RMB 0.99 / 250g pear that landed Nice Tuan a regulatory fine [source: South China Morning Post, "Community group buying: Chinese regulators fine businesses tied to Big Tech for price dumping", 3 Mar 2021, https://www.scmp.com/tech/policy/article/3123894].

**Capital deployed at peak.** The closest sourced public estimate of total VC and corporate capital deployed across the sector through end-2021 is "well over RMB 100 billion" (roughly USD 14 to 15 billion at then-FX) once Meituan's, Pinduoduo's, JD's, Alibaba's, and Didi's internal allocations are added to the independent rounds at Xingsheng Youxuan, Nice Tuan, and Tongcheng Life [source: Sixth Tone, "The Shake-Out of China's Community Group-Buying Market", 24 Aug 2022, https://www.sixthtone.com/news/1010034; source: ChinaTalk, "The rise and fall of community group buying: a recap", 2022, https://www.chinatalk.nl/the-rise-and-fall-of-community-group-buying-a-recap/]. The independent-startup share alone (Xingsheng + Nice Tuan + Tongcheng + smaller) cleared USD 6 billion in disclosed equity. Meituan's "new initiatives" segment, of which Meituan Select was the dominant cash burner, posted RMB 77.7 billion (around USD 11 billion) of cumulative segment losses 2020 to 2022 [source: Pandaily Pro, "Community Group Buying's Decline: How Meituan Lost Its Grocery Gamble", 2024, https://pro.pandaily.com/p/community-group-buyings-decline-how].

**The "nine-character rule" of December 2020.** On 22 December 2020 the State Administration for Market Regulation (SAMR) summoned Alibaba, Tencent, JD, Meituan, Pinduoduo, and Didi and laid down nine restrictions ("九个不得" / "nine shall-nots"): no selling below cost to take share, no monopolistic agreements, no abuse of dominant position, no unreviewed mergers, no false advertising, no misuse of consumer data, no use of algorithms or technical advantage to harm competitors or consumers, no improper price practices, no big-data discrimination [source: Nasdaq / Reuters, "China to tighten regulation of community group buying", 22 Dec 2020, https://www.nasdaq.com/articles/china-to-tighten-regulation-of-community-group-buying-2020-12-22]. On 3 March 2021 SAMR converted the warning into action: Chengxin Youxuan (Didi), Duo Duo Maicai (Pinduoduo), Meituan Youxuan, and Nice Tuan each fined RMB 1.5m; Shixianghui fined RMB 0.5m, all for "improper pricing behaviour" [source: TechNode, "China issues maximum fines on community group-buy firms", 3 Mar 2021, https://technode.com/2021/03/03/china-fines-five-community-group-buy-firms-for-irregular-pricing/].

**Sector inflection.** The arc was: peak-deployment Q4 2020 to Q2 2021; first scale-back August to September 2021 (Nice Tuan, Didi Chengxin); first death November 2021 (Tongcheng Life, the first major casualty); duopoly consolidation by mid-2022 (Pinduoduo's Duo Duo Maicai and Meituan Select); residual cleanup 2023 to 2025. China Merchants Securities cut its CGB market-size estimate to RMB 1 trillion in January 2022, half its prior figure [source: TechBuzz China, "Reports about the death of community group buying are greatly exaggerated", 2022, https://techbuzzchina.substack.com/p/reports-about-the-death-of-community].

## 2. Per-company postmortems

### 2.1 Meicai (美菜)

| Field | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Founded | 2014, Beijing | Bloomberg, Crunchbase |
| Founders | Liu Chuanjun (CEO), Xu Xueyin | Crunchbase, Bloomberg 2018 |
| Original thesis | App-based farm-to-restaurant B2B fresh produce supply for ~10m small and mid-size restaurants | Fortune, "Meicai Vegetable Vendor Startup Is Worth $2.8 Billion", 11 Jan 2018 |
| Total raised | ~USD 1.3bn over 7 disclosed rounds | Tracxn, Crunchbase |
| Peak valuation | USD 7bn, Series F October 2018, USD 600m round | Bloomberg, "Rocket Scientist's Veggie Startup Is Said Valued at $7 Billion", 9 Oct 2018 |
| Lead investors | Tiger Global, Hillhouse, ZhenFund (seed), Blue Lake (A), Shunwei (B), Sequoia, CMB International | Tracxn |
| Status 2024 to 2025 | Operating but radically smaller; ~USD 240m revenue, 1,581 employees, 300k restaurant customers (2024); private valuation marked down to ~USD 600m range on secondary trades | GetLatka 2024, PitchBook 2025 |

**Founder biography.** Liu Chuanjun is unusual in this cohort: master's from the Chinese Academy of Sciences, research contribution to the Shenzhou 6 and 7 manned spaceflight programmes; serial founder; exited his earlier group-buying site Wowo (55tuan) in 2011, then started Meicai in 2014 [source: Bloomberg, "Rocket Scientist's Veggie Startup Is Said Valued at $7 Billion", 9 Oct 2018, https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-10-09/china-veggie-selling-app-is-said-to-raise-more-than-600-million].

**Product evolution.** Three stages. (1) 2014 to 2018: pure B2B, restaurants ordering vegetables in-app the night before, Meicai-owned cold logistics; this is the model that took the company to USD 7bn. (2) 2019 to 2020: pivot toward C2M and "community group leader" model, opening Meicai Mall to direct consumers and recruiting tuanzhang; meant to lift gross margin and ride the CGB wave. (3) 2021 onward: COVID-era restaurant closures killed the core, the C2M arm was caught in the SAMR crackdown, and Meicai retreated to its B2B SKU and small-format restaurant base.

**Failure mode.** Three things broke at once. First, fresh logistics intensity: cold chain to each restaurant was capex-heavy and the 5 to 8 percent gross margin on vegetables could not service the warehouse and trucking burden once order density softened. Second, the IPO window slammed shut: Meicai filed confidentially for a US IPO in May 2021, pulled it after the Didi crackdown of July 2021, switched to Hong Kong (CICC, Citi, Nomura mandated November 2021), and never listed [source: Bloomberg, "Chinese Veggie Supplier Meicai Files Confidentially for U.S. IPO", 26 May 2021; SCMP, "Chinese e-commerce platform Meicai becomes latest mainland firm to shelve US IPO plan", 2021]. Third, layoffs began September 2021, headcount halved, then a further ~40 percent cut in 2022 [source: TechNode, "INSIGHTS: Chinese tech giants are still slashing headcounts", 8 Feb 2022, https://technode.com/2022/02/08/insights-chinese-tech-giants-are-still-slashing-headcounts/].

**Where the people went.** Liu Chuanjun remains CEO of the slimmed-down Meicai entity (confirmed via Crunchbase and The Org as of 2024). Mid-level operators who left in 2021 to 2022 were absorbed by Meituan grocery, JD's Qixian, and Hema (Alibaba); a non-trivial subset moved to Vietnamese and Indonesian quick-commerce operators in 2023 (anecdotal, not separately sourced).

**Reading.** Meicai is the cleanest example of the B2B fresh-supply thesis under-pricing the cost of physical fresh logistics in a fragmented restaurant market. The model worked at growth-equity scale in a zero-rate, zero-COVID world; it did not work once interest rates and freight costs normalised.

### 2.2 Xingsheng Youxuan (兴盛优选)

| Field | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Founded | January 2018, Changsha (Hunan); roots in a Yue family wholesale operation back to 1990 | Week in China, EqualOcean |
| Founders | Yue Lihua, Liu Huiyu, Zhou Yingjie | EqualOcean, Tracxn |
| Original thesis | Convert the existing Furong Xingsheng convenience-store franchise network in Hunan into a next-day-pickup grocery layer; tuanzhang = the existing shopkeeper | Week in China, "The new 'Tuan' economy", Dec 2020 |
| Total raised | Closest sourced estimate USD 4.5 to 5bn across Series A through pre-IPO | KrAsia, Bloomberg, Reuters aggregated |
| Peak valuation | USD 6 to 8bn (Feb 2021 round, USD 2bn raised, pre-money USD 6bn) | Reuters / Yahoo Finance, "Chinese grocery app Xingsheng Youxuan raises $2 bln", 1 Feb 2021 |
| Lead investors | Tencent (Jan 2021, USD 100m at USD 5bn), Sequoia China (lead Feb 2021 USD 2bn round), KKR, Today Capital, JD (Dec 2020 USD 700m), Temasek, Evergrande | Bloomberg, KrAsia, EqualOcean |

**Funding rounds, dated.**
- Series A, Oct 2018, "tens of millions", Today Capital + Jinshajiang + Zhenge.
- Series B, Sep 2019, USD 200m+, undisclosed lead.
- Strategic, Jul 2019, ~USD 40m, KKR.
- Series C, Jul 2020, USD 800m, KKR-led with Sequoia, Tencent, Today, Huaxing, Zhongding; post-money USD 4bn [source: EqualOcean, "Xingsheng Youxuan to Close Series C+ Round of Financing of USD 800 Million", 22 Jul 2020].
- Strategic, Dec 2020, USD 700m, JD.com.
- Strategic, Jan 2021, USD 100m, Tencent top-up at USD 5bn [source: Bloomberg, "Tencent Boosts Stake in Grocery App Xingsheng Youxuan", 8 Jan 2021].
- Series E / pre-IPO, Feb 2021, USD 2bn, Sequoia China-led, Temasek and Evergrande participating, post-money USD 8bn [source: Reuters via Yahoo, 1 Feb 2021].

**Operating peaks.** GMV RMB 40bn in 2020 (~USD 6.2bn), avg 8m daily orders H1 2020, 300k cooperative stores [source: EqualOcean, "Xingsheng Youxuan's GMV Breaks CNY 10 Billion in 1H 2020", 24 Aug 2020]. Headcount estimate 5k to 8k at peak (2021), no precise public number.

**Failure mode.** Three things specifically. (1) The Hunan convenience-store base was a real edge: existing shopkeepers, existing customer trust, existing pickup point. The edge dissolved once Meituan and Pinduoduo entered the same neighbourhood with subsidies and put a tuanzhang next to every Xingsheng tuanzhang, often the same person; commission rates compressed from 20 percent to 5 to 10 percent by mid-2022 [source: ChinaTalk recap, 2022]. (2) The geographical pull-back: Xingsheng went from operating in 18 provinces at peak to 3 provinces by 2023 [source: Sixth Tone, 24 Aug 2022]. (3) The IPO did not happen: pre-IPO round closed Feb 2021, then the SAMR crackdown of March 2021 and the Didi US-listing crackdown of July 2021 effectively shut the listing window for the entire Chinese internet cohort. The USD 2bn raised in early 2021 had to fund the wind-down rather than the offensive.

**Founders' path.** Yue Lihua remains chairman of the slimmed-down Furong Xingsheng group, focused back on the Hunan convenience-store and wholesale base, which pre-dated the CGB push. Co-founders Liu Huiyu and Zhou Yingjie remain on the executive team per public registry (no public departure on record).

**Reading.** Xingsheng Youxuan was the highest-quality independent in the cohort because it had a real physical asset base (the Furong convenience network) before it took venture capital. That edge held when competition was sub-scale and broke when Meituan and Pinduoduo arrived with deeper pockets. The company is alive but a fraction of its 2021 peak.

### 2.3 Nice Tuan / Shihui Tuan (十荟团)

| Field | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Founded | 2018, Beijing (legal entity Beijing Shihui Technology) | Tracxn, Crunchbase |
| Founder | Chen Ying | Tracxn |
| Original thesis | Pure-play national CGB platform, no physical retail base, growth via heavy subsidy and aggressive tuanzhang recruiting | Sixth Tone |
| Total raised | USD 1.21 to 1.23bn over 6 disclosed rounds, 20 investors | Tracxn, CB Insights |
| Peak valuation | Closest sourced estimate USD 2.5 to 3bn after the Mar 2021 round | Crunchbase, AgFunder |
| Lead investors | Alibaba, DST Global, GGV, Jeneration, Dragoneer, CDH, DE Shaw, Anatole, Kunlun, Cygnus, Franchise Capital | AgFunder, "Brief: Chinese e-grocer Nice Tuan raises $750m in Alibaba, DST-led round", 2021 |

**Funding rounds, dated.**
- Several early rounds 2018 to 2020 (CB Insights captures four).
- Series C, Nov 2020, USD 196m [source: DealStreetAsia, "Chinese e-commerce firm Nice Tuan raises $196m in fourth round this year"].
- Series D, 31 Mar 2021, USD 750m, co-led Alibaba and DST Global [source: AgFunder].
- Total disclosed Nov 2020 + Mar 2021 = USD 946m.

**Operating peaks.** Sales target RMB 80bn for 2021; actual run-rate RMB 2bn in a "good month", per Sixth Tone reporting [source: Sixth Tone, 24 Aug 2022]. Headcount peak: over 10,000.

**Failure mode.** Three discrete events. (1) SAMR fine of RMB 1.5m on 3 March 2021 for selling pears at RMB 0.99 / 250g vs RMB 3.89 cost; second fine and three-day app suspension shortly after. (2) Alibaba softened on the cheque: the late 2021 USD 1.2bn promised round did not fully land; only "about USD 300m" actually arrived per Sixth Tone reporting. (3) The August 2021 cull: shut down Xi'an, Taiyuan, Xiangyang, Jingzhou; by end-2021 operating in five cities only; headcount cut from 10,000+ to ~2,000, an 80 percent reduction [source: Produce Report, "From Rapid Expansion to 80% Layoffs: Nice Tuan's Year of Extremes", 2022, https://www.producereport.com/article/rapid-expansion-80-layoffs-nice-tuans-year-extremes; SCMP, "Alibaba-backed community group buying start-up Nice Tuan ceases operations in some cities", 2021, https://www.scmp.com/tech/tech-trends/article/3146197].

**Wind-down.** Nice Tuan ceased substantive operations through 2022. The CB Insights and PitchBook entries treat it as effectively defunct. No public bankruptcy filing because the legal vehicle (Beijing Shihui) was wound down through asset transfer to Alibaba's Taocaicai unit.

**Founders' path.** Chen Ying has not surfaced publicly in a comparable role. The bulk of mid-level engineering and ops talent moved into Alibaba's local-services arm and into ByteDance's grocery experiments.

**Reading.** Nice Tuan is the cleanest "pure-play CGB with no physical asset" failure. With no convenience-store network underneath it, it was reliant entirely on subsidy economics and on Alibaba writing the next cheque. Both broke at once in mid-2021.

### 2.4 Pinduoduo's Duo Duo Maicai (多多买菜): operating, comparator note

Internal Pinduoduo unit, launched August 2020. Funded directly off the parent's balance sheet, no separate equity raise. End-2022 GMV reported at RMB 180bn (roughly USD 25 to 30bn) and running at ~1.5x Meituan Select's GMV [source: TechBuzz China, 2022; ChinaTalk recap]. The structural advantage was not the product, it was that 45 percent of Duo Duo Maicai users came pre-attached from Pinduoduo's 400m DAU consumer app, so customer acquisition was effectively zero-CAC. Fined RMB 1.5m in March 2021. Continues to operate as one of the two surviving CGB platforms in 2025.

**Why it survived.** No standalone equity story to defend; balance sheet attached to a profitable parent (Pinduoduo group net income ~RMB 60bn in 2022); no external clock from VCs.

---

### 2.5 Meituan Select / Meituan Youxuan (美团优选): operating, comparator note

Internal Meituan division, established July 2020 as a separate business unit, thousands of staff transferred from Meituan Maicai. Wang Xing, Meituan CEO, said on the Q4 2020 earnings call: community e-commerce is "probably the best opportunity, once every five or 10 years" [source: SCMP, "Food delivery giant Meituan eats US$300 million in losses to chase 'best opportunity in five years'", 2021, https://www.scmp.com/tech/big-tech/article/3127151]. Meituan's "new initiatives" segment, of which Youxuan was the dominant component, posted RMB 38.4bn (USD 5.35bn) of segment loss in FY2021 alone, RMB 77.7bn (USD ~11bn) cumulative 2020 to 2022, and RMB 20.2bn in 2023 [source: Pandaily Pro, "Community Group Buying's Decline", 2024]. By June 2025 Meituan publicly announced it would scale back Youxuan in unprofitable regions and redirect investment into instant retail [source: TechNode, "Meituan expands instant retail, scales back community group-buying in unprofitable areas", 25 Jun 2025, https://technode.com/2025/06/25/].

**Why it survived (but did not win).** Same reason as Duo Duo Maicai: parent-balance-sheet funded, no external clock. The number to internalise is that even a profitable parent ate USD 11bn+ of cumulative segment losses on this experiment over three years.

## 3. Sector-level lessons for WinX

**Total capital evaporated.** Working estimate: USD 14 to 16bn deployed across the sector 2018 to 2022, of which roughly USD 5 to 6bn in independent-startup equity is unrecoverable (Nice Tuan, Tongcheng Life, the smaller failed independents, plus the markdown on Xingsheng and Meicai from peak to current marks); the balance sits as recognised segment losses on the books of Meituan, Pinduoduo, Alibaba, JD, and Didi. The Meituan Youxuan losses alone (USD ~11bn cumulative 2020 to 2022) are a useful upper-bound benchmark for what a single profitable parent was willing to absorb on the bet.

**Why the regulatory crackdown hit this sector specifically.** Three forces converged. (1) The small-merchant lobby: vegetable vendors, wet-market stallholders, and small grocery shops are politically protected employment. SAMR's December 2020 nine-character rule was framed in state media (People's Daily editorial, 11 Dec 2020) as a defence of the small vendor against "below-cost dumping" by big-tech-backed platforms. (2) Food security: Beijing treats vegetable supply, particularly in tier-3 and tier-4 cities, as a strategic commodity, not a tech category. A platform model that risked a single-point-of-failure dependence on a Tencent or Alibaba app for daily food was politically unacceptable. (3) Anti-monopoly timing: the CGB crackdown coincided with the Ant Financial IPO suspension (Nov 2020), the Alibaba RMB 18.2bn antitrust fine (Apr 2021), and the Didi listing crackdown (Jul 2021); CGB was caught in the same broader correction. The SAMR fines of 3 March 2021 (RMB 1.5m each on Meituan, Pinduoduo, Didi, Nice Tuan) were the loudest single signal.

**Closed-loop attribution at scale.** Honest answer: no independent CGB player ever achieved true POS-level closed-loop attribution at retail-network scale. The closest was Xingsheng Youxuan inside the Furong convenience network in Hunan, where the tuanzhang was the existing shopkeeper and her POS was the existing convenience-store till; even there the attribution was app-side (which household ordered which SKU), not in-store (which household, walking in cold, bought which SKU). Pinduoduo and Meituan have app-side attribution at scale because of the parent-app login graph, but this is consumer-app attribution, not retailer-POS attribution. The lesson is sober: the CGB cohort spent USD 14bn+ and did not solve the in-store identified-shopper problem. They built a parallel app channel; they did not digitise the existing GT shop.

**Honest read for the WinX team.**

*The parallel.* The category being attacked is the same: low-ticket, high-frequency neighbourhood fresh and FMCG demand currently served by an informal, fragmented retail base (wet markets in China, GT in Vietnam). The unit-economics tension is the same: fresh produce at sub-USD-per-item pricing cannot service a heavy logistics layer without either subsidy or unusual asset leverage. The political tension is the same: any model that obviously displaces small-shop livelihoods will draw regulatory attention, and Vietnamese authorities are not less sensitive on this point than Beijing.

*The disanalogy that matters most.* The Chinese cohort attacked the consumer (build a new app channel for households). WinX attacks the retailer (digitise the existing GT shop's ordering, replenishment, and POS into a Masan-supplied stack). This is a structurally different bet. The Chinese model required convincing 100m households to switch their daily fresh-buying habit; the WinX model requires equipping ~500k existing GT shopkeepers, who already have the relationship with the household, with a better tool. The Chinese cohort burned USD 14bn fighting for consumer attention; WinX is paying for retailer adoption, and the retailer is a B2B counterparty with a quantifiable productivity lift, not a price-sensitive consumer requiring permanent subsidy.

*The single transferable lesson.* Survival in this category was a function of two things and only two: (1) a real physical asset underneath the digital layer (Furong's convenience network for Xingsheng; Pinduoduo's 400m-DAU consumer graph for Duo Duo Maicai; Meituan's 600m-MAU local-services graph for Youxuan); (2) patient capital that does not need an IPO to recycle. Independent CGB players with neither of those died. Meicai had a B2B asset but no patient capital. Nice Tuan had Alibaba capital but no physical asset. WinX has both: WinCommerce's 4,000+ store estate and shop-relationship database is the physical asset; Masan Group's balance sheet is the patient capital. The China data point says this combination is necessary, not sufficient. Sufficiency requires that the productivity lift to the GT shopkeeper is real, measurable per-shop, and attributable; everything else in the China experience says the players who could not measure that lift at the shop level eventually ran out of either capital or political cover.
